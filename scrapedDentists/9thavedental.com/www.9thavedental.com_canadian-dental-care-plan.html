What is the Canadian Dental Care Plan?

The Canadian Dental Care Plan is a government-sponsored program that makes available basic dental care for eligible Canadians. The plan will support a person who qualifies with some or all costs of their important dental services, including check-ups, cleanings, x-rays, fillings, etc.
The Canadian Dental Care Plan is particularly useful for people who do not have private dental insurance. If your family has an annual income below an income threshold and meets other guidelines, you may be eligible for the CDCP.
At 9th Avenue Dental, our kind staff is available to help you and your family at every stage of the procedure, including determining your eligibility and what is covered.

Dental problems can get worse over time if not treated. What starts as a small cavity can turn into pain, infections, or even tooth loss. That’s why it’s so important to see a dentist in Downtown Calgary regularly.
The Canadian Dental Care Plan in Downtown Calgary gives more people the chance to take care of their teeth without worrying about the cost. With CDCP, you can:

Many services are either fully or partly covered by the plan, depending on your situation.

How to apply for the Canadian Dental Care Plan

Steps to apply:
Confirm eligibility using the four criteria above.
Apply online through your MSCA account or by using your phone MSCA (1‑833‑537‑4342) or by mail.
Provide your SIN, address, income proof, and tax return, and confirm lack of private insurance.
Once approved, you’ll receive a welcome letter with your start date and member ID.
Once you receive the mail and the date, visit a dentist that is registered with the CDCP program; they will bill Sun Life directly.

Who Can Apply?

To qualify for the Canadian Dental Care Plan near you, you need to meet a few basic rules:
  • You must live in Canada
  • You must not have access to any private dental insurance.
  • You must have filed a tax return recently.
  • Your household income must be below a certain amount (as set by the government)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Canadian Dental Care Plan?
The Canadian Dental Care Plan is a new federal program intended to assist uninsured individuals in obtaining affordable dental care. The program is based on income, and it provides financial coverage for most basic services and some major services.
Who can qualify for CDCP?
You are eligible for CDCP if you have no private dental insurance, can attest to Canadian residency for tax purposes, file taxes, and earn a gross income of less than CAD 90,000.
Is CDCP available in Calgary?
Yes! You can access the Canadian Dental Care Plan in downtown Calgary at clinics like 9th Avenue Dental.
What does CDCP include?
CDCP covers cleanings, exams, cavities, root canals, gum treatment, crowns, dentures, etc. Some services will require preauthorization before completion.
Do I owe any payments?
It depends on your income. If you make less than CAD 70,000/year, you will not owe anything unless your dentist charges over the CDCP fee guide.
Can I access a root canal under CDCP?
Yes, you can access a root canal under CDCP if it is medically necessary for your oral health.
Can I receive CDCP if I have work insurance?
No, CDCP is only available to individuals who do not have any kind of dental insurance, including any work insurance or senior plan.
How do I find a CDCP provider?
You can search online or visit a dentist who lives near you, e.g., 9th Avenue Dental, as it is already enrolled in the CDCP.
