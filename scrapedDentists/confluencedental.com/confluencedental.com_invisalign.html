What Is Invisalign?

Invisalign uses a series of clear, custom-made aligners to gradually shift your teeth into the right position. Each set of aligners is worn for about one week before moving to the next set in the series.

The aligners are virtually invisible. Most people won't notice you're wearing them. You remove them to eat, drink, brush, and floss.

Invisalign treats crowding, spacing, overbites, underbites, and crossbites. Whether your case is mild or complex, your dentist will design a treatment plan specific to your smile.

How Invisalign Works

  • A digital scan of your teeth creates a 3D treatment plan that shows exactly how your teeth will move, step by step.

  • Wear each set for about one week, then switch to the next. Visit us every 6–8 weeks to monitor your progress.

  • Most treatments take 6–18 months. Many patients start seeing noticeable improvement within the first few months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Still have questions? Take a look at the FAQ or reach out anytime.

  • Most treatments take 6–18 months. Mild cases can finish in as few as 3–6 months.

  • You'll feel pressure when you switch to a new aligner set. It's mild and goes away within a day or two.

  • 22 hours a day for best results. You remove them only to eat, drink anything other than water, and brush your teeth.

  • Cost depends on the complexity and length of your treatment. Many dental insurance plans cover a portion of Invisalign, just like traditional braces.
