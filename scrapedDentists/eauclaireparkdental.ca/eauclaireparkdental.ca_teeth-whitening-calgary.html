What Causes Tooth Discoloration?

Your smile’s radiance can fade over time due to everyday habits and natural factors, such as:

Coffee, Tea, and Red Wine

Dark pigments in your favorite beverages can leave stubborn stains on enamel over time.

Tobacco Use

Nicotine and tar from smoking or chewing tobacco create yellow or brown discoloration that is difficult to remove with brushing alone.

Aging

As you age, enamel naturally wears down, revealing the darker dentin underneath.

Certain Medications

Antibiotics like tetracycline or high fluoride levels can cause deep internal staining during tooth development. Whitening has more limited effectiveness in treating internal stains, such as those caused by medications (tetracycline) or previous trauma.

Professional teeth whitening treatments are designed to target and lift external stains, restoring the natural brightness of your teeth.

How Does Teeth Whitening Work?

Teeth whitening is a non-invasive cosmetic treatment designed to remove stains and lighten discoloration, giving your smile a fresher, youthful appearance. Professional whitening works through a process called oxygenation.

  • Breaking Down Stains: Hydrogen peroxide (HP), the active ingredient, breaks down into oxygen molecules that target stains on your enamel.
  • Dissolving Discoloration: These oxygen molecules penetrate the tooth surface, breaking apart the compounds that cause discoloration, such as those from coffee, wine, or tobacco.
  • Custom Treatment: By adjusting the concentration of whitening agents and contact time, we ensure your treatment is personalized to deliver optimal results without discomfort.

Which Whitening Option Is Best for You?

Over-the-Counter Products

While widely available, OTC products like Crest white strips and Aquafresh white trays are the least effective and least predictable for whitening outcomes. They bring a higher risk of tooth sensitivity and gingival irritation vs. professional methods.

Custom Take-Home Kits

The gold standard for whitening at home. These dentist-designed kits include custom trays and professional-grade gel, offering effective, predictable results while minimizing sensitivity.

In-Office Treatment

In-office whitening involves applying a high-concentration peroxide solution to your teeth for up to one hour, depending on the desired results.

What to Expect From Your Whitening Treatment

1. Comprehensive Evaluation

We begin with a detailed oral health assessment to confirm whitening is right for you and identify any underlying concerns that need attention.

2. Personalized Treatment Plan

Every smile is unique, so we tailor your whitening experience to your needs and goals. Our customized plans consider:

  • Type and severity of stains
  • Your current tooth shade and stain susceptibility
  • Oral hygiene habits and overall dental health
  • Past whitening treatments
  • Diet factors like coffee, tea, and red wine
  • Existing dental restorations

3. Professional Care

During in-office treatments, we protect your gums with a special gel and apply a professional-strength whitening agent to safely and effectively brighten your teeth. For custom take-home kits, we ensure the trays fit perfectly to your teeth to minimize the risk of gum irritation.

4. Long-Term Maintenance

After your treatment, we’ll share tips to maintain your smile, from at-home care routines to lifestyle adjustments that prolong your results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does teeth whitening cost in Calgary?

Teeth whitening costs in Calgary can vary depending on the method and provider. Eau Claire Park Dental provides professional whitening treatments tailored to your needs. Contact us to discuss your options and get an accurate quote.

How often should you whiten your teeth?

For most patients, touch-ups for your teeth whitening is recommended every 6–12 months. This depends on lifestyle factors like diet and oral hygiene. Whitening can cause temporary tooth sensitivity, so consult your dentist for a personalized plan.

Does teeth whitening ruin enamel?

No, professional teeth whitening does not ruin enamel when performed correctly. Our treatments are designed to be safe and effective, using carefully formulated whitening agents to ensure your enamel remains intact and your teeth stay healthy.

How white can I expect my teeth to get?

Results vary depending on factors like the original shade of your teeth and your commitment to the process. While whitening can significantly improve brightness, outcomes are unique to each patient. Our team will happily guide you through the process for the best possible results.

How long do results last?

Teeth whitening results can last up to 2 years with proper maintenance, such as good oral hygiene and avoiding stain-causing foods or drinks. Regular touch-ups can help extend your results.
