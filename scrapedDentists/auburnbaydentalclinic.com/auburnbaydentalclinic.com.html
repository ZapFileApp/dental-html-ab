We apply the Alberta Dental Association Fee Guide, which assures Albertans that fees are reasonable and competitive. We respect this by being compliant with the Association’s Fee Guide. For our patient’s convenience, we also offer direct billing.

Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP)

The CDCP is a federal program to help Canadians without a dental plan access affordable care. If you have an adjusted household income under $90,000 per year, you may qualify for dental subsidies. 

But note that, CDCP may not cover the full cost of your dental treatment. Depending on your income, you may be responsible for co-payments. If your income is between $70,000 and $89,999, or if you choose treatments not covered by the CDCP, you must pay the difference.

Oral Health in Calgary

According to Statistics Canada, over 26% of Canadians deal with oral pain or avoid certain foods due to oral health problems. A worrying 4% of Canadian adults have lost all their natural teeth, with this number climbing to 11% among seniors. Yet, the good news is that with proper care, we can prevent or manage these issues effectively. 

In Calgary, oral health mirrors these national trends. While many Calgarians take good care of their teeth, some are still falling behind. Based on the Canadian Community Health Survey, about 14.6% of Albertans aged 12 and older rate their oral health as fair or poor. This number increases with age, peaking at 20.6% in those aged 65-69.

Key issues affecting the city include:

  • Cavities: A common issue affecting children from all demographics, also one of the leading causes of tooth loss.
  • Dental Disease: With the majority of Canadians suffering from some form of periodontal disease, it’s important to regularly monitor your oral health.
  • Income Disparities: Lower-income individuals are more likely to experience oral health problems, from untreated cavities to lost teeth.
  • Access to Care: Regular visits are necessary, but nearly one in five adults has untreated tooth decay, especially among those little to no previous check-ups.

It’s clear that staying proactive with oral care is key. We’re here to help your smile stay healthy for years to come.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between general and family dentistry?

General and family dentistry are both fields of dentistry that focus on maintaining oral health and preventing problems. The main difference lies in the age range of patients. Family dentists treat all ages, from infants to the elderly, while general dentists in Calgary treat patients 16 to 18 years old and older.

Family dentists offer the same services, such as exams, cleanings, fillings, extractions, and gum disease treatments, but they also specialize in procedures for all ages. This means they can provide individual and specific services for children, adults, and seniors.

What is cosmetic dentistry?

It focuses on improving the appearance of your smile by changing your teeth’ colour, shape, size, alignment, and overall look. Common procedures include:

  • Whitening: Bleaching agents to remove stains and discolouration.
  • Veneers: Thin, shells that are placed on the front surface of a tooth to cover chips, cracks, gaps, or discolouration.
  • Bonding: A composite resin material applied to the surface and sculpted to repair cracks or gaps or to improve the shape and colour.
  • Gum Contouring: A procedure to reshape the gum to improve the appearance of a smile or uneven gum line.

It boosts your self-confidence and improves your overall appearance. If you’re considering cosmetic procedures, consult our dental professionals to determine the best dental treatments available.

What classifies as an emergency procedure?

An emergency requires immediate attention from a Calgary dentist to alleviate pain, prevent further damage, or save a tooth. While some issues can wait for a scheduled appointment, others demand prompt action.

Common emergencies include:

  • Severe Toothache. A persistent and intense toothache could signal an underlying issue, such as an infection or decay. If the pain is unbearable, reach out at Auburn Bay for immediate care.
  • Knocked-Out Tooth. Accidents happen, and a knocked-out tooth is an emergency requiring swift action. To give the tooth the best chance of reattachment, preserve it in milk or a tooth-preservation solution and contact us as soon as possible.
  • Broken or Cracked Teeth. A fractured tooth demands prompt attention, whether a minor chip or a break. Rinse with warm water, use a cold compress, and contact us without delay.
  • Lost Filling or Crown. A lost filling or crown exposes the sensitive layers of the tooth. Cover the affected area with sugarless gum, and call us.
  • Abscess or Swelling. An abscessed tooth with swelling and pus indicates a severe infection. Seek care to prevent the spread of infection.

Recognizing an emergency and taking immediate, appropriate action can save a tooth and prevent further complications.
