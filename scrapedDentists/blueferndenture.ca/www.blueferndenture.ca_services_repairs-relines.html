REPAIRS AND RELINES

When your denture breaks or a tooth falls out, it’s an emergency. Our denture team understands that getting it fixed fast is the only thing that really matters to you at that moment. Emergency denture repairs are some of the most routine services we offer. Our in-house lab allows us to make any denture repair or denture reline quick, easy, and affordable.

Even though our dentures are fabricated from extremely durable and top-quality materials, they will eventually wear out, crack or break, their fit will change or a tooth will come out. Denture repairs are inevitable.

In most cases, our Denturists can restore a fractured or damaged denture close to its original state on a same-day basis.

Attempting to fix your denture yourself using a ‘home repair kit’ may end up resulting in further damage and make your denture impossible to repair again and uncomfortable to wear. Department store glues contain harmful chemicals that should not be put inside the mouth. Glue should never be used to fix a broken denture. Broken dentures should only be repaired by denture experts trained and qualified to perform the repair.

A reline is an adjustment made to improve the fit of a denture or partial denture. This procedure helps to fill any gaps that may have been created over time between your dentures and the tissues in your mouth. Relining affects only the fit of your denture and will not change the appearance or address any wear of the denture teeth.

Rebasing is usually done when the denture teeth have not worn out in comparison to the denture base material. It is a process of retrofitting dentures by replacing the entire acrylic denture base with new acrylic, which provides a stable denture without replacing the denture teeth.

Taking Care of Your Dentures

Did you know, cleaning your dentures is as important as cleaning your natural teeth.

Dentures should be cleaned after every meal using a denture brush and soaked nightly in a denture cleaner. Denture teeth are softer than natural teeth and using toothpaste can cause microscopic scratches from the abrasives found on the toothpaste. These scratches give bacteria a place to attach and grow. You should also never use bleach to soak your denture.

If you have any questions regarding maintaining your dentures, please don’t hesitate to give us a call.
