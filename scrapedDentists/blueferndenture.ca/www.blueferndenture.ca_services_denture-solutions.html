Complete Dentures

Dentures are used to replace teeth when there are no teeth remaining. A well crafted denture can improve your smile, and improve your function without compromising comfort. Whether you’ve lost your teeth due to injury, tooth decay or gum disease, our staff is here to help. We understand that the thought of replacing your teeth is a stressful process which is why we dedicate ourselves to providing our patients with a hassle-free experience, paired with the highest quality of service.

Partial Dentures

When a person has lost some but not all of their teeth, a partial denture may be the desired treatment choice. Partial dentures can provide a simple and attractive way to replace missing teeth. They can be taken in and out of the mouth and have a base that closely resembles the colour of your gums and teeth. They are held in with a clasp, or retentive device. The denture will attach to your existing teeth with ease, no one will ever be able to tell that you are wearing dentures!

Implant Dentures

Implant retained dentures are the closest thing to having your natural teeth. They are the single most effective treatment for ill-fitting or loose dentures. Implants provide a long-term solution in making your dentures feel most secure. They provide you the needed stability so you can eat and smile with confidence. No more Glue or sores in your mouth. Ask any of our highly trained staff and we will be happy to talk with you about your denture implant options.

Surgical / Immediate Dentures

Starting any new process we don't understand can cause fear and nervousness. The process of removing your teeth and transitioning to dentures is no different. It is our goal at Blue Fern Denture clinic to inform and prepare you to start this journey with our help and guidance.

What are surgical Dentures?

Surgical or immediate dentures are made prior to extraction of natural teeth. The dentures act as a surgical band aid on the extraction site. They control bleeding, swelling, and general healing. They also help your appearance so you don't have to go without teeth during your healing stage. Consider these surgical dentures to be anti-embarrassment dentures. While the healing process takes time you will be able to smile.

Relines

A reline is an adjustment made to improve the fit of a denture or partial denture. This procedure helps to fill any gaps that may have been created over time between your dentures and the tissues in your mouth. Relining affects only the fit of your denture and will not change the appearance or address any wear of the denture teeth. Relines are recommended every two years or when tissue changes occur due to resorption (shrinkage) which can be due to:

  • loss of weight
  • loss of teeth
  • loss of bone/tissue
  • disease or illness
