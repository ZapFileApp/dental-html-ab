Understanding Fillings and Dental Cavities

Dental cavities are areas of decay in the enamel of your teeth. Left untreated, cavities can grow larger and lead to infections, discomfort, or even tooth loss. Children are especially prone to cavities because their enamel is thinner than adults. Fortunately, most small to moderate cavities can be effectively treated with a dental filling, which involves removing the decayed area and restoring the tooth with a durable material. Regular checkups are key to detecting problems early before they require more complex treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dental Fillings and Cavities

What causes dental cavities?

Cavities are caused by bacteria that feed on sugars and starches left on your teeth. The acids they produce weaken your enamel over time.

How do I know if I have a cavity?

Common signs include tooth sensitivity, pain when chewing, visible holes, or discoloration. Only a dentist in Edmonton can confirm with a proper exam.

Is getting a filling painful?

Not at all. We use gentle techniques and local anesthetic so you can relax during your treatment. Many patients feel little to no discomfort.

Are fillings safe for children?

Yes. We offer child-friendly dental fillings that are both safe and effective, and we strive to make the experience positive and stress-free.

How long does a filling last?

With proper care, most fillings last between 5 to 15 years, depending on the material and oral hygiene habits.
