Sedation Dentistry

Everyone has different tolerance levels for dentistry procedures, which is why we provide sedation dentistry options beyond the local anesthetic provided for treatments. We invite our patients to book an appointment to discuss their treatment options and whether sedation dentistry is right for them or their family members.

IV Sedation Dentistry

IV sedation involves administering sedative drugs directly into the bloodstream using an IV line resulting in moderate to deep sedation. The patient’s vital signs are monitored closely throughout the procedure to ensure safety. This type of sedation takes effect quickly, leaving the patient with little to no memory of the procedure.

Request an Appointment

Nitrous Oxide Sedation Dentistry

Nitrous oxide sedation (also called laughing gas) is a fast-acting, mild sedative that is inhaled through a mask placed over the nose. Patients inhale a mixture of nitrous oxide and oxygen to induce a feeling of euphoria and relaxation during the dental procedure. This type of sedation has minimal side effects and wears off quickly, so you can safely drive yourself home afterward.

Request an Appointment

Oral Sedation

Oral sedation is a pill form of sedative that patients are prescribed and directed to take within a certain time frame before their appointment. This option helps patients feel drowsy and relaxed during the procedure without the need for an IV. This type of sedation is suitable for patients with moderate anxiety about the dentist or dental procedures.
