Root Canal Therapy in Langdon, AB 

Root Canal Therapy Near You

Endodontic or root canal therapy is a common dental procedure that involves removing a tooth’s inner pulp. When this inner mass, containing nerves and blood vessels, becomes infected or exposed, it must be removed to alleviate pain and prevent other issues. A root canal is the last line of defence in saving a tooth and keeping it from being extracted. If you’re in need of this type of care, our dentist near you can assist. Reach out to our local dental practice for more information

Reasons for a Root Canal

You may need to have a root canal performed for several reasons, including an untreated cavity. Over time, if the decay is left unattended, it can reach the centre of the tooth, infecting and compromising the tooth’s structure. Root canals may also be necessary if a tooth’s sustained a deep fracture from excessive force or stress. Continual clenching and grinding, or poor tooth alignment can also contribute to damage that can impact the nerve. 

Some symptoms to keep an eye out for are: 

  • Your tooth’s turned black
  • Jaw pain
  • Tooth sensitivity to hot and cold items
  • Pimples have formed on your gums
  • Swollen or tender gums 

What to Expect During the Procedure

When you come into Langdon Dental for a root canal, you’ll be greeted by our friendly, skilled team and shown to a chair. Our dentist will carefully examine your smile, take x-rays, and discuss your requirements. All these measures allow us to identify the best treatment for you. If that is a root canal, we can get started with the procedure. 

The treatment area is anesthetized for your comfort. A dam is used to protect your airway and isolate your tooth. The decayed pulp is carefully removed before the pulp chamber is cleaned and disinfected. Lastly, the canals are filled with a special medicated material (called gutta-percha) and sealed.  A filling or crown is used to cover the tooth and reinforce it. This prosthetic will be created to blend in with your surrounding teeth and fit your tooth’s oral dimensions. 

If you have questions or concerns at any point during your procedure, don’t hesitate to let us know! Your comfort is our top priority, and we want you to have a positive experience with us. 

Is it Successful? 

This treatment has a success rate of over 90%, meaning that once your treatment concludes, you’ll be able to enjoy your smile without worry. That said, it’s still essential to follow a good oral hygiene routine at home and attend regular cleaning appointments with a dentist in Langdon, AB. This will keep your smile healthy, and more advanced procedures aren’t necessary.
