Did this one surprise you? 🦷

Many of us grew up rinsing with water immediately after brushing, but doing so can wash away much of the fluoride left behind by your toothpaste. Fluoride continues working after you brush by strengthening enamel and helping protect against cavities.

Instead of rinsing, try simply spitting out the excess toothpaste. Leaving a small amount behind allows the fluoride to stay in contact with your teeth a little longer, maximizing its benefits.

Small habits can make a big difference when it comes to protecting your smile! ✨

Regular cleanings help remove plaque and tartar buildup before they can lead to bigger issues, while annual exams allow us to monitor your overall oral health.

That said, there is no one-size-fits-all schedule. Some patients benefit from hygiene visits every 6 months, while others may have different needs. Your recommended recall schedule is based on your individual oral health and should be discussed with your hygienist and dentist.

Whatever interval is recommended for you, that's your normal. Keeping up with your personalized schedule is one of the best ways to maintain a healthy smile and catch problems early. ✨

Did you know? Cavities are not caused by sugar alone.

Bacteria in your mouth feed on carbohydrates too, like crackers, bread, chips, and fruit. As they feed, they create acid that can weaken tooth enamel. Frequent snacking means more acid attacks and less time for your mouth to recover.

Taking breaks between snacks and drinking water can help support a healthier smile.
