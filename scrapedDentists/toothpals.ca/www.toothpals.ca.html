Meet Our Pediatric Dentist in Airdrie

Dr. Peeyush Ranjan

Dr. Ranjan’s journey in dentistry began in 2009 when he earned his General Dentistry degree. It didn’t take long for him to discover his true passion: working with children. This passion led him to pursue specialized training in pediatric dentistry, which he completed through a residency at Nova Southeastern Universityand Joe DiMaggio Children’s Hospital in Ft. Lauderdale, Florida. During his training, he served as Chief Resident. He is also a Board Certified  pediatric dentist in both the USA and Canada.

Dr. Ranjan is deeply committed to providing care tailored to the needs of every child. During his residency, he conducted a master’s research project at the Mailman Segal Center for Human Development, focusing on how picture-guided techniques can help children with Autism Spectrum Disorder improve their oral hygiene. This experience strengthened his dedication to creating an inclusive and supportive environment for all children.

FAQ's

When should I schedule my child's first visit to the dentist?
Your child should see the dentist at age one or six months after the eruption of their first tooth. Early visits allow healthy development to be tracked and dental care to become normal and enjoyable from an early stage.
How is a pediatric dentist different from a general dentist?
Pediatric dentists are a bit more qualified to attend to care, that is, oral care for children. They are aware of children’s ways of thinking and create a comfortable, child-focused environment geared toward smiles in the making.
What can I expect during my child's first dental appointment?
The initial visit is a gentle, instructional, and enjoyable experience! We examine your child’s teeth, provide tips on hygiene, and respond to questions—laying the groundwork for a lifetime of good dental habits.
How often should my child visit the dentist?
Children need to see the dentist every six months. Checkups prevent issues, monitor progress, and establish familiarity and self-confidence with dental care.
Why are baby teeth important if they eventually fall out?
Baby teeth are used for chewing and speaking. They also direct the permanent teeth to the correct place. They play an important role in overall growth and encourage a confident, cheerful smile.
What is the best way to clean my baby's teeth?
Begin by wiping your baby’s gums gently, then clean with a soft toothbrush and a small smear of fluoride toothpaste when teeth emerge. An early start guarantees strong, healthy habits.
What causes cavities in children, and how can they be prevented?
Cavities are the result of sugar and bacteria. Brushing with fluoride toothpaste, no sugary snacks, water drinking, and a visit to the dentist keep your child’s smile cavity-free.
Are dental sealants beneficial for my child?
Yes! Sealants keep the pits of back teeth free of food and germs. They are safe, quick, and a great way to prevent cavities as your child grows.
