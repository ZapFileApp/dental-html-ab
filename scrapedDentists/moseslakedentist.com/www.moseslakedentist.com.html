Meet Your Dentist

Experienced
& Gentle Dental Care

Dr. Nick Smith believes dentistry is about more than teeth; it’s about people, comfort, and helping patients enjoy life with confidence. Originally from High Point, North Carolina, Dr. Smith moved to Pocatello, Idaho, as a teenager, where his passion for serving others grew.

 

After completing a two-year mission in Richmond, Virginia, Dr. Smith attended Idaho State University, earning a Bachelor of Science in Biology with an emphasis in biomedical sciences. He then received his Doctor of Dental Surgery degree from Creighton University in Omaha, Nebraska.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I book an appointment or same-day emergency visit?

You can schedule an appointment by calling the office directly, which is the fastest way to get help, especially for dental emergencies. The team can guide you over the phone and arrange same-day care when needed.

Do you accept dental insurance, including Delta Dental in Moses Lake?

Yes, Moses Lake Family Dentistry accepts many dental insurance plans and is in-network with Delta Dental. The team helps patients in Moses Lake understand their coverage, file claims, and make the most of their benefits while keeping costs clear.

Can I tell the office I have dental anxiety before my appointment?

Yes. Tell them when booking. It helps the team plan a slower, more comfortable visit and explain things clearly.

Will I get X-rays at my first visit?

Maybe, but not always. X-rays are usually based on your symptoms, dental history, and what the dentist sees during the exam.

Can one family dentist usually treat both kids and adults?

In many cases, yes. Family dental offices are commonly chosen because they can help with different ages and different needs in one place.

Is parking easy to access for families with children?

Yes, the office location is designed for convenience, with parking available close to the building. This makes visits easier for families with children, strollers, or multiple appointments.

Do you offer financing for major dental procedures like implants or crowns?

Yes, financing options may be available for more extensive treatments such as dental implants, crowns, or full smile restorations. This allows patients to spread out the cost over time.

Does your office use technology for faster diagnosis and treatment planning?

Yes, digital systems allow for quicker imaging and real-time evaluation. This helps your dentist explain your condition clearly and recommend the most appropriate treatment options.
