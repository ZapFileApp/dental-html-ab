Dental Bridges

Fillings

Inlays & Onlays

Custom-made "partial crowns" are used when a cavity is too large for a standard filling but not severe enough for a full crown. Inlays fit within the tooth's grooves, while onlays cover one or more of the chewing cusps.

Root Canal Therapy

Frequently Asked Questions
How much does teeth whitening cost in Calgary?
The cost of teeth whitening in Calgary can vary, but typically ranges from $300 to $600 for a professional in-office whitening treatment.
What to do before dental sedation?
Before undergoing dental sedation, it is important to avoid eating or drinking for several hours prior to the procedure, as directed by your dentist. This helps ensure the sedation is effective and safe.
What are the benefits of teeth whitening?
The benefits of teeth whitening include improving the appearance of discolored or stained teeth, boosting self-confidence, and creating a brighter, more youthful smile.
Is teeth whitening safe for everyone?
Teeth whitening is generally safe, but individual factors, such as dental health and sensitivity, may affect suitability. Consulting a dentist is recommended to determine if teeth whitening is appropriate for each person.
How long does teeth whitening last?
The duration of teeth whitening results can vary, typically lasting between 6 months to 2 years, depending on factors such as lifestyle, dental hygiene, and the specific whitening treatment used.
What techniques are used for teeth whitening?
Techniques used for teeth whitening include in-office bleaching, take-home whitening kits, and over-the-counter whitening products. These methods can effectively lighten and brighten discolored teeth.
Can I whiten my teeth at home?
Yes, you can whiten your teeth at home using over-the-counter whitening products, such as whitening strips or gels. However, for best results and to ensure your safety, it's recommended to consult with a dental professional before starting any at-home whitening regimen.
Are there side effects of teeth whitening?
Teeth whitening can sometimes cause temporary sensitivity or irritation, but these side effects are usually mild and subside quickly. Patients should consult with their dentist to ensure the procedure is safe and suitable for their individual needs.
What is the best age for whitening?
The best age for teeth whitening is typically between 20 and 50 years old, as this is when most people have good oral health and the enamel is less worn down, allowing for more effective and longer-lasting results.
How often should I whiten my teeth?
The recommended frequency for teeth whitening depends on individual factors, but most dentists suggest whitening every 6 to 12 months to maintain results and prevent tooth sensitivity.
What to expect during dental sedation?
During dental sedation, you can expect to feel relaxed and calm throughout the procedure. The dentist will monitor your vital signs closely and provide you with medication to help you remain comfortable.
How long does dental sedation take?
The duration of dental sedation can vary depending on the type of procedure and the individual patient's response. Generally, dental sedation effects can last for several hours after the procedure.
Is dental sedation safe for all patients?
Dental sedation may not be suitable for all patients, as it depends on the individual's medical history and overall health. Patients should consult with their dentist to determine if dental sedation is a safe and appropriate option for their specific needs.
What types of sedation are available?
Ambiance Dental offers various types of sedation, including local anesthesia, nitrous oxide (laughing gas), and oral sedation, to ensure a comfortable and relaxing dental experience for patients.
Can I drive after dental sedation?
After dental sedation, it is generally not recommended to drive. The effects of sedation can impair your ability to operate a vehicle safely. It is advisable to arrange for alternative transportation or wait until the sedative effects have fully worn off before driving.
Who is a candidate for dental sedation?
Candidates for dental sedation are individuals who experience dental anxiety, have a low pain threshold, or require extensive dental work. Sedation can help them feel relaxed and comfortable during their dental procedures.
What are the risks of dental sedation?
The risks of dental sedation may include drowsiness, nausea, and a temporary loss of coordination or memory. Patients should discuss any medical conditions or concerns with their dentist before undergoing sedation.
What can I eat after teeth whitening?
After teeth whitening, it is recommended to consume soft, room-temperature foods and drinks to avoid sensitivity. Avoid hot, cold, acidic, or crunchy items until the sensitivity subsides, usually a few days after the procedure.
How do I maintain whitened teeth?
Maintaining whitened teeth involves practicing good oral hygiene habits, such as regular brushing, flossing, and using a whitening toothpaste. Avoiding staining foods and drinks, and using at-home whitening treatments as directed can also help preserve the results.
Are there foods to avoid after whitening?
After teeth whitening, it is recommended to avoid highly pigmented foods and drinks, such as coffee, tea, red wine, and dark-colored berries, as they can stain the newly whitened teeth.
What should I discuss before dental sedation?
Before undergoing dental sedation, it is important to discuss your medical history, any medications you are taking, and any concerns or questions you have with your dentist to ensure your safety and comfort during the procedure.
How can I prepare for dental sedation?
Preparing for dental sedation involves fasting for a specified duration, avoiding certain medications, and arranging for someone to drive you home after the procedure. It's important to discuss any medical conditions or concerns with your dentist beforehand.
What is the cost of dental sedation?
The cost of dental sedation varies depending on the type of sedation, the complexity of the procedure, and the dentist's location. Typically, the cost can range from $100 to $500 or more.
Can teeth whitening damage dental work?
Teeth whitening can potentially damage existing dental work, such as fillings, crowns, or veneers. The chemicals used in whitening treatments may cause discoloration or weakening of these dental restorations. It is important to consult a dentist before undergoing any whitening procedures to ensure the safety of your dental work.
What results can I expect from whitening?
Whitening can effectively lighten and brighten your teeth, typically achieving a noticeable improvement in shade and hue. The results may vary depending on your initial tooth color and the specific whitening treatment used.
How is dental sedation administered?
Dental sedation is typically administered through inhalation, oral medication, or intravenous injection, depending on the level of sedation required and the patient's needs. The dentist will carefully monitor the patient's vital signs throughout the procedure.
What happens during a teeth whitening session?
A teeth whitening session typically involves the application of a peroxide-based bleaching agent to the teeth, which helps to remove discoloration and stains, resulting in a brighter, whiter smile.
Are there alternatives to teeth whitening?
Yes, there are alternatives to teeth whitening, such as dental veneers, crowns, and bonding, which can help improve the appearance of discolored or stained teeth.
Is consultation required before teeth whitening?
Before undergoing teeth whitening, a consultation is generally recommended to assess your oral health and determine the most suitable whitening treatment for your needs.
What aftercare is needed after sedation?
After sedation, patients may require aftercare such as rest, monitoring of vital signs, and avoiding driving or operating machinery until the effects of the sedation have fully worn off.
