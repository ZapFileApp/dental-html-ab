FAQs

How often can seniors visit a dentist?

It is recommended that seniors visit a dentist for check-ups and cleaning every six months. Regular check-ups are crucial for detecting problems before they worsen. Moreover, these check-ups will make sure existing deCare work remains effective.

What are common dental issues in older adults?

Common issues include gum disease, tooth decay, dry mouth, and tooth loss. Regular dental visits and proper care help you manage those issues before they become bigger problems.

Are dental treatments for older people safe?

Do you have a question about a dental treatment? This personalized strategy approaches the patient as a whole while developing the treatment care strategy.

How can seniors deal with dry mouth?

There are a variety of solutions that can alleviate dry mouth by keeping your mouth moist. You can hydrate yourself, use a mouth rinse, or adjust the medications or foods you consume. A dentist can suggest appropriate treatment to provide you with long-term relief and comfort.

What Can You Do About Missing Teeth?

Seniors can opt for dentures, bridges, or implants based on what they need most. A dentist helps choose the right one for comfort, function, and long-term oral health benefits.

Why is gum care essential for elderly individuals?

Gums must be healthy for strong teeth and oral health. Gum care helps prevent infections, reduces pain, and lessens the risk of tooth loss, helping seniors wear a happy smile.
