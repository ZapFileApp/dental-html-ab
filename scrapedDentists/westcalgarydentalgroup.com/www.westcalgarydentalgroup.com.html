Dental Implants

Dental implants are a durable, natural-looking solution for replacing missing teeth. Designed to function like real tooth roots, implants are securely placed in the jawbone to support crowns, bridges, or dentures. They help restore your ability to chew, speak, and smile with confidence while also preventing bone loss over time. Each treatment plan is tailored to your needs, ensuring a comfortable and precise fit. With proper care, dental implants can last for many years, making them a reliable long-term investment in your oral health.

Wisdom Teeth

Wisdom teeth, or third molars, often emerge in the late teens or early adulthood and can cause complications if there isn’t enough space in the mouth. Impacted or misaligned wisdom teeth may lead to pain, swelling, infection, or damage to nearby teeth. We evaluate their position using modern imaging and recommend removal if necessary to protect your oral health. Our team ensures the process is as comfortable and efficient as possible, providing clear guidance before and after treatment for a smooth recovery.

Sleep Apnea & Snoring

Sleep apnea and chronic snoring can significantly affect your quality of life and overall health. These conditions often result from airway obstruction during sleep, leading to disrupted breathing patterns. We offer custom oral appliances designed to keep your airway open and improve airflow throughout the night. This non-invasive solution can reduce snoring, enhance sleep quality, and support better daytime energy levels. Our approach is tailored to your specific needs, helping you achieve more restful, uninterrupted sleep.

FAQs

What is a 3-D dental X-ray used for?

A 3-D dental X-ray captures the teeth, jaw, and surrounding structure with greater clarity and detail. It aids in problem diagnosis, treatment planning, and obtaining precise outcomes for intricate procedures.

Is a 3-D X-ray safe?

Yes, 3-D dental X-rays are safe. They use low radiation levels and modern technology to minimize exposure while providing detailed images necessary for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.

How long does the scan take?

The scan usually takes less than a minute. The process is quick, comfortable, and requires no preparation, making it easy for patients of all ages.

Do I need a 3-D X-ray for every visit?

Not every appointment needs a 3-D X-ray. This is advised only when more detailed imaging is needed for diagnosis, treatment planning, or evaluation of complex dental conditions.

Will I feel any pain during the scan?

No, the scan is totally painless. The machine circles your head to collect images safely and comfortably while you stay still.

How soon will I get the results?

Outcomes can be accessed promptly. Dental professionals will review your images and findings with you, so you can rest easy knowing you’ll be presented with treatment options.
