TMJ Treatment in Wetaskiwin

TMJ Treatment Near You

The temporomandibular joint (TMJ) links your jawbone and your skull. It allows us to perform daily activities such as eating, speaking, and breathing. If you have a temporomandibular disorder (TMD), it can cause pain and difficulties in how your jaw functions. TMD can have many causes and factors that affect the cause, which makes it difficult to pinpoint. But do not worry! TMD can be treated in various ways. If you are suffering from TMD, get TMJ treatment in Wetaskiwin at Wetaskiwin Family Dental.

What Are the Symptoms of TMD?

TMD symptoms may include jaw pain, headaches, clicking sounds in the jaw, facial tension, and difficulty chewing or opening the mouth. These issues can sometimes affect sleep quality.

Symptoms of TMD include:

  • Persistent pain around or in your ear
  • Jaw pain or sensitivity
  • Difficulty eating or discomfort
  • Face pain
  • Challenging to move your jaw
  • Clicking sounds when you chew
  • Headaches

What Are the Causes of TMD

There are various different causes of TMD. With so many other causes, it can be difficult to pinpoint the root cause of your troubles.

Common causes of TMD include:

  • Cartilage damage
  • Jaw injuries
  • Disc slips
  • Genetics
  • Previous injury

If you have jaw pain or abnormalities in your or around your jaw, it may be from TMD. Visit our dentist near you for a consultation to see if you need TMJ treatment in Wetaskiwin.

About TMJ Treatment Near You

At our dental clinic in Wetaskiwin, when you receive TMJ treatment near you, there are various different methods of treatment. For example, you can be given a mouthguard or splint (mouthguard you wear day and night) or medication to ease your pain. TMJ therapy can also be used to treat your TMD. The dentist may massage a muscle inside your mouth for a couple of seconds for instant relief. Or, they may massage the muscles around your jaw. If you still feel pain, the muscle that opens and closes your jaw may be massaged.

FAQs

What are common symptoms of TMJ disorder?

Symptoms include jaw pain, headaches, clicking sounds, earaches, and difficulty chewing.

What causes TMD/TMJ issues?

It can be caused by teeth grinding, jaw misalignment, stress, or injury to the jaw.

How is TMJ treated?

Treatment may include oral appliances, bite adjustments, exercises, and pain relief methods.

Can a mouthguard help with TMJ pain?

Yes, a mouthguard can help with TMJ pain.
