Cosmetic Dentistry FAQ

How much does cosmetic dentistry cost?

The cost depends on the treatment you choose and how many teeth are involved. Rather than quote a one-size-fits-all price, we examine your smile and give you an exact, transparent quote at your consultation, then discuss what fits your budget and any insurance coverage that may apply.

How long does professional teeth whitening last?

Professional whitening typically lasts 6–12 months depending on your diet and habits — coffee, wine, tea, and smoking shorten results. Take-home maintenance trays let you touch up between visits, and many patients refresh once or twice a year.

Is cosmetic bonding as durable as veneers?

Bonding lasts several years with good care; veneers last considerably longer and are more stain-resistant. Bonding can chip if you bite hard objects or grind your teeth, so if you grind or clench, veneers (with a night guard) are often the better long-term choice.

Will veneers look fake?

No. Modern porcelain veneers reflect light like natural teeth, and we design each one to suit your face shape, skin tone, and personality. The goal is a better version of your own smile — not an obvious “fake” look.

Does insurance cover cosmetic dentistry?

Most purely cosmetic procedures aren’t covered because they’re elective. However, when a treatment also serves a restorative purpose (for example, a veneer covering a cracked tooth), insurance may contribute. We’ll review your coverage before you commit.

How many appointments does a smile makeover take?

Whitening and bonding are usually completed in a single visit. Veneers typically take two visits — a design/impression appointment and a placement appointment, usually 1–2 weeks apart. If you’re combining treatments, we’ll map out a schedule that works for you.

Will my teeth be sensitive after whitening?

Some temporary sensitivity is normal and usually fades within 24–48 hours. We use professional-grade desensitizing products to keep you comfortable, and if you already have sensitive teeth, let us know so we can take extra precautions.

Can I get veneers if my teeth are crooked?

Often, yes — veneers can improve the appearance of mildly crooked teeth without braces. But if your bite is significantly misaligned, orthodontics or Invisalign may be the better fit. We’ll recommend the right approach at your consultation.

Can I get cosmetic dentistry if I have gum disease?

Gum disease should be treated first, since cosmetic work lasts best on healthy gums. The good news is treatment is usually quick, and many patients are ready for whitening or veneers within a few weeks.
