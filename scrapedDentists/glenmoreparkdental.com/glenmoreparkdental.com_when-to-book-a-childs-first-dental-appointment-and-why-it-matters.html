Why the First Visit Matters More Than You Think

Many parents wait until there’s a problem—but that’s often too late. The Canadian Dental Association recommends that children visit the dentist within six months of their first tooth erupting, or by age one at the latest. Why so early? Because baby teeth play a big role in your child’s development.

At Glenmore Park Dental, we see first visits as an essential part of preventive care. Early checkups allow us to:

  • Catch signs of baby bottle decay or enamel defects
  • Monitor spacing and jaw development
  • Identify habits like thumb-sucking or tongue thrust
  • Help your child get used to the dental chair

Most importantly, early visits help establish positive dental experiences. When children are introduced to the dentist early—before pain, cavities, or anxiety set in—they’re more likely to enjoy long-term oral health and routine care. 

What Happens During a Child’s First Dental Exam?

A Gentle, Age-Appropriate Approach

Your child’s first dental appointment is more about comfort and education than drilling or cleaning. We tailor each visit to your child’s age, attention span, and emotional needs.

Expect:

  • A quick visual check of the teeth and gums
  • Guidance on brushing, flossing, and feeding
  • Questions about pacifier use, thumb-sucking, and bottles
  • Tips for soothing teething discomfort
  • A fluoride varnish, if appropriate

If your child is a little nervous, we go at their pace. Sometimes, we sit knee-to-knee with parents to let the child stay in your lap. For older toddlers, we offer a fun “ride” in the chair and a simple mirror check.

Catching Problems Early

Some issues can begin before your child can speak. During the first exam, we look for:

  • Early signs of tooth decay or enamel wear
  • Developmental anomalies in the gums or bite
  • Lip or tongue ties affecting speech or feeding
  • Fungal conditions like thrush (common in infants)

We also provide guidance for sippy cup use, weaning, and the ideal time to switch from bottles to cups.

Planning the Dental Journey

Based on your child’s oral development, we’ll help you plan a care schedule. This usually includes:

  • Exams every 6 months
  • Hygiene cleanings once teeth begin to touch
  • X-rays if decay or spacing issues are suspected
  • Orthodontic monitoring by age 7

These visits are more than just checkups—they’re part of a long-term oral health foundation that supports speech, eating, confidence, and healthy growth.

Does CDCP Cover Pediatric Visits?

Yes. If your child is eligible under the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP), basic exams, cleanings, fluoride, and X-rays may be covered. We’ll help you understand your CDCP benefits and how they work with other plans.

For more information, visit our pediatric care page or ask us during your child’s visit.
