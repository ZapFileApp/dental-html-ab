A Chipped Tooth Isn’t Always an Emergency — But It Can Become One

Most people chip a tooth at some point — from biting down on something hard to tripping, falling, or playing sports. Sometimes it’s just cosmetic. Other times, it exposes the inner layers of the tooth and causes sharp pain or sensitivity.

The important thing is to treat it seriously, even if it doesn’t hurt right away. Small chips can worsen over time or become entry points for bacteria, leading to infections or more complex repairs.

At Glenmore Park Dental, we offer same-day emergency appointments for chipped teeth. Whether you’re in pain or just want your smile fixed ASAP, we’re here to help you decide what to do next. 

What to Do When You Chip a Tooth

Step 1: Rinse and Assess the Damage

Rinse your mouth gently with warm water to clear debris. If there’s bleeding, apply a piece of clean gauze or cloth with light pressure. 
Try to find and save the broken piece if possible — we may be able to bond it back.

Take a look at the tooth. Is it:

  • Sharp or jagged?
  • Bleeding at the gumline?
  • Painful or sensitive to temperature?

These signs may indicate a more serious injury.

Step 2: Avoid Further Damage

Until you can see us:

  • Avoid chewing on the side of the chipped tooth
  • Stick to soft foods
  • Cover sharp edges with dental wax or sugar-free gum to protect your tongue and cheeks

Pain or swelling? Take an over-the-counter pain reliever and apply a cold compress.

Step 3: Call Glenmore Park Dental for Guidance

Not sure if it’s an emergency? Call us — we’ll walk you through your symptoms and let you know if same-day care is recommended.

We treat chipped teeth of all sizes, including:

  • Cosmetic chips
  • Cracks with pain or sensitivity
  • Breaks involving the nerve or root
  • Fractures caused by trauma or grinding

If you’ve had recent dental work (like a filling or crown) and that’s what chipped, we’ll help you repair or replace it quickly.
