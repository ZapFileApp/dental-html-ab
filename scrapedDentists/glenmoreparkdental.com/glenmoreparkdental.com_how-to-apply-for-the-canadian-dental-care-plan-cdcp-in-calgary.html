What Is the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP)? 

The CDCP is a federal program that provides financial assistance for dental care to eligible Canadians who don't already have access to private dental insurance. This new initiative is designed to help individuals and families maintain their oral health without worrying about cost.

The program began rolling out in stages in 2024 and will continue expanding through 2025. It’s especially beneficial for seniors, children, and lower-income households. If you live in Calgary and are unsure about your eligibility, Glenmore Park Dental can help answer your questions. 

Who Qualifies for CDCP in Alberta? 

To qualify for CDCP, applicants must meet these basic requirements:

  • Be a Canadian resident without access to private dental insurance
  • Have an adjusted family net income of less than $90,000 annually
  • Have filed a tax return for the previous year
  • Be eligible for Canadian federal health benefits

The amount of coverage you receive depends on your household income. For example:

  • Income under $70,000: 100% coverage (for eligible services)
  • $70,000–$79,999: 60% coverage
  • $80,000–$89,999: 40% coverage

This tiered system helps ensure dental care is more accessible to those who need it most.

What Dental Services Are Covered Under CDCP? 

The CDCP includes coverage for a wide range of dental services, including:

  • Exams and cleanings
  • X-rays
  • Fillings and extractions
  • Root canal treatments
  • Dentures (partial and full)
  • Periodontal care (for gum disease)

It's important to note that not all procedures are fully covered, and some may require pre-authorization or additional patient contributions. The team at Glenmore Park Dental can review your treatment plan and help you understand what CDCP will cover and what won’t.

How to Apply for the Canadian Dental Care Plan in Calgary 

The application process is simple and entirely online:

  1. Visit the Government of Canada’s CDCP website
  2. Log in with your CRA My Account credentials
  3. Complete the application by verifying your income and insurance status
  4. Wait for confirmation — eligible individuals will receive a welcome package with their CDCP coverage information

Currently, applications are being accepted in phases based on age groups and eligibility, with all adults expected to be included by the end of 2025.

If you have questions while applying, the team at Glenmore Park Dental can walk you through each step to ensure your application is submitted correctly.

How to Use CDCP at Glenmore Park Dental 

Once you’re approved for the CDCP, you can begin using your benefits at participating providers like Glenmore Park Dental. Here’s how:

  • Book your appointment and let our team know you're covered by CDCP
  • Bring your welcome package or CDCP ID if available
  • We’ll verify your coverage and help estimate any remaining costs before treatment
  • If you qualify for full or partial coverage, we’ll bill directly where possible, so you pay less (or nothing) out of pocket

Our administrative team is trained to help patients with CDCP billing and will ensure everything is submitted properly on your behalf.
