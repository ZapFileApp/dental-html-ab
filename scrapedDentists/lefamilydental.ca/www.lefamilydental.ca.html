    Our Dental Clinic

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do You Follow the Fee Guide?

    Prioritizing your dental care doesn’t have to break the bank. Our dental team feels strongly about catering to our patients in every way we can to ensure their dental experience is comfortable and smooth. We follow the current Alberta Dental Fee Guide to ensure our patients and their families receive high-quality care in an honest and transparent manner.

    Is Direct Billing Offered?

    We accept all dental insurance plans and submit them electronically on your behalf whenever possible to your insurance. We want your dental experience to be hassle-free and convenient! Insurance companies may be difficult to understand and navigate, so if you have any questions, don’t hesitate to ask a member of our friendly front desk team. We are glad to help!

    When Should My Child First Visit the Dentist?

    At Le Family Dental, we follow the Canadian Dental Association’s guidelines, which recommend that children have their first dental evaluation within six months of their first tooth eruption or by the age of one, whichever comes first. Early visits help set the stage for a lifetime of healthy smiles and good oral habits. We’re here to support your child’s journey to great oral health from the very beginning!

    Do You Accept CDCP and Government Dental Plans?

    We accept CDCP and other government dental plans, such as refugee insurance and social services for people with disability.
    Our practice is committed to providing compassionate, personalized, high-quality care to every patient in a respectful and non-judgmental manner, regardless of insurance coverage.

    How Do I Practice Good Oral Hygiene at Home?

    Practicing good oral hygiene at home is essential for maintaining a healthy smile and preventing dental issues. Here are some simple, effective steps you can follow:

    1. Brush Twice a Day
    2. Floss Daily
    3. Use Mouthwash
    4. Limit Sugary Foods and Drinks
    5. Drink Plenty of Water
    6. Eat a Balanced Diet
    7. Replace Your Toothbrush Regularly
    8. Visit Your Dentist Regularly

    By following these tips and being consistent, you’ll be well on your way to keeping your smile bright and healthy.

    How Often Should I Receive a Cleaning?

    At Le Family Dental, we recommend a professional dental cleaning every six months to keep your smile bright and healthy. However, we understand that every smile is unique, so we take your individual needs into account. If you tend to have more plaque buildup, have specific gum health concerns, or are managing other health conditions or smoking habits, we may suggest more frequent cleanings to ensure your teeth and gums stay in top shape.
    Regular cleanings are an important part of your overall oral care routine, as they help remove plaque and tartar that can’t be fully addressed with brushing and flossing alone. By sticking to a routine maintenance program, we can catch potential issues early -keeping you and your smile healthy and sparkling for years to come.
    Feel free to reach out to our team for any questions about good oral health, as it correlates to systemic health.

    Guided Biofilm Therapy

    Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) revolutionizes dental hygiene by employing cutting-edge technology to achieve superior results. This innovative approach utilizes state-of-the-art devices to precisely detect and remove dental plaque, tartar, and biofilm from the teeth and gums. By combining ultrasonic scalers, air-polishing devices, and fluorescence-based detection systems, GBT ensures thorough cleaning while minimizing discomfort and damage to surrounding tissues. Unlike traditional scaling methods, GBT targets biofilm…the root cause of many dental issues…resulting in a more effective and long-lasting clean.

    Nitrous Oxide Sedation Available

    We provide nitrous oxide as a safe and effective option for managing anxiety and discomfort during dental procedures. Also known as laughing gas, nitrous oxide is administered through a mask placed over the nose, inducing feelings of relaxation and calmness without rendering the patient unconscious. One of the primary benefits of nitrous oxide is its rapid onset and offset, allowing patients to recover quickly and resume normal activities following the procedure.
