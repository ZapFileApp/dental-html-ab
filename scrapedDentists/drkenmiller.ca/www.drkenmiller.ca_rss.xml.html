&lt;span&gt;TMJ&lt;/span&gt;

            &lt;div&gt;&lt;div class="mainimage-wrap"&gt;
&lt;img class="mainimage" src="https://www.drkenmiller.ca/files/images/main-images/tmd-lg.jpg"&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;

&lt;p&gt;TMJ disorder, or TMJ, is the inflammation of the soft joint where your lower jaw connects to your skull. Diagnosis and treatment of TMJ involves determining the source of the inflammation and taking steps to reduce stress on the joint.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Diagnosis&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p class="bottom-margin-tall"&gt;TMJ can result when stress is put on your jaws. This can be caused by misaligned teeth, grinding or clenching your teeth, arthritis, excessive gum chewing, or other causes. We diagnosis TMJ by examining your jaws and reviewing your eating habits. &lt;/p&gt;



&lt;h2&gt;Treatment&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;TMJ is treated by reducing the inflammation in the joint and addressing its root cause. We may recommend anti-inflammatory medication, massage therapy, physiotherapy, or orthodontic correction of bite issues.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Call us to book an TMJ examination if you suffer from chronic facial pain.&lt;/p&gt;

            &lt;div&gt;&lt;div class="mainimage-wrap"&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Up to one-third of injuries to the teeth occur while playing sports. Broken or knocked out teeth are painful and can require dental restoration.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Sports guards are the best defense for your teeth. A properly fitted sports guard protects your teeth from injury resulting from impact.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Sports Guards&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p class="bottom-margin-tall"&gt;We provide sports guards that are customized to fit your mouth. This provides a high level of protection and ensures that the guard is comfortable and doesn’t interfere with your game.&lt;/p&gt;



&lt;h2&gt;Bite Appliances &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;If you grind your teeth at night you could be damaging your teeth. Grinding, or bruxism, can gradually wear away the enamel of your teeth, making them vulnerable. Over time, this can result in decay or lost teeth. A bite appliance holds your jaws in place while you sleep, preventing you from grinding your teeth.&lt;/p&gt;


&lt;p&gt;Call us today to learn more about custom-fit sports guards and appliances.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;span&gt;Wisdom Teeth Extraction&lt;/span&gt;

            &lt;div&gt;&lt;div class="mainimage-wrap"&gt;
&lt;img class="mainimage" src="https://www.drkenmiller.ca/files/images/main-images/wisdom-lg.jpg"&gt;

&lt;p&gt;The last set of teeth to emerge are the third set of molars, called the “wisdom teeth.” These teeth usually begin to grow in the late teens or early twenties. They can cause complications when they grow, such as crowding, pain, infection, tooth decay, and impaction. &lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;The location of the wisdom teeth makes them difficult to clean, and they often impact the health of adjacent teeth. The common treatment for these issues is to extract the wisdom teeth before they emerge.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Wisdom Teeth Assessment&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p class="bottom-margin-tall"&gt;We can assess your wisdom teeth before they emerge to determine whether they will need to be removed. As part of the assessment we’ll X-ray your teeth and gums. &lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;If we determine your wisdom teeth will pose a risk to your mouth, we’ll discuss your options, explain the extraction process, and refer you to a trusted specialist for extraction.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;How NuCalm Works&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p class="bottom-margin-tall"&gt;To begin NuCalm, you take a dietary supplement prior to your treatment. This helps to counteract the effects of adrenaline on your body.   <title>Periodontics</title>
  <link>https://www.drkenmiller.ca/service/periodontics</link>
  <description>
&lt;span&gt;Periodontics&lt;/span&gt;

            &lt;div&gt;&lt;div class="mainimage-wrap"&gt;
&lt;img class="mainimage" src="https://www.drkenmiller.ca/files/images/main-images/periodontics-lg.jpg"&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Periodontal disease can destroy the gum and bone tissue around your teeth. Symptoms can include receding gums, bad breath, loose teeth, and bleeding or sore gums. Left untreated, gum disease can result in tooth loss and has been linked to heart disease.&lt;/p&gt;



&lt;div class="videowrap bottom-margin-tall"&gt;
&lt;div class="optio-video" data-video-id="dentistry/periodontal/about"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Periodontal Treatment&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p class="bottom-margin-tall"&gt;Periodontal treatments help treat and prevent gum disease. Here are some of the services we offer:&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Scaling and root planing&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Deep cleaning above the gumline can help prevent and treat the buildup of infection-causing bacteria. Scaling helps remove tartar buildup, and root planning removes rough areas from the roots of your teeth that make form a home for bacteria.&lt;/p&gt;


&lt;h2&gt;Tooth extraction&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Infection and decay can result in a tooth needing to be extracted to preserve the surrounding teeth and tissues. When extracting a tooth, we take care to minimize damage to your jaws and gums and we provide post-extraction care, including replacement options.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Ridge socket preservation&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;When a tooth has been extracted the resulting socket in your jawbone can collapse and cause possible bone loss. To prevent this, we preserve the socket by filling it with biocompatible material.&lt;/p&gt;


&lt;h2&gt;Ridge augmentation&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;If you’ve been missing teeth for some time you may have suffered gradual bone loss in your jaws. We can partially reverse this loss by augmenting the area with bone grafts, restoring the appearance and shape of your gumline.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Bone grafts&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Bone grafts are used to repair and restore areas where you’ve lost bone tissue. Donor bone tissue is grafted onto the area. Over time the donor tissue is absorbed by your body and the area is strengthened.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Gum grafting&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Gum grafting is used to repair damaged gums or to make cosmetic enhancements to your smile. Grafts are taken from within your mouth or from donor tissue and placed on the area to be restored.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Pocket depth reduction&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;The gums around your teeth can form small “pockets” by pulling away from the roots of your teeth. The pockets can create a home for harmful bacteria if they become too deep. To keep this from happening we reduce the depth of the pockets.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Flap procedures&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Flap procedures are any surgical procedure where we need to reach tissue beneath your gums. To do this we make incisions to open a small “flap” of gum tissue. We close the flap with sutures after the procedure.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Frenectomy&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;A frenum is a piece of connective tissue within your mouth. There is one between your upper lip and the gums underneath that you can feel with your tongue. Sometimes we are born with a frenum that is too short and it can cause discomfort. A frenectomy is the procedure we use to remedy this by removing or altering the frenum.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Crown lengthening&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p bottom-margin-tall&gt;Crown lengthening is when we remove gum tissue from around the base of a tooth. We use this procedure to make a tooth appear longer, or to remove gum tissue that may interfere with the placement of a crown.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;Call us today if you are experiencing the symptoms of gum disease or to book a periodontics appointment.&lt;/p&gt;
