Invisalign in Calgary – A Clear Path to Confidence

Whether you’re a teen preparing for high school photos or an adult wanting a professional appearance at work, Invisalign in Calgary makes it easier to pursue the smile you’ve always wanted while fitting seamlessly into your daily life!

Why Invisalign Matter

A straight smile is about more than appearances – it supports oral and overall health.
  • Oral health benefits: According to the Canadian Dental Association (CDA), crooked teeth can affect your bite, making it difficult to chew certain foods and causing some teeth to wear down.
  • Jaw alignment: An improper bite can also cause issues with your jawbone and joints, which may lead to the breakdown of the bones that support your teeth, as well as TMJ pain, headaches, and migraines
  • Avoiding Sleep Apnea: Research also suggests that jaw position and size may be the root of some sleep issues, including sleep apnea. The benefits of straight teeth and proper jaw alignment are truly important for your overall well-being.
Invisalign aligners address these concerns while offering comfort and subtly. Unlike braces, they’re removable, making it easier to maintain oral hygiene and eat normally.

What to Expect at Southcentre Dental

Many patients are curious about how Invisalign works. Here’s what you can expect during your treatment journey. 

  • This process typically takes about 12 – 18 months, though treatment time varies.
  • Some cases may be shorter or longer depending on complexity.
  • Aligners must be worn consistently for effective results.

Consultation and Evaluation

Prior to beginning your Invisalign treatment, our doctor will take X-rays, images, and impressions of your teeth. This information will be used to create a 3D image of your mouth.

Digital Treatment Plan

Your personal treatment plan will be mapped out using physical and digital images and impressions of your teeth. This plan will include the necessary tooth movements to achieve a beautifully aligned smile. Invisalign will then use this data to create a series of custom-made aligners for you.

Custom Aligner Creation

Your aligners are made to fit your teeth precisely. Each set is slightly different, gradually shifting your teeth into place.

Wearing Aligners

You’ll receive more than one series of aligners, and each one has minor alterations designed to progressively shift your teeth into the desired position over time. You’ll swap out the aligners at home approximately every two weeks, according to your individual treatment program. With Invisalign, no tightening is necessary and your follow-up visits are limited to approximately every six weeks.

Progress Check-Ins

You’ll visit Southcentre Dental to allow our dentist to monitor progress. These visits are quick and simple compared to braces adjustments. You will need to wear a retainer after your treatment, and various styles are available; we will choose what is ideal for your needs.

Link Between Oral & Overall Health

The benefits of Invisalign go beyond aesthetics.

  • The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) states that good oral health can lead to lower cancer rates, better control of diabetes, and fewer opioid prescriptions. It can also mean less time away from school for children, improved work productivity, and a longer life.
  • Studies have also shown that malocclusion, or an improper bite, can lead to anxiety, depression, and social challenges related to concerns about appearance.
  • A healthy, confident smile supports mental health, reducing stress in social and professional settings.

Since straighter teeth are easier to clean, Invisalign treatment can lower your risk of gum issues and the overall health problems linked to them. By investing in Invisalign, you’re not just straightening your teeth but also investing in long-term wellness.

How Often / Who Needs Smile Makeovers

Invisalign is ideal for:

  • Teens: Addressing crooked teeth or spacing concerns while maintaining confidence.
  • Adults: Correcting alignment without the appearance of braces. Invisalign is a solution for patients with mild to moderate crowding, spacing, and bite problems.
  • Professionals: Maintaining a polished, professional look during treatment.
  • Healthy Teeth: You must have healthy teeth and gums to be a candidate for Invisalign. To be effective, Invisalign requires you to wear the aligners for 20 to 22 hours a day. Patients who are committed to following the treatment plan are suitable candidates.

At-Home Tips Between Visits

Keeping your Invisalign aligners in great shape is simple:

  • Wear them at least 20–22 hours daily.
  • Rinse aligners before placing them back in your mouth.
  • Clean your aligners by using a soft-bristle toothbrush with water and a small amount of toothpaste. You might find it easiest to brush the outside of your aligners while they are still on your teeth, then remove them to clean the inside surfaces.
  • Avoid hot water, which can warp the plastic.
  • Brush and floss after meals before reinserting aligners.
  • Do not chew gum with your aligners in.
  • Keep aligners in their case when not in use to prevent loss or damage.

These habits keep treatment effective and aligners fresh.

Signs You May Need Invisalign Now

  • Crowding & Spacing: Aligners move teeth into proper position, resolving overcrowding or gaps. This results in a cleaner, more aligned smile that is easier to keep clean.
  • Overbite/Deep Bite: Invisalign can fix an overbite or deep bite, helping you avoid future problems like worn-down teeth or jaw pain.
  • Underbite: Invisalign can correct some underbites. However, severe cases may require a combination of Invisalign treatment and surgery.
  • Crossbite: This system can fix some types of crossbites, but not all. Our dentist can help determine if you are a suitable candidate.
  • Open Bite: Invisalign can close open bites, which restores proper chewing function and improves your smile.
  • Minor Adjustments: If your teeth are already fairly straight, aligners can make small adjustments to achieve an aligned smile.

Common Questions About Invisalign

Is Invisalign as effective as braces?
Yes, for many cases. Invisalign effectively treats crowding, gaps, and mild bite issues. Severe misalignments may still require braces, but most patients achieve optimal results with aligners. Invisalign treatment typically is comparable in cost as braces.
What if I lose or break my aligners?
If you lose or break an Invisalign aligner, let our friendly doctor know right away. We will work with you to ensure you stay on track with your treatment plan, which may involve wearing a previous set of aligners, your next set of aligners, or ordering replacements.
Does Invisalign hurt?
Invisalign treatment moves your teeth by applying gentle, constant force. Some people may feel temporary discomfort for the first few days of wearing a new set of aligners. People typically describe this as feeling “pressure,” and it’s a normal sign that your Invisalign clear aligners are working and transforming your smile. If you have any concerns or experience continued discomfort, see your doctor.
Will insurance cover my Invisalign?

Many dental insurance plans cover Invisalign treatment the same way they cover braces. Your insurance may pay for a certain percentage or a specific dollar amount of the cost, often with a lifetime cap on the amount it will pay for orthodontic treatment. To determine your coverage for Invisalign, you should:

  • Ask your benefits manager at work about your plan’s coverage details.
  • Call your insurance company’s customer service line and ask about orthodontic benefits.
  • Review your benefits package for information on “orthodontic treatment.”
  • Consult with our team during your Invisalign consultation

Orthodontic treatment is sometimes covered by health insurance rather than dental insurance or by a separate orthodontic rider that you can purchase.

Will Invisalign affect my speech?
It may take a couple of days for your tongue to get used to having your Invisalign aligners in your mouth, and you may have a slight lisp. This should disappear quickly.
What happens if I lose an aligner?
If you lose or break an Invisalign aligner, let our doctor know immediately. We will work with you to ensure you stay on track with your treatment plan, which may involve wearing a previous set of aligners, your next set of aligners, or ordering replacements.
