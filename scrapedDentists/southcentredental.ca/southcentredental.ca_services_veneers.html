What to Expect at Southcentre Dental

Many patients wonder what the veneer process involves. At Southcentre Dental, we guide you step by step so you feel comfortable from start to finish.

Consultation and Smile Design

Porcelain veneers aren’t a one-size-fits-all solution, which is why smile design is such a critical first step. During your initial consultation, our dentist will sit down with you to understand your specific smile goals and discuss every detail of the procedure. She’ll go over the ideal colour, shape, length, and position of your teeth, then review photographs of your smile and show you examples from other patients. This ensures the final result is exactly what you envision.

Tooth Preparation

During your second appointment, our dentist will prepare your teeth for your veneers. Impressions and digital photos will be taken and sent to a lab, where your custom-designed porcelain veneers will be created.

Temporary Veneers

Before you leave, you will be fitted with temporary veneers that serve as a crucial trial run for your new smile. These temporaries will be shaped to match the goals you discussed during your first visit, giving you a chance to get used to the feel of your new smile. This is also the ideal opportunity to get feedback from friends and family and to make any small changes you would like before our world-class ceramist crafts your final veneers.

Placement of Veneers

Your journey to a new smile concludes at your third and final visit. During this appointment, your custom-crafted veneers will be carefully positioned and permanently bonded to your teeth. While the veneers are already created to match the colour of your surrounding teeth, our dentist can fine-tune the shade during placement to ensure a perfect, seamless look.

Final Smile Reveal

Once complete, you’ll see an immediate transformation. Just like your natural teeth, the longevity of your new veneers depends on how well you care for them, so maintaining proper dental hygiene is crucial for keeping your smile beautiful and healthy for years to come.

How Often / Who Needs Veneers

Veneers are a suitable option for many adults, but they aren’t for everyone.

Ideal candidates for veneers include:

  • Adults with permanent teeth and healthy gums.
  • People who want to improve stained, chipped, or uneven teeth.
  • Patients looking for a customized cosmetic solution without braces.
  • Anyone seeking a long-lasting smile makeover.

Veneers may not be ideal if:

  • You have untreated cavities or gum disease.
  • You grind or clench your teeth heavily.
  • You prefer a reversible cosmetic option.

Longevity of veneers:

  • Porcelain veneers are designed to be a lasting cosmetic solution, acting as a lifelong replacement for your natural teeth. 
  • With regular dental checkups and good home care, veneers can endure.

At-Home Tips Between Visits

Veneers are durable, but they still require care. Here’s how to make them last:

  • Practice good oral hygiene. Brush and floss at least twice a day to keep your veneered teeth free of plaque and prevent tooth decay from forming underneath.
  • Avoid using your teeth as tools. Opening bottles, tearing packaging, or biting on things other than food can put extreme pressure on your veneers, which may cause them to chip, crack, or even break.
  • Don’t bite into hard objects or foods. Be mindful of what you’re chewing. Avoid hard items like ice, pencils, and your fingernails, as well as hard-textured foods such as candy, apples, and nuts.
  • Protect your smile with a sports mouthguard. If you play sports or have a habit of clenching or grinding your teeth, a mouthguard can provide a protective barrier against potential trauma.
  • Limit food and drinks that can stain. While the veneers themselves are stain-resistant, the dental cement that holds them in place can discolour. Try to minimize your intake of staining foods and beverages like coffee and red wine, and rinse your mouth afterward.
  • Maintain regular dental visits. It’s crucial to visit your dentist regularly for checkups and professional cleanings. This allows them to monitor your veneers, teeth, and gums to ensure everything remains stable and healthy.

Common Questions About Veneers

Do veneers look natural?
Yes. Customized porcelain veneers are designed to mimic natural enamel. They reflect light like real teeth and are colour-matched for a smooth look.
Can veneers stain?
Porcelain veneers are highly stain-resistant, though natural teeth around them can discolour. Limiting staining foods and drinks helps maintain even colour.
Do I need to replace veneers?
With proper care, porcelain veneers can last for 10 years or even longer. While veneers are durable, they are still subject to normal wear and tear, just like your natural teeth. The longevity of your veneers will ultimately depend on how well you maintain them, as factors such as your diet and daily dental hygiene habits play a significant role in their lifespan.
Can I get veneers on just one tooth?
Yes. Veneers can be applied to a single tooth or a full set, depending on your needs. Many patients choose a small number to correct visible imperfections.
Do veneers actually break?
Porcelain veneers are incredibly durable once bonded to your teeth, but they are not immune to damage. Just like natural enamel, they can break or crack under certain circumstances.
