Why Professional Dental Cleanings Matter

Modern dentistry focuses on more than treating cavities. Managing and removing dental biofilm and plaque is essential for preventing both tooth decay and gum disease, while supporting long-term oral and overall health.

Think of biofilm as a living community of bacteria that stick to your teeth, gums, and tongue. Everyday it grows and matures. These bacteria are the source for most dental diseases and if it is not disrupted routinely, will cause cavities, gingivitis, bad breath, and eventually bone loss around your teeth.

The goal of brushing and flossing along with professional preventative dental hygiene treatment is to break up and remove the biofilm before it causes harm.

As your trusted Airdrie dentist, our goal is to help you maintain a healthy smile for life through preventive care and patient education.

A Gentler Approach to Cleaning Your Teeth

One of the biggest advancements in dental hygiene is the use of modern air polishing technology. 

Air polishing is one of the most effective, least invasive tools we have for removing dental biofilms. It uses a gentle mist of water, air and a therapeutic powder. By adding air polish to your dental hygiene treatment it allows for amore complete biofilm removal compared with traditional polishing and instrumentation alone.

Patients often describe the experience as noticeably more comfortable than traditional cleaning methods.This procedure is available for all patients, but especially helpful when cleaning orthodontic appliances and braces, implants, and crown and bridge.

By combining precision, efficiency, and comfort, we’re able to provide a higher standard of preventive dental care for families throughout Airdrie.

Better Overall Health Through Dental Health

The health of your mouth affects the health of your body. When oral health is not properly managed, it can contribute to systemic inflammation that has been linked to conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and respiratory infections.

For families looking for a family dentist in Airdrie, preventive care remains one of the most important investments you can make in your overall health.
