What Impacts Teeth Whitening Prices

Several factors can influence the cost of teeth whitening services:

  • Type of Treatment: In-office whitening treatments tend to cost more due to their immediate and dramatic results, while take-home kits are a more affordable option, offering flexibility and gradual improvement.
  • Level of Staining: Teeth with heavy discoloration or deeper stains may require stronger whitening agents or additional sessions, which can increase the overall cost.
  • Custom Whitening Trays: These trays are custom-made to ensure even application and better results, but they are more expensive than generic trays and strips.
  • Additional Dental Work: Some patients may need pre-treatment procedures, like scaling or polishing, to prepare the teeth for whitening. These additional steps can impact the total price.
  • Dentist Expertise and Technology: Treatments performed by experienced professionals using advanced whitening systems may cost more but deliver safer and more effective results compared to over-the-counter alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does teeth whitening cost in Calgary?

The cost depends on the type of treatment and the level of staining. In-office whitening typically costs more due to its fast results, while take-home kits are more affordable and allow for gradual whitening. Contact us for a detailed estimate.

How long do teeth whitening results last?

Results from professional teeth whitening can last between 6–12 months or longer with good oral hygiene. Avoiding stain-causing foods and drinks, such as coffee or wine, and using touch-up treatments can extend the results.

Does professional teeth whitening hurt?

Teeth whitening is generally safe and comfortable. Some patients may experience mild sensitivity during or after the procedure, but this is temporary and usually resolves quickly. We can recommend products to minimize sensitivity if needed.

Can professional whitening remove all stains?

Professional whitening effectively removes most surface and some deep stains, but certain discoloration caused by medication or injury may require other cosmetic options, like veneers, for the best results. Our team can recommend the right solution based on your needs.

Is professional teeth whitening safe for everyone?

Yes, when performed or supervised by a dentist, teeth whitening is a safe procedure. It protects your enamel while brightening your teeth. However, it may not be suitable for individuals with active gum disease, severe tooth decay, or extremely sensitive teeth. A consultation helps determine if whitening is right for you.
