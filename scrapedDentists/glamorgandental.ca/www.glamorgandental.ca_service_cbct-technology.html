What is CBCT?

CBCT Technology near you is an acronym that stands for cone-beam computed tomography. It is a unique piece of equipment that captures detailed scans of your teeth and oral cavity, jawbone, neck and throat, and even your ears and nose.

An x-ray machine rotates around your entire head in over to do this, taking hundreds of pictures from multiple angles. Be sure to stay as still as you can. This process is completely painless and only takes about a minute or so to do. The images are then sent to a computer where they are assembled into 3D figures.

Benefits of CBCT

In comparison to traditional x-rays, CBCT Technology scans are more beneficial for dentists and a patient alike due to the following reasons:

  • The images captured are significantly more accurate.
  • Less radiation is emitted.
  • It’s quick.
  • No discomfort or pain at all.
  • Takes accurate measures of your oral cavity.

Additionally, CBCT technology in SW Calgary can help your dental team identify irregular lesions, the source of oral pain, evaluate the current condition of your nasal cavity, nerves, and sinus, as well as ensuring that any more invasive surgeries are performed as effectively as possible. These days, more and more dentists near you are relying upon CBCT scans to install dental implants.

Note: If you’re pregnant, it’s important to speak with your dentist as receiving x-rays of any kind may not be recommended for you.

FAQs

What are the main advantages of using CBCT in dental imaging?

CBCT offers high-resolution 3D soft tissue and bone images, lower scan times than conventional CT, and significantly lower radiation doses. Clinics that offer CBCT technology in SW Calgary can utilize reconstructed multiplanar views to make precise diagnoses.

What types of conditions or procedures are best diagnosed using CBCT?

CBCT is applicable for implant planning, endodontic assessment of complex root anatomy, assessment of impacted or supernumerary teeth, orthognathic surgery planning, and diagnosis of jaw pathology.

What are the radiation risks associated with CBCT scans?

While CBCT exposes patients to more radiation than standard dental X‑rays (5–16×), it’s about 96% less than conventional medical CT scans; proper use of small fields of view and low-dose settings minimizes risks.

Can CBCT scans be used for orthodontic planning?

Yes, CBCT is superior in orthodontics by providing undistorted 3D images of jaws, airway, roots, and teeth to support precise treatment planning and follow-up.

Is CBCT effective for evaluating sinus issues and pathology?

Yes, CBCT also provides high-resolution 3D imaging of sinus structures and is used more commonly (with or without AI enhancers) to diagnose sinus diseases such as sinusitis, fungal balls, cysts, or lesions.

Is CBCT beneficial in assessing airway issues or sleep apnea?

CBCT is a model non-invasive technology for the evaluation of airway morphology, quantification of airway volume and stricture, and screening of obstructive sleep apnea; local practices providing CBCT technology near you include airway analysis protocols.
