We Welcome Dental Emergencies!

At Cochrane Dental Centre, we are available to help you and your family when a dental emergency arises. Cochrane Dental Centre is pleased to provide Cochrane Emergency Dentistry Services – Please Contact Us at (403) 932-3031 if you are experiencing a dental emergency.

What is a Dental Emergency?

A dental emergency is any situation that involves pain or discomfort in the teeth, gum tissue or surrounding bone and jaw joints. If you are experiencing pain from a sudden or a lingering toothache, fracture or knock-out tooth, or have been involved in an accident that involves your teeth and/or jaw should be treated by a dentist immediately.

The Cochrane Dental Centre Team accommodates emergency dental patients by reserving time in our daily schedule specifically for dental emergencies. We are also pleased to accommodate both existing and new patients who are unable to see their regular dentist in a timely manner. To help you before you arrive at our Cochrane Dental Centre office, we will offer our assistance over the phone to advise you on how to remain as comfortable as possible until you are able to be seen at our Cochrane Dental Centre Office.

If you are experiencing any of the following, please contact our office immediately for assistance:

  • Knocked out tooth or teeth
  • Chipped, cracked or broken teeth
  • Injuries to the soft tissues such as biting your lip or tongue
  • Loose teeth
  • An infection of the teeth or gums that is not controlled with over-the-counter pain medications
  • Broken or damaged restoration such as a dental crown, filling, or dental bridge
  • Pain and/or discomfort after recent dental treatment
  • Jaw pain could be an early sign of a heart attack. If you suspect you are having a heart attack, call 911 immediately.

Preventing Dental Emergencies

Preventing a dental emergency one of the best ways to avoid accidental dental emergencies. Booking an appointment at Cochrane Dental Centre on a regular basis for a comprehensive dental exam and teeth cleanings helps us check for signs of weakened teeth, loosened or aging restorations and early the signs of infection that may suggest the presence of infection.

Patients who participate in sporting activities that could cause a dental emergency are encouraged, regardless of their age, to wear a custom sports mouth guard while playing contact sports. A large percentage of dental injuries are related to sports and wearing a custom mouth guard prevents many of these injuries from occurring.
