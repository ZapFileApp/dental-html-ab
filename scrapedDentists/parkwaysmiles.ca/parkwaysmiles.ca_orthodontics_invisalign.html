No Wires & Brackets Necessary!

Invisalign is a clear alternative to Braces

Instead of wearing traditional metal braces, with Invisalign, you now have the option to wear a series of clear plastic trays made out of Invisalign’s patented SmartTrack Material. Ideally, we recommend that you wear each aligner for 22 hours a day. Each Invisalign aligner is specifically designed for you and it will place gentle force on each individual tooth that needs to be moved. Approximately every 10 days, as the aligners are replaced, the teeth will eventually reach their optimal aesthetic place previously designed by our dentists.

Once your treatment is completed, you are then given a retainer appliance to ensure your teeth do not drift from their final position.

Why should you choose Invisalign?

Discreet Appearance:

Invisalign aligners are the perfect option for those looking to straighten their teeth subtly and discreetly. 

More Comfortable Wear:

Traditional braces have metal wires that often cause discomfort. Invisalign is made of soft, flexible plastics called SmartTrack designed to reduce irritation.

Improved Oral Hygiene:

The removable aligners make it easy to maintain a healthy smile because there are no wires or metal brackets to inhibit your traditional brushing and flossing habits.

Fewer Dietary Restrictions:

During typical treatment with Braces, it is common that patients must refrain from eating many foods they previously enjoyed for fear of damaging their wires or brackets. Unlike traditional braces, Invisalign aligners are removable, allowing you to take them out during meals and maintain your usual diet throughout your treatment.

How exactly does Invisalign work?

Over the course of your treatment, Dr. Bullock will supply you with a series of clear aligners customized for your teeth. Each aligner will make slight adjustments to tooth position, a process that is mapped out in advance by your doctor, specifically for your situation. When the aligners are placed on the teeth, they cause the teeth to gradually shift from their current position.

On average every 2 weeks, you will begin using the next set of aligners, which will continue the teeth straightening process until you have reached the end of your treatment plan! 

Your Invisalign Treatment Plan In 3 Easy Steps

Free Invisalign Consultation

During your Invisalign Consultation in Calgary, we will evaluate your needs and see if Invisalign is actually right for you. 

Once we figure out if treatment with Invisalign is suitable for you, one of our trained dental professionals will use an iTero® digital scanning system to quickly create a precise 3D image of your teeth. Once we have the scan, we will map out a specific plan, including the exact movements that Invisalign will make.  This will allow us to determine a rough estimate for how long treatment will actually take. You’ll then get to see how your teeth will move and preview just what your future smile can look like once you complete treatment.

Your Invisalign Treatment

Your custom-made Invisalign aligners will be virtually invisible, so most people won’t even notice that you’re straightening your smile. Over the next few months, during your Invisalign treatment Dr. Bullock, he will constantly monitor you along the entire way to ensure that everything is 

  1. Wear your Invisalign clear aligners 20 to 22 hours a day for the best results, taking them out to eat and to brush and floss.
  2. Visit us every few months so that we can see how progress is coming along.

Your Brand New Smile

As you wear your Invisalign aligners, your teeth will gently and gradually shift into place. At every stage you’ll be able to see your progress and imagine how far your new smile will take you.

Does Invisalign work for my Children and Teens?

Yes it does! In fact, we have a specific type of Invisalign called Invisalign Teen which has a few key differences from the traditional Invisalign treatment plans available to adults:

  1. Coloured Compliance Tabs: Invisalign Teen features a distinct blue dot on each aligner, which will eventually fade away after two weeks of wear (the recommended wear for time for each tray). This technology helps Dr. Bullock determine if movements are finished and ready for the next stage in the treatment process. It also reminds your children to wear the aligner as often as they should, as Dr. Bullock will be able to tell if the duration of has been inadequate.
  2. Replacement Aligners:  Invisalign Teen comes with 6 free replacement aligners, just in case your child loses theirs.
  3. Eruption Tabs: Many teens still have permanent teeth emerging. This is why there is additional space left in the aligners to make room for any adult teeth which need to erupt.

What is included as part of a free orthodontic consultation?

We want to make sure you have a good idea of your orthodontic condition is and what your treatment options. In an orthodontic consultation, you will get an opportunity to tour the office, meet the doctor and the team. The doctor will look at your mouth and assess the complexity of your treatment and review what treatment options are available to you. How does Invisalign straighten your teeth?

The Invisalign® system uses a series of clear, removable aligners.  As you change to each new set of aligners in your series, your teeth gradually move until they reach the desired position.

Is Invisalign right for me?

For the majority of people, the answer is Yes. However, Invisalign is not a plug and play solution, your dentist’s input into the design of the aligners can have drastic impacts to your results. 

Once you visit us for a FREE consultation, we will then determine the best solution for you and explain exactly how Invisalign will work. 

Why should I straighten my teeth?

Misaligned teeth can lead to a variety of different health issues that you may not have even considered. Airway issues, premature wear, tooth loss, and periodontal (gum) disease are just some of the negative causes.

Properly aligned teeth reduce the risk of tooth chipping, breaking, and wear, which could cause pain and lead to expensive procedures in the future to save your teeth.  Proper alignment can also help reduce stress on the jawbone and lessen some of the symptoms of TMJ.

Should I wait for my daughter/son’s adult teeth to come in before considering Orthodontics?

The Canadian Dental Association recommends every child have an orthodontic assessment no later than age 7. That’s because not all orthodontic problems can be treated if we were to wait for all adult teeth to be present. Waiting on treatment can lead to a less than ideal result. Certain orthodontic problems are best treated when children have a combination of adult and baby teeth.
