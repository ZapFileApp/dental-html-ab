So, How Does Invisalign Actually Work?

Unlike other orthodontic appliances which consist of a single brace worn for the entire duration of your treatment, the Invisalign system incorporates multiple aligners. Each bespoke aligner is fashioned from lightweight clear acrylic and is made specifically to fit your mouth. As it’s worn over the teeth like an invisible gum shield, natural forces apply to move the teeth a fraction of an inch. When the teeth have been moved to the desired position, that particular aligner is discarded and the next one in the series is worn. The whole process is repeated until the desired effect has been reached. Treatment can take up to 2 years, but typically patients will have straighter teeth within 12-18 months. Is Invisalign Right for you?

Why Invisalign is the right option

Discreet Appearance

Unlike metal braces the Invisalign aligners are made of clear translucent plastic called SmartTrack, which make it the perfect option for those looking to straighten their teeth without having others notice.

Increased comfort

Unlike traditional braces that can often cause discomfort, Invisalign is made of a flexible soft plastic which makes it comfortable to wear and designed to reduce irritation.

Improved Oral Hygiene

With removable aligners, you avoid having to deal with wires or metal brackets which inhibit you from your traditional brushing and flossing habits.

Fewer Dietary Restrictions

It is common for patients who have metal braces to refrain from eating certain foods they previously enjoyed due to the possibility of damaging brackets or wires. With Invisalign clear aligners, you can remove them at ease during meals and maintain your usual diet during treatment.

Invisalign for Teens and Children!

Just as providing Invisalign treatment options for Adults, we also have Invisalign for Teenagers and Children. In addition, we offer the Invisalign Teen treatment which differs from traditional Invisalign in a few ways such as:

Colored Compliance tabs

Under the Invisalign Teen treatment, there is an indicator on each aligner which fades away after wearing for two weeks. This technology enables us to determine if the movements are complete and ready for the next phase. It also acts as a reminder for patients to wear the aligner for the full 22 hours as well.

Replacing Lost Invisalign Aligners

We understand that mistakes happen and that it can sometimes be easy to lose your aligners. With Invisalign teen, you have 6 complimentary replacement aligners, instead of the 2 aligners provided under a regular Invisalign treatment just in case your child misplaces their trays.

New Teeth Eruption Tabs For Young Children

Many teenagers still have permanent teeth emerging from beneath their gum line. For this reason, there is additional space in the aligners designed to make room for any adult teeth still emerging.

Is Invisalign right for me?

For the majority of people, the answer is Yes. However, Invisalign is not a turnkey solution in that your dentist’s input into the design of the aligners can have drastic impacts to your results. As well, Invisalign must be worn a minimum of 22 hours a day to see the movements necessary.

How does Invisalign straighten your teeth?

The Invisalign® system uses a series of clear, removable aligners.  As you change to each new set of aligners in your series, your teeth gradually move until they reach the desired position. This process can take months to complete.

Why should I straighten my teeth?

Misaligned teeth can lead to airway issues, premature wear, tooth loss, and periodontal (gum) disease.  Properly aligned teeth reduce the risk of tooth chipping, breaking, and wear, which could cause pain and lead to expensive procedures in the future to save your teeth.  Proper alignment also reduces stress on the jawbone and TMJ.

Should I wait for my daughter/son’s adult teeth to come in before considering Orthodontics?

The Canadian Dental Association recommends every child have an orthodontic assessment no later than age 7. That’s because not all orthodontic problems can be treated if we were to wait for all adult teeth to be present. Waiting on treatment can lead to a less than ideal result.
