Invisalign Edmonton – Clear, Comfortable, and Confident Smiles

If you’ve been putting off orthodontic treatment because you don’t want the look or feel of traditional metal braces, Invisalign offers a modern, discreet, and comfortable alternative. Using clear, custom-fitted aligners, Invisalign allows you to straighten your teeth without the wires, brackets, or frequent tightening appointments that come with braces. At Spearmint Dental, we’ve helped patients across South Edmonton and West Edmonton achieve beautiful, healthy smiles while still enjoying the freedom to eat their favourite foods and maintain their usual lifestyle. Whether you’re a busy professional, a teen in school, or simply someone who values a low-maintenance approach to orthodontics, Invisalign is designed to fit seamlessly into your daily routine. With personalized treatment plans, convenient appointment scheduling, and the latest dental technology, our team ensures that your journey to a straighter smile is smooth, efficient, and stress-free. It’s time to get the results you’ve always wanted—without the compromise.

What is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a modern, highly advanced orthodontic treatment that straightens teeth using a series of clear, custom-made aligners. These aligners are designed from precise 3D digital scans of your mouth, ensuring a comfortable fit and predictable results. Each set of aligners works by applying gentle, consistent pressure to gradually shift your teeth into their ideal positions according to your personalized treatment plan. Because the aligners are made from smooth, medical-grade plastic and are virtually invisible, most people won’t even notice you’re wearing them—making it an excellent choice for those who want a discreet solution. Unlike traditional braces that are fixed in place, Invisalign aligners are completely removable, allowing you to eat your favourite foods, brush, and floss without any restrictions. This combination of convenience, comfort, and subtlety has made Invisalign a top choice for busy adults, working professionals, and image-conscious teens in Edmonton.

Benefits of Invisalign Over Braces

1. Discreet Appearance

One of the biggest advantages of Invisalign is its virtually invisible design. The aligners are made from clear, smooth, BPA-free plastic that blends in with your natural teeth, so most people won’t even realize you’re undergoing orthodontic treatment. This makes Invisalign especially appealing for professionals, teens, and anyone who feels self-conscious about wearing braces. You can attend meetings, social gatherings, and special events without worrying about noticeable brackets or wires in your smile. With Invisalign, you can focus on living your life while working toward the straight, beautiful smile you’ve always wanted.

2. Comfortable Fit

Unlike traditional braces, which can sometimes cause discomfort from metal wires and brackets, Invisalign aligners are custom-made to fit your teeth perfectly. They’re created using advanced 3D imaging technology to ensure they apply gentle, even pressure without unnecessary irritation. The smooth plastic material means there are no sharp edges rubbing against your cheeks, lips, or gums. This makes them far more comfortable to wear throughout the day, even for extended hours. Many patients find they adapt to wearing Invisalign quickly, forgetting they’re even in place most of the time.

3. Removable for Eating and Cleaning

One of the standout benefits of Invisalign is the ability to remove your aligners whenever necessary. This means you can still enjoy your favourite foods without worrying about breaking a bracket or bending a wire. It also allows you to maintain your usual brushing and flossing routine, keeping your oral health in top shape during treatment. There’s no need for special tools or complicated cleaning methods—just remove the aligners, clean your teeth as normal, and pop them back in. This flexibility is especially convenient for special occasions, sports, or important social events.

4. Fewer Dental Visits

With Invisalign, you won’t need to visit the dentist as often as you would with traditional braces. Instead of frequent tightening appointments, you’ll simply switch to a new set of aligners every one to two weeks at home. Your check-ups with the dentist are usually scheduled every six to eight weeks, making it easier to fit treatment into a busy lifestyle. This reduced need for in-office visits is perfect for professionals, students, or parents managing a full schedule. Fewer appointments mean less disruption to your daily life while still making steady progress toward your dream smile.

5. Predictable Results

Invisalign uses advanced digital treatment planning to map out the exact movements of your teeth from start to finish. Before you even begin, you’ll be able to see a 3D simulation of your expected results, giving you a clear picture of your future smile. This technology helps ensure a precise, customized treatment plan tailored specifically to your needs. It also means fewer surprises along the way—you’ll know what to expect and when. Having a visual roadmap not only boosts your confidence in the process but also helps keep you motivated as you move through each stage of treatment.

Who is a Good Candidate for Invisalign?

Invisalign can address a wide range of orthodontic concerns, including:

  • Crowded Teeth

    Crowded teeth occur when there isn’t enough space in your jaw for all of your teeth to fit properly. This can cause teeth to overlap, twist, or become misaligned, making them harder to clean effectively. Over time, crowded teeth can lead to a higher risk of cavities, gum disease, and even uneven wear on your enamel. Invisalign aligners gradually create more space by gently shifting teeth into better positions, improving both appearance and oral health. By addressing crowding early, you can enjoy a more balanced smile and maintain it more easily in the long term.

  • Gaps Between Teeth

    Gaps, or spaces between teeth, can affect the overall harmony of your smile and sometimes cause speech issues or difficulty chewing certain foods. These spaces can also leave your gums more exposed, which may increase the risk of gum irritation or periodontal problems. Invisalign works by carefully moving teeth closer together, closing these gaps while maintaining proper bite alignment. This results in a more even, symmetrical smile and improved dental function. Closing gaps also helps protect your gums and reduces the likelihood of food particles getting trapped between teeth.

  • Overbite

    An overbite happens when the upper front teeth overlap the lower front teeth more than normal. While mild overbites are common, severe cases can cause jaw discomfort, uneven tooth wear, and even speech difficulties. Invisalign can be designed to gradually shift both upper and lower teeth into a healthier alignment, reducing the degree of overlap. This not only improves the way your smile looks but also supports better jaw function and long-term dental health. Correcting an overbite can also help prevent premature wear and protect your teeth from potential chipping.

  • Underbite

    An underbite occurs when the lower front teeth extend past the upper front teeth. This misalignment can make chewing less efficient, strain the jaw joints, and affect facial balance. Invisalign can be customized to help guide the lower teeth back into a more natural position, improving both function and appearance. This correction can reduce the risk of jaw pain and help prevent excessive wear on certain teeth. Achieving a proper bite also supports better speech and overall oral health.

  • Crossbite

    A crossbite is when some upper teeth sit inside the lower teeth rather than outside when biting down. This condition can cause uneven tooth wear, gum recession, and even jaw discomfort over time. Invisalign treatment uses targeted aligner pressure to move the affected teeth into their correct positions, balancing the bite. This reduces strain on the teeth and gums while enhancing your smile’s appearance. Fixing a crossbite can also make it easier to chew and maintain good oral hygiene.

  • Mild to Moderate Misalignments

    Even if you don’t have a severe orthodontic issue, mild to moderate misalignments can still impact your confidence and dental health. Crooked or slightly rotated teeth can make cleaning more challenging, increasing the risk of plaque buildup and gum disease. Invisalign is an excellent solution for these cases, offering a subtle yet effective way to align your teeth without drawing attention to your treatment. Because the changes are gradual, you’ll see steady progress with each set of aligners. By the end of treatment, you’ll have a straighter smile that’s easier to maintain for years to come.

During your consultation at Spearmint Dental, we’ll assess your teeth and bite, discuss your smile goals, and determine if Invisalign is the best option for your needs.

The Invisalign Process at Spearmint Dental

1. Consultation

Your Invisalign journey begins with a comprehensive consultation at Spearmint Dental. During this appointment, we take the time to assess your teeth, bite, and overall oral health to determine if Invisalign is the right treatment for you. We’ll also discuss your smile goals—whether you’re looking to fix crowding, close gaps, or correct a bite issue—so we can tailor the treatment to your needs. This is the perfect time to ask any questions about the process, cost, or expected results. By the end of the consultation, you’ll have a clear understanding of your treatment options and what to expect moving forward.

2. Digital Scans

Instead of messy, uncomfortable impressions, we use advanced 3D imaging technology to capture highly accurate digital scans of your teeth. This ensures that every aligner is custom-made to fit your mouth perfectly. The scans also allow us to plan each stage of your treatment with exceptional precision, making sure your teeth move efficiently and comfortably. You’ll even be able to see a virtual simulation of how your smile will look once treatment is complete. This cutting-edge approach not only improves comfort but also speeds up the process of getting your first set of aligners.

3. Custom Treatment Plan

Once we’ve gathered your scans and discussed your goals, we’ll create a custom Invisalign treatment plan just for you. This plan outlines the exact movements of each tooth and the timeline for achieving your results. Each set of aligners is designed to move your teeth in small, controlled steps, ensuring comfort and predictability. We’ll also provide you with an estimated treatment duration, which can range from several months to over a year depending on your needs. Having a personalized plan keeps you informed and motivated throughout the process.

4. Aligner Wear

To get the best results, you’ll need to wear your aligners for 20–22 hours a day, removing them only for eating, drinking anything other than water, brushing, and flossing. Each set of aligners is worn for about one to two weeks before switching to the next in the series. This gradual transition minimizes discomfort and helps your teeth adjust more easily to their new positions. Because the aligners are nearly invisible, you can continue your daily activities—work, school, or social events—without feeling self-conscious. Staying consistent with wear time is one of the most important factors in achieving your desired results on schedule.

5. Progress Check-Ins

Throughout your Invisalign treatment, we’ll schedule quick progress check-ins to monitor your results and ensure your teeth are moving as planned. These visits are typically shorter and less frequent than those required for traditional braces, making them easier to fit into your schedule. We may make small adjustments to your treatment plan based on your progress, ensuring the best possible outcome. These check-ins are also a great opportunity to address any questions or concerns you might have. By keeping track of your progress, we can help ensure your treatment stays on course from start to finish.

6. Retention

Once your treatment is complete, the final step is retention—maintaining your new smile. We’ll provide you with a set of custom retainers designed to keep your teeth in their new positions. Wearing your retainers as instructed is crucial, as teeth have a natural tendency to shift back over time. This retention phase is simple but essential for protecting your investment in your smile. With proper care, your retainers will help ensure your results last for many years to come.

Invisalign for Teens and Adults

Invisalign isn’t just for adults. Teens love Invisalign because it’s comfortable, removable, and doesn’t interfere with sports or playing musical instruments. Adults choose Invisalign for its low-profile appearance and convenience, especially for work or social events.

FAQs About Invisalign

How long does Invisalign treatment take?

Most cases take 12–18 months, but minor adjustments may be completed in as little as six months.

Will Invisalign affect my speech?

Some patients experience a slight lisp at first, but it usually goes away within a few days.

Is Invisalign painful?

You may feel mild pressure when switching to a new aligner, but this is a sign that your teeth are moving.

How much does Invisalign cost?

Costs vary depending on the complexity of your case. We’ll provide a clear estimate during your consultation.

Do I have to wear Invisalign all day?

Yes, for best results, you should wear them 20–22 hours a day, only removing them for eating, drinking (except water), and oral hygiene.
