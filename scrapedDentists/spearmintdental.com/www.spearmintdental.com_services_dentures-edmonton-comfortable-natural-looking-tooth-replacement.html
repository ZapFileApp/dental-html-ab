Dentures Edmonton – Comfortable, Natural-Looking Tooth Replacement

Missing teeth can affect far more than just your smile. They can make eating difficult, change the way you speak, and even impact your confidence in social or professional settings. Fortunately, modern dentures provide a reliable, comfortable, and natural-looking way to restore both function and appearance. At Spearmint Dental, we provide custom dentures in South Edmonton and West Edmonton, designed to fit your unique needs and lifestyle. Whether you need a partial denture to replace a few teeth or a full denture to restore your entire smile, our experienced team is here to help you regain comfort, health, and confidence.

What Are Dentures?

Dentures are removable appliances that replace missing teeth and surrounding tissue. They are custom-made to fit your mouth, ensuring comfort and functionality while improving your smile’s appearance. Modern dentures are made from advanced materials that look natural and feel more comfortable than older designs. Depending on your needs, you may receive a partial denture (replacing a few teeth) or a full denture (replacing an entire arch). At Spearmint Dental, we also offer implant-supported dentures, which provide added stability and security compared to traditional removable options.

Types of Dentures We Offer

Partial Dentures

Partial dentures are used when some natural teeth remain. They fill the gaps left by missing teeth, preventing surrounding teeth from shifting out of place. They are designed to match your natural teeth in colour and shape for a seamless appearance.

Full Dentures

Full dentures replace an entire arch of teeth—either upper, lower, or both. These are ideal for patients who have lost most or all of their teeth. Our full dentures are carefully crafted to provide a natural look and comfortable fit.

Implant-Supported Dentures

Implant-supported dentures combine the affordability of dentures with the stability of dental implants. The dentures snap onto strategically placed implants, providing security, better chewing power, and improved confidence.

Benefits of Dentures

1. Restores Chewing and Speaking Ability

One of the biggest advantages of dentures is that they restore your ability to eat comfortably and speak clearly. Missing teeth can make it difficult to chew certain foods, forcing you to limit your diet. With properly fitted dentures, you can enjoy a wider variety of meals again, improving both nutrition and quality of life. They also restore speech clarity, eliminating the slurring or whistling sounds that often accompany tooth loss. At Spearmint Dental, each denture is custom-designed to fit securely, ensuring smooth and natural function.

2. Improves Appearance

Modern dentures are crafted to look remarkably natural, mimicking the color, shape, and translucency of real teeth. They restore fullness to your smile and help support facial muscles, preventing the sunken look that can occur with tooth loss. By rejuvenating your facial structure, dentures create a more youthful and refreshed appearance. Patients often notice an instant boost in confidence once they see their restored smile. With today’s dental technology, no one needs to know you’re wearing dentures—they’ll simply see a healthy, beautiful smile.

3. Supports Oral Health

Dentures do more than replace missing teeth—they help maintain proper oral balance. When gaps are left unfilled, remaining teeth can drift out of alignment, affecting your bite and leading to jaw strain. Dentures prevent this by stabilizing your mouth and evenly distributing chewing pressure. This reduces stress on the jaw joint and supports overall oral health. With consistent care and professional checkups, dentures can help keep your smile functional and balanced for years.

4. Boosts Confidence

Tooth loss can take a toll on self-esteem, especially in social or professional settings. Dentures restore not only your smile but also your confidence to laugh, speak, and interact freely. When you know your teeth look natural and secure, it’s easier to engage with others without worry. Many patients describe a sense of renewal and joy after receiving their dentures. At Spearmint Dental, we design dentures that help you feel comfortable and confident in every situation.

5. Affordable Solution

Compared to other tooth replacement options like implants or bridges, dentures offer a more budget-friendly way to restore your smile. They provide reliable function and aesthetic improvement at a fraction of the cost of surgical treatments. For patients who need to replace several teeth at once, dentures are often the most practical solution. Even though they’re affordable, today’s dentures are designed with premium materials for comfort and longevity. With proper care, they deliver long-term value and satisfaction.

The Denture Process at Spearmint Dental

Consultation & Planning

Your journey begins with a detailed consultation and oral examination. During this visit, our team will assess your dental health, discuss your goals, and explain all available denture options. Whether you need full dentures, partial dentures, or implant-supported dentures, we’ll help you choose the right solution. This personalized approach ensures comfort, functionality, and a natural-looking result. We take time to answer all your questions so you feel fully informed before moving forward.

Impressions & Measurements

The next step involves taking precise impressions and bite measurements of your mouth. These details help us create dentures that fit securely and feel comfortable throughout the day. Accuracy is key—ill-fitting dentures can cause irritation and affect how you eat or speak. Our technology allows us to capture exact measurements for a snug, natural fit. This attention to detail ensures your dentures look great and function flawlessly.

Fabrication

Once your impressions are complete, we send them to a trusted dental lab for fabrication. There, your dentures are carefully crafted using high-quality, durable materials that mimic the look and feel of natural teeth. The lab technicians work closely with our team to ensure the shape, color, and bite are perfect. This process typically takes a few weeks, during which we may schedule try-ins to fine-tune the details. The result is a set of dentures that fits your mouth perfectly and enhances your appearance.

Fitting & Adjustments

When your dentures are ready, we’ll have you come in for a fitting appointment. Our dentists will check the fit, comfort, and bite alignment to make sure everything feels just right. It’s common to need minor adjustments as your mouth adapts to the new appliance. We make these refinements quickly and carefully to eliminate any pressure points or discomfort. Once adjusted, your dentures should feel comfortable, secure, and natural to wear.

Follow-Up Care

After receiving your dentures, follow-up care is essential to maintaining comfort and performance. We’ll provide detailed instructions on how to clean and care for your dentures, as well as how to handle them safely. Regular checkups allow us to monitor the fit and make adjustments as your mouth naturally changes over time. We also check the health of your gums and supporting structures during these visits. Ongoing care helps your dentures last longer and keeps your oral health in excellent condition.

Myths & Facts About Dentures

Myth 1: Dentures look fake.

Fact: Modern dentures are designed with advanced materials that closely resemble natural teeth in color, shape, and translucency. Today’s prosthetics are hand-finished and customized to match your facial features and smile. At Spearmint Dental, we ensure your dentures fit comfortably and enhance your appearance. Most people won’t even realize you’re wearing them—they’ll just see a confident, genuine smile.

Myth 2: Dentures are uncomfortable.

Fact: Properly fitted dentures should feel comfortable and secure. While it may take a short adjustment period, most patients adapt quickly and find dentures easy to wear. Advances in materials and fitting techniques have significantly improved comfort compared to older designs. If you ever experience irritation, we can make small adjustments for a perfect fit. Our priority is ensuring you can eat, speak, and smile with complete ease.

Myth 3: Dentures last a lifetime.

Fact: While dentures are durable, they aren’t permanent. Over time, your mouth naturally changes shape, which can affect how your dentures fit. Most dentures need to be relined, adjusted, or replaced every 5–10 years to maintain comfort and function. Regular dental visits allow us to monitor these changes and keep your appliance performing its best. Maintaining your dentures properly will extend their lifespan and keep your smile in great shape.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How long do dentures last?

Most dentures last 5–10 years. Over time, changes in your gums and jaw may require adjustments or replacements.

Are dentures painful to wear?

New dentures may cause some initial soreness, but adjustments quickly resolve this. Properly fitted dentures should feel comfortable.

Can I sleep with my dentures in?

It’s generally recommended to remove dentures at night to give your gums a rest and maintain proper hygiene.

How do I clean my dentures?

Brush them daily with a denture brush and soak them in a denture-cleaning solution overnight.

What if my dentures feel loose?

Loose dentures are common over time due to gum changes. Relining or replacement restores a secure fit.

Are implant-supported dentures worth it?

Yes. They provide superior stability, function, and confidence compared to traditional removable dentures.

Will dentures affect the way I eat?

There may be an adjustment period, but most patients adapt quickly and can eat comfortably again.

Will dentures affect the way I eat?

Some patients notice minor changes at first, but speech typically improves as you adapt to your new dentures.

Are dentures covered by insurance?

Many plans cover partial or full dentures. Our team can help confirm your benefits.

Can dentures break?

Yes, dentures can crack or break if dropped. Handle them with care, and we can repair or replace them if needed.
