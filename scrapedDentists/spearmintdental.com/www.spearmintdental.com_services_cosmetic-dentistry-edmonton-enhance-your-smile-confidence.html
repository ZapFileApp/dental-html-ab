What is Cosmetic Dentistry & Why It Matters

Cosmetic dentistry focuses on improving the appearance of your smile while often enhancing function as well. These treatments go beyond basic oral health, helping you address issues like discoloration, misalignment, chips, or gaps that affect your confidence.

At Spearmint Dental, our cosmetic services include professional whitening, porcelain veneers, bonding, gum contouring, and Invisalign® clear aligners. We take time to understand your concerns and design a plan that blends aesthetics with oral health benefits.

For patients in South Edmonton and West Edmonton, our cosmetic dentistry options can help you smile brighter, feel more self-assured, and enjoy long-lasting results.

Common Concerns Cosmetic Dentistry Can Address

Cosmetic dentistry isn’t just about looks—it can correct a wide variety of concerns that impact both appearance and function, often improving oral health and comfort at the same time:

  • Tooth discoloration – Stains from coffee, tea, wine, or smoking can gradually penetrate the enamel and dentin layers, leaving your teeth looking dull, yellowed, or aged. Over time, these stains can become more stubborn and resistant to regular brushing, making them difficult to remove without professional care. Lifestyle factors, certain medications, and even natural aging can also contribute to a darker tooth appearance. Professional cosmetic treatments such as in-office whitening use concentrated bleaching agents and protective techniques to lift deep-set stains safely and effectively, often producing noticeable results after just one session. Many patients report feeling an immediate boost in confidence when their brighter, refreshed smile is revealed, and with good oral care, the results can last for years.
  • Chips, cracks, or worn edges – Even small imperfections, such as chips, cracks, or worn edges, can make teeth appear uneven or damaged, impacting the overall appearance of your smile. These flaws may also weaken the tooth structure over time, making them more susceptible to further damage. Cosmetic dentistry treatments like dental bonding, porcelain veneers, or dental crowns can restore the tooth’s smoothness, strength, and natural symmetry. Each of these options is carefully designed to blend seamlessly with your surrounding teeth for a flawless look. By repairing imperfections, you not only improve aesthetics but also protect and preserve your teeth for long-term health.
  • Gaps or spaces – Uneven spacing can affect the overall balance of your smile and may trap food particles, increasing the risk of plaque buildup and gum irritation. These gaps can also impact speech and bite alignment, making everyday activities like eating or speaking less comfortable. Cosmetic dentistry offers several solutions to close or minimize these spaces, including orthodontics, bonding, or veneers, each tailored to your specific needs and goals. By creating a more even and harmonious look, these treatments not only improve aesthetics but also promote better oral hygiene by reducing areas where debris can collect. Closing gaps can give your smile a more polished appearance, enhancing both your confidence and your long-term dental health.
  • Mild misalignment – Slightly crooked teeth can impact both bite and aesthetics, sometimes leading to uneven wear or difficulty cleaning certain areas effectively. Even minor crowding can create tight spaces where plaque builds up, increasing the risk of cavities or gum disease over time. Clear aligners like Invisalign® offer a discreet way to gradually straighten teeth without the need for metal brackets or wires, making them a popular choice for adults and teens alike. As alignment improves, not only does your smile become more symmetrical and attractive, but oral hygiene becomes easier to maintain. This combination of functional and cosmetic benefits makes addressing mild misalignment a worthwhile investment in your overall dental health.
  • Uneven gum lines – Gums that appear too high or low can make teeth look short, long, or irregular, which can affect the overall harmony of your smile. In some cases, excess gum tissue covers more of the tooth surface than desired, creating a "gummy" appearance, while in others, gums recede and make teeth look unusually long. Gum contouring is a precise cosmetic procedure that reshapes the gumline, removing or repositioning tissue to achieve better balance. This treatment not only enhances aesthetics by creating proportional tooth-to-gum ratios, but it can also improve oral hygiene by reducing hard-to-clean areas where bacteria may build up. Many patients notice an immediate difference, with their teeth appearing more even, symmetrical, and natural-looking after the procedure.

By addressing these concerns individually or in combination, cosmetic dentistry can create a more attractive smile while supporting long-term oral health.

Popular Cosmetic Dentistry Treatments

Professional Teeth Whitening

Our in-office whitening treatments can safely brighten your smile by several shades in just one visit, making it an excellent choice for anyone seeking quick, noticeable results. During your appointment, we use professional-grade bleaching agents and protective measures to ensure your teeth and gums remain safe. For patients who prefer a gradual approach, we also offer take-home kits that allow you to whiten on your schedule, with custom-fitted trays that enhance comfort and effectiveness. Both options deliver longer-lasting results compared to store-bought alternatives, helping you maintain a radiant smile for years with proper care and occasional touch-ups.

Porcelain Veneers

Veneers are ultra-thin, custom-made shells crafted from high-quality porcelain that bond to the front of your teeth. They instantly enhance the shape, size, and color of your smile, making them ideal for addressing chips, deep stains, gaps, and mild misalignment. The porcelain material mimics the light-reflecting properties of natural teeth, creating a realistic, beautiful appearance. With attentive at-home care and regular dental visits, veneers can last 10–15 years or more, offering a long-term solution for a flawless smile.

Invisalign® Clear Aligners

For patients seeking straighter teeth without the look and feel of traditional braces, Invisalign® offers a discreet, comfortable solution. These clear, removable aligners are custom-designed to gradually shift your teeth into their ideal positions. You can enjoy your favorite foods without restrictions and maintain your regular oral hygiene routine without difficulty. Regular check-ins ensure your treatment stays on track, and many patients see results in as little as 12–18 months, depending on their needs.

Dental Bonding

Dental bonding uses a tooth-colored composite resin to repair chips, close gaps, or reshape teeth for improved aesthetics. The material is sculpted directly onto your teeth, then hardened with a special curing light, creating a strong and natural-looking restoration. This treatment is minimally invasive, often completed in a single visit, and requires little to no enamel removal. Bonding is an affordable option for making quick cosmetic improvements that can last several years with proper care.

Gum Contouring

If you have a "gummy" smile or uneven gum lines, gum contouring can help create a more balanced, proportional appearance. Using advanced laser or surgical techniques, we reshape the gum tissue to expose more of your natural teeth and create symmetry across your smile. This procedure can dramatically improve aesthetics and may also enhance oral hygiene by reducing areas where plaque can accumulate. Recovery is typically quick, with noticeable results almost immediately.

FAQs About Cosmetic Dentistry

Is cosmetic dentistry only for appearance?

No, many cosmetic treatments also improve function in meaningful ways. For example, correcting misalignment with Invisalign® can make daily cleaning easier, help prevent cavities in hard-to-reach spots, and reduce uneven wear on teeth over time. In some cases, cosmetic procedures can even improve bite alignment, making chewing more comfortable and reducing jaw strain.

How long do results last?

It depends on the specific treatment and your personal care habits. Professional whitening can maintain brightness for 1–3 years if you avoid staining foods and practice good oral hygiene, while porcelain veneers can last well over a decade with proper brushing, flossing, and regular dental checkups. Preventive care and occasional touch-ups can extend the life of most cosmetic results significantly.

Is cosmetic dentistry painful?

Most cosmetic treatments are minimally invasive and involve little to no discomfort for the patient. We use gentle techniques, advanced equipment, and, when needed, local anesthesia to make procedures comfortable from start to finish. If you’re anxious or have a low pain threshold, we also offer sedation options for a more relaxed experience.

Can I combine treatments?

Yes, many patients choose a combination of treatments to achieve their ideal results in fewer visits. For example, whitening before veneers ensures the surrounding natural teeth match the shade perfectly, or Invisalign® followed by bonding can fine-tune shape and symmetry. Our dentists will guide you on the best sequence for your goals and budget.

Is it covered by insurance?

Cosmetic treatments are typically not covered by dental insurance, as they are considered elective. However, we provide transparent pricing upfront, offer flexible payment plans, and can guide you in finding financing options so your dream smile is more accessible.
