    Sedation Dentistry

    Oral Sedation

    Deeper relaxation for a worry-free visit.

    For patients whose anxiety goes beyond simple nervousness — the kind that makes you cancel appointments, lose sleep the night before, or avoid the dentist entirely for years — oral sedation offers a deeper level of calm that makes dental care genuinely possible again.

    What's Included

    • Prescription medication taken before your visit
    • Deeper relaxation while remaining responsive
    • Thorough health screening for safety
    • Companion required for transportation home

    Benefits

    • Significantly reduces anxiety and discomfort
    • Little to no memory of the procedure
    • Allows us to complete more work in fewer visits

    How Oral Sedation Works

    Before your appointment, we prescribe a medication — typically a benzodiazepine — that you take about an hour before arriving at our office. By the time you sit down in the dental chair, you'll feel deeply relaxed and possibly quite drowsy, though still conscious and responsive.

    You'll remain able to answer questions and follow simple instructions throughout the procedure, but most patients feel like they're in a comfortable, dreamlike state. Time seems to pass remarkably quickly, and many patients remember very little of the actual procedure afterward.

    This amnesic effect is often the most valued benefit of oral sedation. For patients with dental phobia or traumatic past experiences, the ability to get through a full appointment without forming a stressful new memory is genuinely transformative.

    Before and After — What to Know

    Because oral sedation affects your coordination, alertness, and reaction time, you'll need a responsible companion to drive you to and from your appointment. Plan to take the rest of the day off — the medication's effects can linger for several hours after your procedure is complete.

    We'll give you specific instructions about eating and drinking before your appointment. Typically, we ask that you avoid food for several hours prior to your visit. These guidelines exist for your safety and comfort during the sedation period.

    After your appointment, you may feel groggy and drowsy for the remainder of the day. Most patients rest comfortably at home and feel completely normal by the following morning. Don't make any important decisions or sign any documents on the day of your sedation appointment.

    Who Benefits Most From Oral Sedation?

    Oral sedation is ideal for patients with moderate to severe dental anxiety, those who need extensive or lengthy work completed in a single session, or anyone who has had traumatic dental experiences in the past that make it difficult to sit through appointments.

    It's also useful for patients with conditions that make it difficult to sit still for extended periods, or for those with a very strong gag reflex that lighter sedation options like nitrous oxide don't fully address.

    We carefully screen every patient before prescribing any sedation medication. We review your complete medical history, current medications, allergies, and any health conditions to ensure oral sedation is the safest and most appropriate option for your situation.

    Oral Sedation vs. Other Options

    Compared to nitrous oxide, oral sedation provides a deeper level of relaxation and is better suited for patients with more significant anxiety. The tradeoff is that it requires advance planning — a companion for transportation and a day off from responsibilities.

    Compared to IV sedation, oral sedation is less invasive (no needles for sedation delivery) and typically less expensive. However, IV sedation allows us to adjust the level of sedation more precisely in real time, which can be important for longer or more complex procedures.

    The right choice depends on your anxiety level, the complexity of the treatment, and your personal preferences. We'll discuss all options during your consultation and recommend the approach that gives you the most comfort with the least disruption to your life.

    Common Questions

    Will I be unconscious during oral sedation?

    No. You'll be deeply relaxed and possibly drowsy, but you'll remain conscious and responsive throughout. It's not general anesthesia — you're sedated, not asleep. You can still communicate with us.

    Is oral sedation safe?

    Yes, when properly screened and administered. We review your full medical history, check for medication interactions, and monitor your vital signs throughout the entire procedure to ensure your safety.

    Can I eat or drink before my appointment?

    We typically ask you to avoid food for several hours before sedation. You'll receive specific, personalized instructions when we prescribe your medication so you know exactly what to do.

    How long until I feel normal again?

    Most patients feel drowsy for the rest of the day following their appointment. By the next morning, you should feel completely back to normal. Plan to rest and relax on the day of your procedure.

    What if oral sedation isn't enough for me?

    If you've tried oral sedation and still felt too anxious, IV sedation provides a deeper level of relaxation with more precise control. We can discuss upgrading to IV sedation for future appointments.
