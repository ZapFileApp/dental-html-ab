    Pre-Appointment Instructions

    Depending on your procedure, there may be specific steps to follow beforehand. We'll provide detailed instructions when you book, but the general guidelines below cover the most common scenarios.

    View Instructions

    Preparation guidelines

    These are general guidelines for the most common procedures. We'll always provide specific instructions tailored to your treatment when you book your appointment.

    Teeth Cleaning

    Brush and floss normally before your visit. No special preparation needed. Eat a normal meal beforehand — you won't be numb after a cleaning.

    Whitening

    Avoid staining foods/drinks (coffee, red wine, berries) 24 hours before. Brush normally but don't use whitening toothpaste. Sensitivity is normal and temporary.

    Fillings or Crowns

    Eat a light meal beforehand (you may be numb for 1-2 hours after). Inform us of any medication changes. Bring a list of allergies if this is your first procedure with us.

    Root Canal

    Take prescribed medications as directed. Eat beforehand as numbness may last several hours. Arrange a ride if sedation is planned. Wear comfortable clothing.

    Extractions

    Do not smoke for 24 hours before. Inform us of all medications including blood thinners and supplements. Arrange a ride home. Wear loose, comfortable clothing.

    Sedation Appointments

    No food or drink 8 hours before (clear fluids up to 2 hours before). Wear comfortable clothing with short sleeves. A companion must drive you home and stay with you for the first few hours.

    General aftercare

    Following these guidelines after your procedure will help ensure smooth healing and the best possible outcome. We'll also provide procedure-specific instructions at your appointment.

    • Take prescribed medications as directed — complete the full course of antibiotics if prescribed
    • Apply ice packs for 15 minutes on, 15 minutes off to manage swelling
    • Stick to soft foods for 24-48 hours after procedures (yogurt, soup, mashed potatoes, smoothies)
    • Avoid smoking, straws, and vigorous rinsing for 24 hours to protect the treatment site
    • Gently rinse with warm salt water starting 24 hours after the procedure (½ tsp salt in 8 oz water)
    • Call us if you experience unexpected pain, bleeding, or swelling that worsens after 48 hours
    • Attend all follow-up appointments as scheduled — they're essential for ensuring proper healing

    Daily oral health tips

    The best dental care happens between visits. Here's what we recommend for keeping your smile healthy at home — practical advice you can start today.

    Brush Properly, Twice a Day

    Use a soft-bristled brush and fluoride toothpaste. Brush for a full two minutes — most people stop too early. Use gentle circular motions rather than aggressive scrubbing, and don't forget your tongue and the gum line. Replace your toothbrush every 3-4 months or when bristles look frayed.

    Floss Daily (Yes, Really)

    Flossing removes plaque and food particles from between teeth where your brush can't reach — about 35% of your tooth surface. If string floss is difficult, try floss picks, a water flosser, or interdental brushes. The best flossing tool is the one you'll actually use consistently.

    Don't Skip Regular Checkups

    Professional cleanings remove tartar that brushing can't, and regular exams catch problems early — when they're simpler and less expensive to fix. Most people benefit from visits every 6 months, but we'll recommend a schedule based on your specific oral health needs.

    Watch What You Eat (and When)

    Sugary and acidic foods feed the bacteria that cause cavities. It's not just about what you eat — it's about frequency. Constant snacking keeps your teeth in an acidic environment. Try to limit snacks, rinse with water after eating, and wait 30 minutes after acidic foods before brushing.

    When to call us

    Not sure if something warrants a call? Here's a quick reference guide. When in doubt, call — we'd always rather hear from you early.

    Persistent toothache

    Pain that doesn't respond to over-the-counter medication or lasts more than 24 hours.

    Same day

    Swollen gums or face

    Swelling can indicate infection. Don't apply heat — call us right away.

    Same day

    Broken or chipped tooth

    Even small chips should be evaluated. Save any pieces and avoid the area when chewing.

    Same day

    Lost filling or crown

    The exposed tooth is vulnerable. Avoid chewing on that side and keep the area clean.

    Within 24-48 hours

    Bleeding gums during brushing

    Occasional bleeding may indicate early gum disease — highly treatable when caught early.

    Schedule this week

    Sensitivity to hot/cold

    Could indicate a cavity, worn enamel, or exposed root. Usually an easy fix.

    Common questions

    Quick answers to what patients ask us most often. If your question isn't here, give us a call — we love hearing from you.

    What should I bring to my first appointment?

    Bring your Alberta Health Care card, any dental insurance information, a list of current medications and allergies, and completed new patient forms if you've downloaded them. If you have recent dental records or X-rays from your previous dentist, those are helpful too.

    Do you accept walk-ins?

    We primarily see patients by appointment to minimize your wait time and ensure you get our full attention. However, we always make room for dental emergencies — call us and we'll get you in as soon as possible, often the same day.

    What insurance plans do you accept?

    We accept all major insurance plans and submit claims electronically on your behalf — you don't need to file any paperwork. We're happy to verify your coverage and explain your out-of-pocket costs before treatment begins.

    What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

    We ask for at least 24 hours' notice so we can offer your time slot to another patient who may need it. You can call us or use our online booking to reschedule — no penalty fees.

    How do I pay if I don't have insurance?

    We accept Visa, Mastercard, and debit. Our fees follow the Alberta Dental Fee Guide for transparent pricing. For larger treatment plans, we can discuss phased payment arrangements. We'll always provide a cost estimate before starting any work.

    What should I do in a dental emergency after hours?

    Call our office number — our voicemail provides after-hours emergency instructions and an emergency contact number. For life-threatening emergencies (severe bleeding, breathing difficulty, jaw fracture), go to your nearest emergency room.

    How often should I visit the dentist?

    Most people benefit from checkups and cleanings every 6 months. However, some patients need more frequent visits depending on their oral health. We'll recommend a personalized schedule based on your needs during your first exam.

    Is dental work painful?

    We use modern anesthetics and techniques that make most procedures comfortable. We'll always discuss your options for pain management, go at your pace, and check in throughout the procedure. For anxious patients, we offer sedation options.

    Questions about your visit?

    We're happy to help — whether you need clarification on prep instructions, aftercare, or just want to know what to expect. No question is too small.
