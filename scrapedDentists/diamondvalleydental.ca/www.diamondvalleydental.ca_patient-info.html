Patient Info

Everything you need before your first visit.

Whether you're brand new to the practice or returning after a long break, here's what to expect, and how to make your visit as smooth as possible.

Your first visit

Your initial appointment is about getting to know each other. We encourage you to ask questions, express your concerns, and share anything you want us to know, that way we can tailor your care from day one.

Medical history review

Please bring a list of any medications and dosages you take, including vitamins and supplements. These can influence our treatment recommendations.

Clinical examination

Your first visit includes a full-mouth series of x-rays and a comprehensive oral exam of your teeth, gums, mouth, and jaw, plus an oral cancer screening.

FAQ

Questions new patients ask.

What should I bring to my first visit?
A list of any medications and dosages you take, including vitamins and supplements, plus your insurance information if you have coverage. We handle the rest.
What happens at a first appointment?
Your first visit includes a conversation about what brought you in, a full-mouth series of x-rays, a comprehensive oral exam of your teeth, gums, mouth, and jaw, and an oral cancer screening. We'll discuss any findings before you leave.
Do you direct-bill insurance?
Yes, we direct-bill most major insurance companies. Bring your benefits card or send us your provider info ahead of time and we'll handle the rest.
What are your office hours?
Monday 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Tuesday and Wednesday 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM, Thursday 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM, and Friday 7:00 AM to 2:00 PM. We are closed Saturday and Sunday.
Do you accept dental emergencies?
Yes. Call us at 403-933-2225 and we will do our best to see you the same day. Same-day emergency appointments are available throughout the week.
