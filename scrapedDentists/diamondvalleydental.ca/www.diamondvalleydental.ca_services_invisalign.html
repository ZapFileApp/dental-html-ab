What is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a system of custom-made, clear plastic aligners that gradually move your teeth into a straighter position. Unlike traditional braces, the aligners are removable, virtually invisible, and don’t involve any metal brackets or wires.

You wear each set of aligners for one to two weeks, switching to the next in the series as your teeth shift toward their target position. Over the course of treatment, that small steady pressure adds up to a noticeable change in your smile.

Who is it for?

Invisalign works well for adults and teens dealing with:

  • Mild to moderate crowding — teeth that overlap or sit out of line.
  • Gaps and spacing — small spaces between teeth.
  • Bite issues — overbite, underbite, or crossbite (within limits).
  • Relapse from previous orthodontics — teeth that have shifted since you wore braces years ago.

It’s especially popular with professionals, parents, and anyone who doesn’t want braces showing up in family photos or video calls for the next two years.

FAQ

Questions we hear most often

How long does Invisalign take?
Most treatments are 6–18 months. Mild cases can finish in as little as 3 months; complex bite corrections can run closer to 24 months. We'll give you a clear timeline at your consultation.
Does Invisalign hurt?
You may feel pressure or mild soreness for a day or two when you start a new set of aligners. Most patients describe it as a feeling of tightness rather than pain. It fades quickly as your teeth adjust.
How much does Invisalign cost in Calgary?
Treatment cost varies based on case complexity and length. We provide a complete written quote at your consultation, and we direct-bill most major insurance plans. Many patients are surprised it's closer to traditional braces than they expected.
Can I eat and drink with Invisalign?
You'll take your aligners out to eat or drink anything other than water. This keeps the trays clear and means there are no food restrictions like there are with traditional braces.
Will Invisalign affect my speech?
Most people adjust within a day or two. You might notice a very slight lisp at first — your tongue gets used to the aligners quickly.
Am I a candidate for Invisalign?
Invisalign treats most common orthodontic issues — crowding, spacing, overbites, underbites, crossbites. Severe cases sometimes still benefit from traditional braces. The best way to know for sure is to come in for a consultation.
