What Veneers Can Fix

Dental veneers are a versatile solution for common cosmetic concerns, including:

  • Chipped or cracked teeth
  • Stains and discolouration that don’t respond to whitening
  • Gaps between teeth
  • Uneven or misshapen teeth
  • Worn enamel

Veneers vs. Crowns: What’s the Difference?

Veneers are thin shells that cover the front of your teeth, ideal for cosmetic improvements. Crowns, on the other hand, encase the entire tooth and are better suited for more severe damage or structural issues. During your consultation, we’ll discuss which option is best for your unique needs.
If you’re ready to enhance your smile, veneers may be the perfect option for you.

What to Expect During Your Veneers Journey

Personalized Consultation

We’ll evaluate your smile, discuss your goals, and determine if veneers are right for you.

Tooth Preparation

A small amount of enamel is gently removed to make room for your veneers.

Digital Impressions

Using advanced technology, we’ll create precise 3D impressions to ensure a perfect fit.

Temporary Veneers

You’ll wear temporary veneers while your custom ones are being crafted.

Final Placement

Your permanent veneers are bonded to your teeth, giving you a radiant, natural-looking smile.

Caring for Your Veneers

Maintaining veneers is simple. Brush and floss daily, avoid biting into hard objects, and schedule regular dental checkups. With proper care, your veneers can last 10-15 years or more, keeping your smile bright and beautiful.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do veneers last?

With good care, veneers typically last 10-15 years or longer.

‍

Are veneers permanent?

Veneers are a long-term solution, but they may need replacement after years of wear.

‍

Does getting veneers hurt?

The procedure is minimally invasive, and we offer sedation options to ensure your comfort.

‍

How many veneers do I need?

The number depends on your goals. Some patients enhance one tooth, while others choose a full smile makeover.

‍

Can veneers stain?

Veneers are stain-resistant, so they stay bright longer than natural teeth.

‍

Are veneers covered by insurance?

Veneers are typically considered cosmetic, so coverage varies. We can help explore your payment options.

‍

What happens if a veneer chips?

Contact us right away. We’ll assess the damage and recommend repair or replacement.

‍

Am I a candidate for veneers?

If your teeth and gums are healthy, veneers may be an excellent option for you. Schedule a consultation to learn more.
