Can Sedation Dentistry Help with a Gag Reflex?

/ Sedation Dentistry / By carstairsdental

Dental visits are not always a pleasant experience for everyone. Some people fear dental checkups for various reasons. Sensitive gag reflex is one such reason. 

Do you also have a gag reflex? Then, you will know how uncomfortable and unpleasant dental visits can be.

Fortunately, now dentists have sedation dentistry to help people like you.

With this latest dental technique, you can visit your dentist without any fear and have a normal checkup like anyone else.

Let’s find out more about this.   

What is Gag Reflex?

This is a natural response of your body that protects your throat from foreign objects. When a foreign object touches the back of your throat, tongue, or the roof of your mouth, this reflex is triggered. 

This reflex protects your airway from choking, but an oversensitive reflex can create extra problems for you. It makes dental procedures uncomfortable.  

If you face regular gag reflex, you might experience:

  • Discomfort during procedures like cleanings, X-rays, etc.
  • Anxiety about upcoming dental sessions

These situations can compel you to postpone your dental visits. This is where the role of a Sedation Dentist in Carstairs comes.

How Sedation Dentistry Helps in Gag Reflex?

If you feel relaxed during dental visits, then your dentist’s job becomes much easier. 

Let’s find out how sedation dentistry works in such cases.

  • Muscle Relaxation: Sedation helps in relaxing the muscles of your throat and mouth. This reduces the chances of triggering a gag reflex. When you feel comfortable at your dentist, you can better receive dental care.
  • Reduction of Anxiety: Having anxiety means triggering your gag reflex. With the help of sedation, your dentist first calms down your nerves, and as a result, you feel more at ease. This helps you in regular checkups with your dentist. The result is better oral health.
  • More Confidence: When you know your gag reflex will not be triggered, you will feel more confident in yourself.  This means a fast dental procedure. 

How Can You Manage Your Gag Reflex?

Besides sedation dentistry, here are some tips to manage your gag reflex when you visit your dentist:

  • First thing, consult with your regular dentist about your issue and take their suggestion.
  • You can try listening to music during your visit. A distraction may be the best bet for your mind to divert it from a gag.
  • Try focusing on deep and steady breathing. This practice can help you concentrate on the present situation more and less on your gag reflex.

Is Sedation Dentistry Right for You?

If you are still wondering whether sedation will be the correct option for you, contact your dentist as soon as possible. Discuss your medical history and all your concerns so your dentist can make the right choice for you.

Have you been delaying your dental visits due to the fear of gag reflex? Then, it is time for you to make the much-awaited visit to your dentist. With the help of sedation dentistry, your dentist can ensure you receive the best possible dental care without triggering your reflex. 

Do you need more information on this? Contact our dental office today and get professional guidance on sedation and gag reflex.
