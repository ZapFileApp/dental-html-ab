Dental Problems

Infection / Abscess in Tooth

When a cavity or a crack goes too deep, bacteria can sneak into the very center of your tooth. Your body tries to fight back, but because the infection is trapped inside a hard tooth, it has nowhere to go. This creates a painful pocket of infection called an abscess.

It won’t go away with over-the-counter painkillers, and it definitely won’t heal on its own. To help address a tooth infection, visit Twin Dental in Edmonton. 

What is a Tooth Abscess?

Your tooth enamel is like a protective shell. When that shell gets damaged by a deep cavity or a bad break, bacteria get inside and reach the sensitive nerves. Once the bacteria take over, the nerve dies, and the infection spills out into the root and jawbone. The infection creates a lot of pressure, which is why it hurts so much. If you leave it alone, the infection can spread to your face, neck, or other teeth, making a simple problem much harder to fix.

Signs You Have a Tooth Infection

A tooth infection is usually pretty loud about letting you know it’s there. You will likely notice a few of these warning signs:

  • Pounding Pain
    • A constant, throbbing ache that doesn’t stop and often travels up into your jaw, ear, or neck.
  • A Bump on the Gums
    • A small, painful bump that looks like a pimple near the bad tooth. It might even drain a bad-tasting fluid.
  • Swelling or Fever
    • Puffy cheeks, tender glands under your jaw, and/or a low-grade fever.
  • Bad Breath
    • A constant bitter taste in your mouth or bad breath that doesn’t disappear even after you brush.

Tooth Infection Treatment in Edmonton, AB

We use practical, gentle treatments designed to help clear out the bacteria and address the infection. Here is how we can help:

  • Emergency Dentistry
    • An infection is a real dental emergency that needs to be looked at right away. We make room for urgent cases.
  • Root Canal Treatment
    • We open up the tooth, work to relieve that awful pressure, remove the infected tooth pulp, and help stop the infection from spreading.
  • Tooth Crowns
    • After we clean out the infection, the remaining tooth can be left weak and hollow. We place a custom dental crown over it to help protect it, supporting your ability to chew comfortably again.
  • Tooth-Coloured Filling
    • If we catch a deep cavity early, before the bacteria destroy the nerve, we can treat it with a filling.  We clean it out and use a tooth-colored composite filling to seal the tooth. 
  • Sedation Dentistry
    • We know that dealing with an infection is scary and painful. If you are nervous about your treatment, we offer safe sedation options to help you feel more calm and comfortable.

A tooth infection will only get worse the longer you wait. Book an appointment with our team at Twin Dental for tooth infection treatment in Edmonton, AB.
