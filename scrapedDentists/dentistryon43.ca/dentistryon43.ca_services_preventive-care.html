Preventive Dental Care in Beaverlodge

Taking care of your smile doesn’t have to be complicated. At Dentistry on 43, we believe the best dental work is the kind you never need—and that starts with preventive care.

Book Online

From your child’s first cleaning to managing jaw pain that’s been bothering you for months, our team handles it all right here in Beaverlodge.

With a wide range of services offered under one roof, we aim to keep your care close to home and limit unnecessary referrals.

Why Preventive Care Matters

Here’s the truth: most dental problems don’t happen overnight. Cavities, gum disease, and jaw issues develop slowly over time. The good news? Regular preventive visits give us the chance to spot warning signs early and take action before you’re dealing with pain, infection, or costly emergency treatments.

Preventive care also saves you time and money in the long run. A simple filling caught early costs far less than a root canal down the road. Plus, keeping your mouth healthy affects your whole body—gum disease has been linked to heart disease, diabetes, and other health conditions.

For families, preventive care means building good habits early. When your kids grow up seeing the dentist as a positive, routine part of life, they’re more likely to maintain healthy smiles as adults.

Composite Fillings

Caught a cavity early? Our tooth-colored composite fillings blend seamlessly with your natural teeth while protecting against further decay. We use durable, BPA-free materials that look natural and last for years.

Dental Bonding

Minor chips, cracks, or gaps don’t have to be permanent. Dental bonding is a quick, affordable way to restore your smile’s appearance and prevent damage from getting worse—all in a single visit.

Oral Cancer Screening

Early detection saves lives. Our thorough oral cancer screenings are part of every routine exam, checking for signs of abnormalities in your mouth, throat, and neck. It’s a simple step that provides invaluable peace of mind.

Orofacial Myology

If you or your child struggles with tongue thrusting, mouth breathing, or swallowing issues, orofacial myology therapy can help. This specialized treatment retrains oral muscles to function properly, improving speech, sleep, and overall oral health.

Jaw pain, clicking, headaches, or trouble chewing could be signs of a TMJ disorder. We offer comprehensive TMJ diagnosis and treatment to relieve your discomfort and restore proper jaw function.

Frequently Asked Questions About Preventive Care

How often should I visit the dentist for preventive care?

Most people benefit from dental checkups and cleanings every six months. However, if you have gum disease, a history of cavities, or other risk factors, we might recommend more frequent visits—typically every three to four months. During your exam, we’ll create a preventive care schedule that’s right for your specific needs.

Is preventive dental care covered by insurance?

Most dental insurance plans cover the majority of preventive care costs, including routine exams, cleanings, and X-rays. We work with most insurance providers and offer direct billing to make the process easy. Our team can verify your coverage before your appointment and explain what’s included in your plan.

What do cleanings look like at our office?

Our team follows GBT (Guided Biofilm Therapy), a gentle and highly effective system that targets the biofilm and buildup responsible for gum disease and cavities. When combined with our WoodPecker ultrasonic technology, we’re able to remove plaque and calculus with greater precision and less discomfort.

During your exam, we assess your gum and bone health and tailor your cleaning to what you specifically need that day. This ensures safer, more comfortable, and more effective care at every visit.

Can preventive care really save me money?

Absolutely! Catching problems early—like small cavities or early gum disease—means simpler, less expensive treatments. A filling caught early might cost a couple hundred dollars, while waiting until you need an emergency root canal and crown could cost thousands.

What if I haven't been to the dentist in years?

No judgment here—we promise! Many of our patients come to us after years away from the dentist, and we’re here to help you get back on track. We’ll start with a thorough exam to assess your current oral health, discuss any concerns you have, and create a personalized plan to address any issues and prevent future problems. The hardest part is making that first appointment, and we’ll make sure the rest is easy.
