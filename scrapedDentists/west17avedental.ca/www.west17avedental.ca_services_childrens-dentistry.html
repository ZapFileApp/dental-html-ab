Starting Right

First Dental Visits

We recommend a child's first dental visit within 6 months of their first tooth emerging, or by their first birthday. The earlier they start, the more comfortable they become — and the better we can catch developmental issues before they become real problems.

First visits are short, gentle, and completely pressure-free. We let your child explore the chair, meet the team, and get used to the sounds and tools — building a positive relationship with dental care that lasts a lifetime.

  • Fun, low-pressure introduction to dental care
  • Meet the team and explore the environment
  • Parental guidance on at-home oral care routines
  • No scary procedures — just building trust
Prevention First

Preventive Cleanings & Exams

Regular checkups every 6 months are the single most important thing you can do for your child's oral health. We use age-appropriate instruments, work at your child's pace, and make the experience as pleasant as possible — so they actually look forward to coming back.

Every checkup includes a thorough cleaning, cavity check, gum health assessment, bite evaluation, and personalized advice for you as a parent. We monitor growth and development over time, so we catch any concerns early.

  • Professional cleaning with child-friendly tools
  • Digital X-rays only when clinically needed
  • Growth & development monitoring
  • Personalized home care advice for parents

Protection

Fluoride Treatments & Dental Sealants

Two of the most effective — and most underused — tools for protecting children's teeth. Fluoride treatments take less than a minute and provide months of cavity protection by strengthening developing enamel. Dental sealants coat the deep grooves of back teeth where 80% of childhood cavities occur.

Both are completely painless and take just a few minutes. Combined with regular brushing, they dramatically reduce your child's risk of cavities throughout childhood.

Fluoride

Strengthens enamel. Applied in under 1 minute. Ages 2+

Sealants

Coats molar grooves. Lasts several years. Ages 6–14

Advice for Parents

Building Healthy Habits at Home

Start Early

The earlier dental visits begin, the more comfortable children become. Early visits also let us catch developmental issues before they become problems.

Model Good Habits

Brush with your children and make oral hygiene a family activity. Kids who see parents prioritizing dental health adopt those habits themselves.

Limit Sugary Drinks

Juice, sports drinks, and soda are major contributors to childhood tooth decay. Water and milk are always the best choices for growing smiles.

Protect Baby Teeth

Baby teeth hold space for permanent teeth and are critical for speech and chewing. They deserve the same care as adult teeth.

Parents Ask Us…

When should my child first see a dentist?

The Canadian Dental Association recommends a child's first dental visit within 6 months of their first tooth appearing, or by age 1. Early visits establish a positive relationship with dental care and allow us to catch issues at the earliest — and most treatable — stage.

My child is nervous about the dentist. How do you handle this?

Dental anxiety in children is very common, and our team is experienced at making even the most nervous kids feel safe and calm. We use a tell-show-do approach, explain everything in child-friendly language, and go at your child's pace. We never rush or force. Many children who start out scared become enthusiastic dental patients.

Does my child qualify for the Canadian Dental Care Plan?

Eligibility for CDCP is based on family income and whether the child has private dental insurance. Children in qualifying families receive coverage for preventive care, basic treatments, and more. We'll help you navigate the application and understand exactly what's covered at no charge.

How can I prevent cavities in my child's teeth?

Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste (a rice-grain amount for under 3, pea-sized for 3+), floss daily once teeth touch, limit sugary snacks and drinks, attend regular checkups every 6 months, and ask us about fluoride treatments and sealants for added protection.
