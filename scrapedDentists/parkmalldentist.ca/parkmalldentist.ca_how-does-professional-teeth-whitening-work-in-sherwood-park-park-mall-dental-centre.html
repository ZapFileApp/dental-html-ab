How Does Professional Teeth Whitening Work in Sherwood Park? | Park Mall Dental Centre

A bright, confident smile is one of the first things people notice—it can instantly enhance your appearance and boost your self-esteem. Over time, however, our teeth can lose their sparkle due to factors like aging, coffee, tea, red wine, or even certain medications.

If you’re considering professional teeth whitening in Sherwood Park, you’re already on the right track toward a healthier, whiter smile. Visiting a trusted dental clinic in Sherwood ensures you receive safe, effective whitening treatments tailored to your needs. In this detailed guide, we’ll explain how professional whitening works, what to expect during the procedure, its benefits, costs, and how to maintain your dazzling results.

Understanding the Professional Teeth Whitening Process

Professional whitening is a safe, fast, and effective cosmetic dental procedure designed to lighten the shade of your teeth by several levels. At Park Mall Dental Centre, the process begins with a detailed dental assessment.

  1. Comprehensive Examination : Before whitening begins, your dentist examines your teeth and gums to ensure they’re healthy enough for treatment. Any issues such as cavities or gum disease are addressed first, as whitening on compromised teeth can cause sensitivity.
  2. Shade Selection : The dentist records your current tooth shade and discusses your desired outcome. This helps set realistic expectations and tailor the whitening process to your goals.
  3. Preparation & Protection : A protective gel or rubber barrier is applied to your gums and lips to prevent irritation from the whitening solution.
  4. Application of Whitening Gel : A professional-grade gel containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide is applied to the surface of your teeth. These active ingredients penetrate the enamel and break down stains caused by food, beverages, or aging.
  5. Light Activation (Optional) : In many cases, an LED or laser light is used to activate the gel and accelerate the whitening process. This enhances the gel’s effectiveness and allows for quicker, more uniform results.
  6. Rinse & Results Review: After 15–20 minutes, the gel is removed, and the process may be repeated for deeper whitening. At the end of your session, the dentist compares your before-and-after shades most patients see a difference of 6 to 8 shades lighter in just one visit.

Is Professional Whitening Safe for Everyone?

Absolutely, when done by a licensed dentist, professional teeth whitening is safe and clinically tested. The treatment is customized to your specific oral condition, ensuring your comfort and protection.

That said, whitening might not be suitable for:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women
  • Children under 16
  • Patients with untreated cavities or gum disease
  • Individuals with dental restorations (like crowns or veneers) that don’t respond to whitening agents

If you fall into any of these categories, your dentist will suggest safe alternatives such as polishing or whitening toothpaste designed for sensitive teeth.

What to Expect After Whitening

You’ll notice immediate results after your whitening session. Your smile will appear brighter and cleaner. However, for the first 24 to 48 hours, your enamel is more porous and prone to staining.

To protect your results:

  • Avoid foods and drinks that can stain (coffee, wine, dark sauces, berries).
  • Quit smoking or using tobacco products.
  • Brush with a non-abrasive whitening toothpaste.
  • Rinse with water after meals to minimize discoloration.

Your dentist may also recommend a touch-up whitening kit for home use to maintain your results between professional visits.

How Long Do Whitening Results Last?

The results of professional teeth whitening in Sherwood Park can last anywhere from 6 months to 1 year, depending on your habits and oral care routine. With proper maintenance, including regular dental cleanings and avoiding staining foods, you can extend the life of your bright smile even longer.

To keep your teeth whiter for the long term:

  • Schedule dental cleanings every 6 months.
  • Limit stain-causing foods.
  • Drink through a straw when possible.
  • Use whitening toothpaste periodically.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How long does the whitening session take?

A typical professional whitening session lasts about 60 to 90 minutes, depending on your current shade and desired brightness.

2. Does whitening cause tooth sensitivity?

Some patients may experience mild sensitivity, but it’s temporary and usually resolves within 24 hours. Your dentist can use desensitizing gels to minimize discomfort.

3. How white will my teeth get after treatment?

Most patients achieve teeth that are 6–8 shades lighter after one treatment. Results vary based on the natural color of your enamel and the severity of stains.

4. Can I eat normally after whitening?

Yes, but avoid dark-colored foods and beverages (like coffee, wine, or soy sauce) for at least 48 hours to maintain your results. 

5. How often should I get professional whitening?

For best results, schedule touch-ups every 6–12 months, depending on your lifestyle and oral hygiene habits. 

6. Is teeth whitening suitable for crowns or veneers?

No, whitening products only affect natural teeth. Your dentist can help you match your restorations with the new shade if needed.
