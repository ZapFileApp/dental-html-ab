Keeping Your Teeth Healthy in Maple Ridge

Poor oral hygiene can lead to several problems, including gum disease, tooth decay, bad breath or even dental surgery. But with proper care, you can keep your mouth and gums healthy and avoid these issues. That’s where general dentistry comes in.

The area of general dentistry is responsible for identifying and preventing dental diseases and conditions affecting the natural teeth, gums, and other oral structures. Even if you take exceptional care of your teeth at home, it is recommended to visit the dentist regularly for examinations and professional teeth cleanings.

A handful of dental clinics offering general dentistry in Maple Ridge, BC, provide quality dental care services. These clinics deliver dental care services, such as teeth cleaning, whitening, and more. Westgate Dental Clinic is an excellent place to go if you need any of these services. 

What is General Dentistry?

General dentistry encompasses a comprehensive spectrum of preventive care, restorative methods, and diagnostic procedures aimed at maintaining your sparkling smile. It forms the foundation for good oral health and directly impacts a person’s overall well-being, especially those with an active lifestyle.

Preventative Care

Includes regular checkups or cleanings, oral examinations, and diagnostic procedures like X-rays. Cleanings remove plaque and tartar buildup, which harbour bacteria and lead to gingivitis and periodontitis. Dental examinations identify early signs of decay, gum disease, and oral cancer.

Restorative Dentistry

This focuses on restoring the function and aesthetics of damaged teeth compromised by decay, trauma, or fractures. Common procedures include fillings (composite resin or amalgam), crowns (porcelain or zirconia), and bridges (fixed restorations replacing missing teeth). 

Other General Dentistry Services

Extractions (removal of teeth), root canal care (removal of infected pulp), and periodontal treatments (addressing gum disease) also fall under the umbrella of general dentistry.

Our General Dentistry Services

Dental Fillings

Dental fillings restore the function and beauty of affected tooth compromised by decay (cavities). Early detection and treatment with fillings prevent the need for more complex procedures in the future.

Composite Resin Fillings

These tooth-coloured fillings are common due to their natural look and strong bond to the tooth structure.

Amalgam Fillings

These traditional metal fillings are durable and cost-effective compared to composite fillings.

Glass Ionomer Fillings

Promote remineralization of the surrounding tooth structure. Well-suited for pediatric dentistry and particular areas with minimal occlusal (biting) forces.

The filling procedure involves:

  • Local anesthesia to numb the area
  • Removal of decayed tooth structure using a dental drill
  • Cleaning and disinfecting the cavity
  • Placing and shaping the filling material
  • Curing the filling material with a light source

Wisdom Tooth Extraction

Third molars (wisdom teeth) erupt later than all other permanent teeth. But in some cases, we need to extract third molars due to several reasons:

  • Impaction (unable to erupt fully due to lack of space)
  • Recurrent infections
  • Damage to surrounding teeth

Wisdom tooth extraction is a simple or surgical procedure, depending on the tooth’s position and angulation. Simple extractions involve removing the tooth with forceps after numbing the area. Surgical extractions involve an incision in the gum tissue and bone to access the tooth.

Sleep Apnea Treatment

A sleep disorder marked by breathing interruptions during sleep, which can lead to reduced oxygen levels and fragmented sleep.

Westgate Dental & Implant Centre offers sleep apnea treatment like custom-made mouthpieces and night guards that reposition the lower jaw slightly forward to open the airway and prevent apnea episodes. 

Implant Overdentures

Implant overdentures receive additional support and stability from dental implants. Compared to traditional dentures, this improves:

  • Tooth function
  • Chewing ability
  • Overall patient satisfaction

The process involves placing dental implants in the jawbone and attaching the denture to these implants using specialized attachments.

Dental Implants

A general dentist places short titanium screws in your jawbone to replicate tooth roots to provide a strong foundation for supporting dental implants, crowns, bridges, or dentures.

This method offer many benefits over traditional bridges or dentures, including:

  • Improved functionality and chewing ability
  • Enhanced aesthetics and a more natural appearance
  • Preservation of jawbone health due to osseointegration (fusion between implant and bone)

White for Life Program

Westgate Dental & Implant Centre may offer various teeth whitening options, including the “White for Life” program. 

This program involves a combination of in-office professional whitening treatments and take-home whitening kits to help our patients maintain their results over time.

Dental Cavity Treatment

Cavities are diagnosed through visual examination (regular exams), dental X-rays, and potentially using diagnostic tools like lasers. These are areas of tooth decay caused by bacterial breakdown of tooth enamel and dentin. Early detection and treatment are necessary to prevent further damage.

The primary dental cavity treatment  is filling the decayed area. In advanced cases, root canal therapy or tooth extraction may be necessary.

  • Fluoride Treatment: For remineralization of early-stage cavities.
  • Dental Fillings: As discussed earlier, to restore decayed tooth structure.
  • Crowns: For extensive decay affecting a significant portion of the tooth structure.

Periodontal Disease Treatment (Gum Disease)

Periodontal disease is a progressive inflammatory condition affecting the gums and supporting bone. Gingivitis is the early stage and is characterized by gum inflammation. Periodontitis is the advanced stage, leading to bone loss and tooth loss if left untreated.

Treatment for gum disease includes:

Scaling and Root Planing (SRP)

Non-surgical removal of plaque and calculus (tartar) from teeth and root surfaces to remove bacterial build-up and promote healing.

Antibiotics

May be prescribed in some cases to combat infection.

Surgical Procedures

For advanced periodontitis, gum surgery may be necessary to access deeper areas for cleaning or regenerating lost bone.

Gingivitis Treatment

Gingivitis is the first stage of gum disease, characterized by inflammation and bleeding gums. It is treated with correct dental hygiene practices and professional cleanings.

Gingivitis treatment focuses on removing plaque and calculus buildup through cleanings and an improved dental practice at home, including brushing and flossing twice daily.

Professional Dental Cleaning

Regular professional cleanings remove plaque and tartar buildup inaccessible with brushing and flossing alone.

Improved Oral Hygiene

Maple Ridge family dentist will instruct on correct brushing and flossing techniques to remove plaque effectively at home.

Antimicrobial Mouthwash

May be recommended as an adjunct to mechanical cleaning to control plaque and gingivitis.

Root Canal Treatment

When a cavity reaches the pulp (inner chamber) of the tooth, causing inflammation or infection, root canal treatment is needed.

The general dentist starts by removing the infected pulp, cleaning and disinfecting the canals within the tooth root, and sealing them with a filling material. A crown is placed on the tooth to restore strength and function.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the perks of composite resin fillings over amalgam fillings?

Composite resin fillings offer several advantages:

  • Tooth-coloured: They blend seamlessly with the natural tooth structure. Provides a more aesthetically pleasing appearance compared to the dark metal of amalgam fillings.
  • Bonding: Composite resin bonds straight to the tooth enamel. This creates a stronger seal and potentially reduces the need for extensive tooth removal for cavity preparation.
  • Conservative approach: Because of their bonding capability, composite fillings demand less removal of healthy tooth structure than amalgam fillings.

When is wisdom tooth extraction necessary?

Wisdom tooth extraction may be necessary in several situations:

  • Impaction: If the wisdom tooth is unable to erupt fully due to lack of space, causing pain, infection, or pressure on surrounding teeth.
  • Pericoronitis: Swelling of the gum tissue around a partially erupted wisdom tooth. This leads to pain, swelling, and difficulty opening the mouth.
  • Angulated wisdom tooth: When the wisdom tooth grows in at an angle, increasing the risk of damaging adjacent teeth or nerves.
  • Cyst or tumour development: Wisdom teeth contribute to the formation of cysts/tumours in the jawbone.

Are dental implants suitable for everyone?

Dental implants are a versatile solution for replacing missing teeth. However, they are not suitable for everyone. Here are some factors to consider:

  • Jawbone Health: Adequate bone density in the implant placement area is necessary for successful osseointegration (fusion with the jawbone). Patients with bone loss may require bone grafting procedures before implant placement.
  • Overall Health: Certain medical conditions may increase the risk of implant failure, so a thorough medical history evaluation is essential.
  • Smoking: Smoking can significantly impede healing and increase the risk of implant complications.
