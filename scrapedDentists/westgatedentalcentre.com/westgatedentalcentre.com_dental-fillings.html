Are you struggling with tooth decay or cavities? Wondering how to restore the functionality and aesthetics of your damaged teeth? Westgate Dental & Implant Centre offers dental filling services paired with advanced materials and precise techniques to ensure durable, natural-looking restorations.

What are Dental Fillings?

These are common restorative procedures used to repair teeth damaged by decay, wear, or minor fractures. This involves removing the caries and filling the cavity with a specialized material to restore the tooth’s structure, function, and appearance. When damage is too extensive for a filling, a dental crown may be recommended to strengthen and protect the remaining tooth

Westgate Dental & Implant Centre offers aid with our best oral care practices to address your discomfort.

Types of Dental Fillings We Offer

Amalgam Fillings

Dental amalgam, which uses composite metals fillings, such as silver amalgam fillings, mercury, tin, and copper, has long been a staple in restorative dentistry in Maple Ridge due to its robustness and durability. Well-suited for posterior teeth where the forces of chewing are most significant.

  • Dental amalgams are highly resistant to wear and tear. Ideal for any chewing surface. With proper care, they can last 10-15 years or longer.
  • Cost-effective and relatively simple to place. They also do not shrink over time, which helps maintain the integrity of the amalgam filling. 

Composite Fillings

Composite resin is a top pick for our patients seeking a more aesthetic solution. Made from a combination of plastic and fine glass particles, a composite filling can be nearly identical to the teeth’s natural colour.

  • Highly resistant to damage. Ideal for areas that need heavy chewing. Like amalgam restorations, these dental filling materials last 10-15 years or longer.
  • Composite resin material provides good adhesion to the tooth structure, which helps to support the remaining tooth.

Gold Fillings

Gold fillings are renowned for their durability and biocompatibility. Composed of a gold alloy, these are perfect for patients who prioritize long-term performance and strength.

  • Resistant to wear and corrosion and provide a service life of 15-20 years or more. Also, they’re effective in tooth areas subject to high pressure.
  • Gold’s biocompatibility minimizes the risk of allergic reactions or irritation. They do not shrink or change shape over time, maintaining their effectiveness and fit.

Porcelain Fillings

Porcelain, also known as ceramic, offers a high aesthetic appeal and is often used in tooth areas where both function and appearance are important.

  • Made from a type of ceramic matched to the natural colour of the teeth.
  • Highly resistant to staining and durable enough to withstand normal biting forces. Compared to metal fillings, it provides a more natural look.

What to Expect During Your Dental Filling Appointment

Initial Examination and Diagnosis

The first step in our tooth-filling process is an oral examination. We’ll check your medical or family history and use diagnostic tools, including digital radiography and intraoral cameras, to accurately assess the extent of the recurrent decay or damage.

  • Digital radiography provides high-resolution images of the sound tooth structure remains. We do detailed analysis of decay, fractures, or structural weaknesses.
  • Intraoral cameras are small, portable devices that capture detailed images of the tooth’s surface. Gives real-time visuals that aid in diagnosis and even in an emergency dental treatment.

Anesthesia and Tooth Preparation

Once the diagnosis is ready, the next step is to prepare the tooth for filling. This includes:

  • Local anesthetic is administered via an injection near the ailing tooth, this ensures that the patient remains comfortable throughout the procedure. It desensitizes the tooth and surrounding tissues to minimize discomfort.
  • For preparation, the decayed or damaged portion of the tooth is removed using burs and hand instruments. The cavity is cleaned to remove any remaining decay and debris to ensure a clean surface for the restorative material to adhere to.

Filling Placement

With the cavity prepared, the next phase is to place the cavity fillings of your choice.

Amalgam

Involves cleaning the cavity, placing the amalgam, and allowing it to harden in the cavity. The material is then carved to match the tooth’s natural contours.

Composite Resin

The resin is put in layers, each hardened with a special curing light. This allows for precise shaping and bonding to the enamel structure.

Gold

Involves creating an impression of the tooth, fabricating the gold filling in a dental laboratory, and then cementing it into place.

Porcelain

Custom-made in a laboratory based on impressions taken from the patient’s existing teeth. Once fabricated, the porcelain is bonded using an adhesive.

Finishing and Polishing

After placing the filling, we ensure that it integrates with the surrounding tooth structure.

  • The filling is checked for proper occlusion, or bite alignment, to ensure that it does not interfere with normal chewing and speaking.
  • We polish the replacement filling to smooth any rough surfaces and enhance the overall finish to reduce the risk of decay causing bacteria and ensures a comfortable bite.

Post-Procedure Care

Following the placement of the filling, our patients receive detailed instructions for care:

  • Advise on how to care for inserts, including recommendations for oral hygiene practices and dietary adjustments. (Note: New composite fillings may require avoiding sticky or hard foods for a short period).
  • Regular follow-up visits to inspect the condition of the filling, assess its health, and address any concerns.

Benefits of Treating Cavities Early

Restoration of Teeth Function

Repair teeth’s structural integrity to withstand the forces of chewing and tooth grinding. By filling cavities and repairing damage, you can preserve the tooth structure remaining. 

Prevention of Further Decay

They act as a barrier, sealing the cavity and preventing bacteria from entering and causing further decay. By restoring, patients experience reduced tooth sensitivity and improved comfort during daily activities.

Enhancement of Aesthetics

Porcelain and composite materials match the innate colour and translucency of teeth. This provides an aesthetically pleasing result. 

Cost-Effectiveness

Among restorative options, traditional fillings are more affordable than partial crowns or inlays – a practical choice for many patients.

Versatility in Treatment

The standard treatment for cavities is to address tooth decay in both primary (baby) and permanent teeth.

Dental Filling FAQs

What foods should I avoid after getting a dental filling?

After cavity filling, it’s advisable to avoid sticky or hard foods such as caramel, chewing gum, nuts, and hard candies, as these can dislodge or damage the fills.

Avoid very hot or cold foods and drinks if you experience tooth sensitivity. You better choose soft foods and bite on the opposite side of your mouth for the first 24 hours.

How should I care for my teeth after getting a dental filling?

Continue brushing your teeth twice with fluoride toothpaste and flossing every day to remove plaque and prevent cavities. Be gentle around the newly filled tooth initially. Regular check-ups and cleanings are needed to maintain them and overall oral hygiene.

Does smoking affect the longevity of fillings?

Yes, smoking negatively impacts their longevity. The chemicals in tobacco can cause the filling material to degrade over time, leading to staining and increased wear. It contributes as well to gum disease and decay affecting the overall health of your teeth.
