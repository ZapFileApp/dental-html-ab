How Orthodontic Treatments Influence Your Facial Symmetry and Profile

While perfect symmetry is rare, looking for an “orthodontist near me” helps create an aesthetically pleasing and harmonious appearance. Orthodontists have the power to improve function and aesthetics, whether correcting a misaligned bite, adjusting the chin position, or reshaping the jaw.

Do you want to change your face symmetry without plastic surgery? With the aid of orthodontic specialists, you can gain confidence by improving your smile.

Key Takeaways:

  • Many perceive symmetrical faces as more attractive and healthy. Even small genetic differences can affect symmetry.
  • A shifted oral midline, an imbalance along the imaginary center line of the face, can make the face appear uneven.
  • Braces and clear aligners are common ways to correct unevenness by shifting teeth, fixing bite issues, and improving smile aesthetics.

Why Does Symmetry Matter

Facial symmetry is a highly desirable trait where facial features match in size, shape, and placement. The Evolutionary Advantage theory suggests that facial evenness signals good health, strong genes, and a robust immune system.

Unevenness can arise from developmental disruptions caused by disease, infection, or parasites, indicating underlying issues. A study published in Science Direct reinforces that more symmetrical faces are more attractive and healthier. Even minor genetic variations can subtly affect facial evenness.

Common Causes of Facial Asymmetry

  • Genetics. Inherited traits may cause one side of the face to develop differently.
  • Growth Irregularities. Differences in how the upper and lower jaws grow can lead to imbalances.
  • Bad Oral Habits. Thumb sucking, prolonged pacifier use, or mouth breathing can cause uneven facial growth.
  • Trauma or Injury. A childhood accident can result in permanent unevenness. Consider this a dental emergency and visit your family dentist immediately. 
  • Sleeping Position. Regularly sleeping on one side can, over time, subtly affect skull development.
  • Dental Health Issues. Missing teeth, an uneven bite, or crowding can contribute to asymmetry over time.
 
Perfect evenness is not always necessary for beauty. Some degree of asymmetry adds uniqueness and character to a person’s face. However, it can sometimes cause functional issues such as pain, uneven tooth wear, and difficulty chewing or speaking.

How Orthodontics Affects Symmetry

Orthodontists analyze, diagnose, and treat imbalances. The treatment approach depends on whether the asymmetry is skeletal (bone-related) or tooth-related:

  • Correct mild cases (caused by teeth misalignment) with braces or clear aligners.
  • Moderate cases may require functional appliances or expansion techniques to guide jaw growth.
  • Severe situations (caused by jaw misalignment) require orthognathic surgery in combination with traditional braces to reposition the bones.

Key Areas Affected by Orthodontic Treatment

Midline Alignment

The midline of the face is an imaginary vertical line that runs down the center. A shifted oral midline can make the whole face appear uneven. A reliable dental team treatment realigns this with the midline to improve balance.

Jawline and Chin Positioning

A misaligned jaw can make the chin appear off-center. Braces and elastics help guide them into a more symmetrical position. You may require genioplasty (chin surgery) in severe cases.

Occlusal Plane and Lip Symmetry

The occlusal plane (how the upper and lower teeth meet) should be level. If it’s tilted, it can cause an uneven smile. Orthodontic treatment corrects this by adjusting the bite and lip canting (when one side of the lip sits higher than the other).

Orthodontic Diagnosis and Treatment Planning

Once you decide to visit a dental practice, your dentist will create a Canadian dental care plan to determine what treatment you need to fix the issue.

Clinical Evaluation of Symmetry

Assessment involves subjective and objective analyses. Orthodontists employ several tools to evaluate discrepancies:

Classification of Asymmetry

  1. Skeletal: Caused by discrepancies in development and requires orthognathic surgery.
  2. Oral: Resulting from malocclusion or compensatory movements.
  3. Functional: Due to habitual postures or muscle imbalances affecting growth.

Orthodontic Treatments

Braces and Clear Aligners

Braces and clear aligners (such as Invisalign) are the most common methods for correcting asymmetry.

How they help:

  • Shift misaligned teeth into a more balanced position.
  • Correct crossbites, underbites, and overbites that cause unevenness.
  • Improve lip support and smile aesthetics.

Who benefits most?

  • People with minor to moderate asymmetry.
  • Patients with midline deviation without major jaw discrepancies.

Functional Appliances

For children and teenagers, functional appliances can guide jaw growth to prevent asymmetry from worsening.

  • Palatal Expanders: Widen the upper jaw to fix crossbites.
  • Twin Block Appliances: Encourage forward growth for underdeveloped lower jaws.
  • Herbst Appliance: Align the lower jawbone in growing patients.

Best for:

  • Kids and teens whose jaws are still developing.
  • Preventing the need for surgery later in life.

Orthognathic Surgery

A person may need surgery to reposition the upper or lower jaw for severe skeletal asymmetry. Planning uses 3D imaging to simulate post-treatment results and certify optimal alignment. Common procedures include:

  • LeFort I Osteotomy: Moves the upper jaw forward or backward.
  • Bilateral Sagittal Split Osteotomy (BSSO): Adjusts the lower jaw’s position.
  • Genioplasty: Centers or reshapes the chin.

Post-Treatment Considerations and Long-Term Stability

After-care is important to keep your face’s aesthetic appeal longer and better. Oral procedures, like plastic surgery, take time to heal.

Soft Tissue Adaptation

Following orthodontic or surgical correction, soft tissues gradually conform to the new skeletal structure. This adaptation can take several months to a year, with some residual asymmetry possible.

Retention and Stability

  1. Fixed Retainers: Bonded wires placed behind the teeth to prevent relapse.
  2. Removable Retainers: Hawley or clear retainers to maintain arch position.
  3. Muscle Training Exercises: Myofunctional therapy helps stabilize bone positioning.

Long-Term Monitoring

Periodic follow-ups guarantee that changes remain stable. Imaging techniques such as CBCT scans and cephalometric analysis help track structural adaptations over time.

“If I hadn’t had braces, I could very well be quite self-conscious on a day-to-day basis about my teeth and my face, which would be a real shame. It just helps with, l’d say, almost your overall happiness and well-being, even sort of mental well-being,” a participant said in the 2024 qualitative study titled ‘Patients’ perspectives of the long-term impact of orthodontic treatment.’

Achieve Facial Harmony Through Comprehensive Dental Care

High-quality dental care goes beyond straightening teeth. Whether through braces, functional appliances, or surgery, orthodontics can align the jaw for better balance and improve confidence with a more symmetrical appearance.

If you seek comprehensive evenness, consult the best dentists in Maple Ridge for optimal and lasting results. From orthodontic and family dentistry to cosmetic dental services like teeth whitening, our dental clinic offers everything you need to boost your confidence through optimal oral health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my voice or speech change after the treatment?

It’s possible, but temporarily. Suppose your teeth affect the way your tongue interacts with them. In that case, you may notice slight changes in speech.

Just like in cosmetic dentistry, you may experience difficulty in pronouncing certain sounds. This is more common in severe overbites, underbites, or palate expansion cases. Most patients adapt quickly with time, but you can always talk to your dental family clinic if you’re experiencing prolonged struggles.

Can orthodontic treatment fix a crooked nose?

No, but it can create the illusion of a more balanced nose. Since the maxilla and nasal structure are connected, repositioning the maxilla during treatment or surgery may slightly affect nasal symmetry.

Does having one missing tooth cause unevenness on my face?

Yes, over time. A missing tooth can cause bone loss, shifting of surrounding teeth, and changes in bite alignment. This results in one side of the face appearing more sunken or asymmetrical. Dentists help by closing gaps or preparing the space for a dental implant to maintain facial balance. Maximize the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) and schedule regular dental visits to avoid issues with tooth loss.
