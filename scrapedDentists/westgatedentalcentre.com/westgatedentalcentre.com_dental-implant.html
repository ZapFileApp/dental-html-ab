What Is A
Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium post placed in the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. It acts as a stable foundation for a crown, bridge, or denture. Over time, the implant integrates with the bone, helping support function and appearance.

Dental implants are commonly used to restore chewing ability, maintain alignment, and improve overall smile confidence.

Why Patients Consider
Dental Implants The Gold Standard For Missing Teeth

Dental implants are widely considered the gold standard for replacing missing teeth because they are designed to mimic both the root and crown of a natural tooth.

Unlike other options, they can help preserve jawbone structure, prevent nearby teeth from shifting, and restore strong chewing ability. Many patients also find that implants feel more stable and natural over time, making them a reliable, long-term solution for both function and appearance.

Why Replacing Missing
Teeth Matters More Than You Think

Leaving a missing tooth untreated can affect more than just your smile. Over time, it may lead to bone loss, shifting of nearby teeth, and changes in how you chew or speak. Many patients exploring options choose this treatment because implants can help preserve jawbone structure, maintain alignment, and support facial appearance.

Compared to other solutions, dental implants may offer a more stable, long-term way to restore both function and confidence.

Are You a Candidate
for Dental Implants?

Patients with one or more missing teeth who are looking for a stable, long-term solution may be good candidates. Ideal candidates typically have healthy gums and sufficient bone to support the implant. However, certain conditions, such as active gum disease or significant bone loss, may delay treatment.

Using CBCT imaging, we assess bone structure, density, and overall oral health to determine suitability. If needed, procedures like bone grafting or gum treatment can help prepare for dental implants and improve long-term success.

Dental Implant Process & Timeline

Most dental implant treatments take over several months, depending on your oral health, healing, and whether bone or gum preparation is needed. While timelines vary, many implant cases take approximately 3 to 9 months from consultation to final restoration.

Step 1: Consultation

We begin by discussing your goals, missing teeth, medical history, and treatment options. This helps us understand whether implants, bridges, dentures, or implant overdentures may be right for you.

Step 2: Imaging & Treatment Planning

CBCT imaging & digital scans help assess your bone structure, gum health, and implant position. If bone loss or gum disease is present, we may recommend treatment before moving forward.

Step 3: Implant Placement

The implant is placed into the jawbone using careful digital planning. Local freezing is used for comfort, and oral sedation may be discussed for anxious patients when appropriate.

Step 4: Healing Period

Over the next few months, the implant gradually integrates with the bone. This healing phase is important for creating a stable foundation for your new tooth or denture.

Step 5: Final Restoration

Once healing is complete, we attach your custom crown, bridge, or denture. This restores chewing function, appearance, and smile support.

Step 6: Review & Maintenance

Follow-up visits allow us to check healing, comfort, bite, implant stability, and gum health. Ongoing hygiene and regular dental visits help protect your results long term.

Our Comprehensive Techniques For Dental Implants

Single Tooth Dental Implants

A single implant topped with a custom crown is used to replace one missing tooth. This option restores function and appearance without impacting surrounding teeth, making it a common choice for patients considering dental implants in Maple Ridge for long-term stability.

Implant-Supported Bridges

For multiple missing teeth, implant- supported bridges anchor to implants instead of natural teeth. This provides improved support and may help maintain jawbone health. Many patients exploring dental implants near Maple Ridge choose this option for a more secure alternative to traditional bridges.

Implant Overdentures (Full Arch Solutions)

Implant overdentures are a primary focus at our dental implant clinic, offering a secure, removable solution for patients missing most or all teeth. Often considered as an option for full mouth dental implants, they improve retention, comfort, and daily function compared to conventional dentures.

Bone & Gum Preparation for Implants

In cases where additional support is needed, procedures like bone grafting or gum grafting may be recommended. These treatments help create a stable foundation for dental implant solutions and can make implants possible for patients who were previously not ideal candidates.

Types of Dental Implants

Endosteal

These are the most common type that feature a cylindrical or tapered shape, which helps achieve skeletal stability in surgical procedures. They are often made from threaded titanium alloys.

  • Surgically placed into the jawbone, where they substitute for the root.
  • After placement, it undergoes a healing period known as osseointegration, during which it integrates with the surrounding area. This process lasts between 3 to 6 months.
  • Suitable for our patients with sufficient osseous tissue density and volume. They’re used in single-tooth replacements and full-arch restorations for new teeth.

Subperiosteal

Ideal for patients with insufficient bone height or volume and are not candidates for traditional replacements.

  • Uses a metal framework that rests on or above the jawbone but beneath the periosteum.
  • Unlike endosteal prostheses, they don’t integrate with the skeletal framework but are anchored by the surrounding soft tissue during a dental implant procedure.
  • Used in cases where grafting is not feasible, or the patient prefers to avoid augmentation.

Hybrid

These may incorporate elements from endosteal, subperiosteal, or zygomatic types for stability and function. For example, a hybrid might include features to enhance soft tissue integration.

Material Considerations

  1. Titanium remains the exemplar due to its biocompatibility, strength, and osseointegration properties. Titanium-aluminum-vanadium (Ti-6Al-4V) is commonly used for enhanced mechanical properties.
  2. Zirconia is a ceramic alternative used for its aesthetics and biocompatibility. They are best for our patients with metal allergies or those seeking an authentic restoration.

Zygomatic

This specialized type is used for our patients with severe maxillary bone loss, where conventional methods cannot be placed due to insufficient volume. These are longer than traditional ones and are anchored into the zygomatic area rather than the maxillary—made from titanium and stabilize areas with insufficient maxillary.

For Post-Operative Care and Maintenance

Most dental implant treatments take over several months, depending on your oral health, healing, and whether bone or gum preparation is needed. While timelines vary, many implant cases take approximately 3 to 9 months from consultation to final restoration.

Immediate Care

You may experience discomfort following the procedure. We may prescribe analgesics, usually nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen, to manage pain and reduce inflammation.

  • Swelling is a common post-operative reaction managed with ice packs applied to the external area of the face near the site. The application should be done intermittently for 15-20 minutes at a time during the first 24 hours.
  • Keep the site clean and avoid touching or disturbing the area. Gentle rinsing with an antimicrobial mouthwash or a saline solution can help maintain oral hygiene.

Oral Hygiene

Oral hygiene is important for preventing plaque accumulation and reducing the risk of peri-implant disease. We advise our patients to use soft-bristle or electric toothbrushes.

  • Traditional flossing may be challenging due to the presence of abutments. We advise using floss threaders or interdental brushes designed for its care.
  • Antimicrobial or antibacterial mouth rinses, such as chlorhexidine gluconate (0.12% solution), can help reduce the risk of infection. Follow the recommended usage guidelines twice daily for a period specified by our dentist.

Dietary Considerations

Soft Diet

We recommend tender foods that do not require biting into, such as yogurt, mashed potatoes, and soups.

Avoiding Certain Foods

You should avoid sticky, hard, or crunchy foods that could disrupt the healing process or cause damage to the site. Also, hot and spicy foods should be avoided to prevent irritation.

Follow-Up Appointments

If sutures are placed, they are removed within 7-10 days after the placement. Our dentist schedules a follow-up appointment to assess the healing process and remove any sutures if necessary. During these visits, we check for signs of osseointegration, assess the health of the gums, and ensure that it is functioning as intended.

Frequently Asked Questions on Dental Implants

Am I a good candidate for dental implants?

Most healthy adults with one or more missing teeth are good candidates. Ideal candidates have healthy gums, sufficient jawbone, and good overall health. Even if you’ve been told otherwise before, modern techniques can often make implants possible.

What could prevent me from getting dental implants?

Conditions like active gum disease, severe bone loss, uncontrolled diabetes, or heavy smoking can delay or complicate treatment. These don’t always disqualify you; many issues can be treated first to improve your eligibility for implants.

What does a CBCT scan check for before implant treatment?

A CBCT scan provides a 3D view of your jaw, allowing us to assess bone density, volume, and anatomy. It also helps identify nerves, sinuses, and optimal implant placement for safer, more precise, and predictable results.

Why should I replace a missing tooth instead of leaving the gap?

Leaving a gap can lead to bone loss, shifting teeth, bite problems, and changes in facial structure. Replacing the tooth with an implant helps maintain jawbone health, restores function, and preserves your natural smile long-term.

Are dental implants better than bridges or dentures?

Dental implants are often considered one of the most stable and long-lasting options. Unlike bridges or dentures, they preserve bone, don’t rely on neighboring teeth, and feel more natural. However, the best option depends on your needs, budget, and oral health.

Does getting a dental implant hurt?

The procedure is typically done under local anesthesia, so you won’t feel pain during treatment. Most patients report mild soreness afterward, similar to a tooth extraction, which can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication.

How long does the dental implant process take?

The full process usually takes 3 to 9 months, depending on healing time. It includes consultation, implant placement, healing (osseointegration), and final crown placement. Some cases may take longer if additional procedures like bone grafting are needed.
