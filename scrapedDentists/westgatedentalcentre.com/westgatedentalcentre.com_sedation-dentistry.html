Serving Maple Ridge and Surrounding Areas 

Dental anxiety, a prevalent condition affecting a portion of the population, often impedes oral healthcare. Recent studies reveal that up to 40% of adults have dentophobia, with approximately 12% avoiding visits due to fear.

Studies performed by the Canadian Dental Association indicate that sedation dentistry can reduce patient anxiety and improve satisfaction, with over 70% of patients reporting a positive experience when anesthesia is used. Westgate Dental & Implant Centre addresses this challenge head-on with advanced sedation dentistry in Maple Ridge.

What is Sedation Dentistry?

It is a clinical practice designed to manage anxiety, discomfort, and pain associated with oral procedures through the use of pharmacological agents. This is beneficial for our patients who experience:

  1. Dentophobia
  2. Have a low pain threshold
  3. Require extensive or invasive treatments

Our practice aims to create a more comfortable and manageable experience, enhancing patient satisfaction and compliance.

Sedation Dentistry Techniques

Nitrous Oxide (Laughing Gas)

Nitrous Oxide is a colourless, non-flammable gas administered via a nasal mask to help patients relax. This alleviates anxiety and discomfort while fully awake.

  • The gas is absorbed rapidly through the bloodstream and exhaled quickly for swift onset and recovery.
  • This is ideal for minor procedures, such as cleanings and simple extractions, where moderate sedation is sufficient.

Oral Conscious Sedation

Conscious sedation in dentistry involves the ingestion of a sedative medication taken one hour before the procedure.

  • Like nitrous oxide, you will remain conscious but relaxed.
  • Suitable for our patients with moderate anxiety or for procedures that require more extensive intervention.

Is Sedation Dentistry Right For You?

With Anxiety or Phobia

These patients avoid oral care, leading to deteriorating oral health. This treatment helps mitigate anxiety by inducing a state of relaxation, making it possible for patients to undergo treatments without fear or stress.

Key Indicators:

  • History of cancelled or avoided appointments due to anxiety.
  • Physical symptoms of anxiety like sweating, rapid heartbeat, or nausea at the thought of visits.
  • Previous negative experiences contributing to ongoing fear.

Patients with a low pain threshold

These patients may find even routine procedures unbearable. Sedatives can reduce pain perception to receive the care they need comfortably.

Key Indicators:

  • Self-reported low tolerance to pain.
  • Difficulty coping with the discomfort
  • Requests for high doses of local anesthesia during previous treatments.

Requiring Extensive or Complex Procedures

Beneficial for our patients who need extensive or invasive dental work, such as:

  • Multiple extractions
  • Root canals
  • Implant placements

Ensures patient comfort and enables our dentist to perform procedures more efficiently.

With Strong Gag Reflexes

A strong gag reflex makes operations difficult and uncomfortable for both the patient and the dentist. Suppressing the gag reflex facilitates smoother and more efficient treatment.

Key Indicators:

  • History of gagging during oral impressions.
  • Difficulty tolerating instruments in the mouth.
  • Need for frequent breaks during treatment due to gagging.

With Medical Conditions

Certain medical conditions make it difficult for patients to remain still during dental procedures, such as:

  1. Epilepsy
  2. Parkinson’s disease
  3. Severe arthritis

Sedatives help patients by minimizing involuntary movements and ensuring a safe and comfortable experience.

How to Prepare

Pre-Procedure Consultation

  1. We first gather a medical history to assess your condition, the dental services required, and whether concerns such as breathing pauses during sleep should be reviewed before treatment.
  2. Our dentist conducts an examination to determine the appropriate type and level of anesthesia.
  3. Then, our dentists in Maple Ridge explain the type to be used, the expected effects, the date of the dental appointment, and any precautions before you sign the consent form.

What are the Preparation Requirements?

Pre-Consultation

  1. We first gather a medical history to assess your condition and the dental services required.
  2. Our dentist conducts an examination to determine the appropriate type and level of anesthesia.
  3. Then, our dentists in Maple Ridge explain the type to be used, the expected effects, the date of the dental appointment, and any precautions before you sign the consent form.

 

Nitrous Oxide (Laughing Gas)

  • No specific fasting requirements; you can eat and drink normally.
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing to ensure ease during the operation.
  • Avoid heavy meals before the appointment.

Oral Sedation

  • Avoid eating or drinking for at least six hours before the appointment.
  • Our dentist administers a sedative medication to be taken an hour before the procedure.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and avoid wearing contact lenses if possible, as it can cause dry eyes.

Intravenous (IV)

  • Fast for at least eight hours before the procedure to prevent aspiration.
  • Report any changes in health, such as colds, flu, or infections, to our experienced dentists before the appointment.
  • Provide a list of all medications and supplements to our dentist. Some medications may need to be adjusted or paused before the procedure.
  • Wear loose-fitting, short-sleeved clothing to facilitate the placement of the IV line while in a dental chair. Avoid wearing makeup, nail polish, and jewelry.

General Anesthesia

  • Do not eat or drink for at least eight hours before the procedure.
  • Notify our dentist of any recent illnesses or changes in health.
  • Follow instructions given by our anesthesiologist, such as taking or discontinuing certain medications.
  • A responsible adult must accompany the patient pre- and post-appointment and stay with them for 24 hours post-dental procedure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I experience side effects after sedation dentistry?

Common reactions include drowsiness, dry mouth, and mild nausea, which resolve within a few hours. If you experience any severe or prolonged side effects, such as difficulty breathing, chest pain, or persistent dizziness, it is important to contact us immediately.

Our team is available to provide guidance and support, ensuring that any post-treatment concerns are addressed promptly and effectively.

How does sedation dentistry interact with chronic conditions like diabetes or hypertension?

Individuals with diabetes or hypertension can still undergo the procedure, but it requires careful management. We coordinate with the patient’s primary care physician or specialist to understand their medical condition and adjust the plan accordingly.

For instance, blood glucose levels and blood pressure are monitored before, during, and after the procedure to ensure stability and safety.

Are there specific post-procedure restrictions?

Yes, post-procedure restrictions depend on the type used:

  • Nitrous Oxide: Minimal restrictions; they can resume normal activities immediately.
  • Oral and IV: They should avoid driving, operating machinery, and making important decisions for at least 24 hours. It’s also recommended to rest and avoid alcohol and caffeine.
  • General Anesthesia: A full day of rest is advised, with restrictions on physical activities and requiring assistance with daily tasks.
