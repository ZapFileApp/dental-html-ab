            &lt;div&gt;&lt;p&gt;Root canal therapy is a common and well-known dental treatment used to save teeth that are infected or damaged. In a nutshell, root canal treatment involves the removal of the soft, vulnerable tissue inside the tooth to treat and prevent infection.&lt;/p&gt;
 &lt;img class="img-fluid mb-2" alt="Endo&amp;quot;" src="https://www.12thavenuedental.com/files/images/mainimages/main-endo.jpg"&gt;
&lt;h2 class="mt-2 h2alt"&gt;How does root canal treatment work?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The soft tissue inside your tooth, called the pulp, is a bundle of blood vessels and nerves. If the tooth is damaged and bacteria get to the pulp an infection can result. Left untreated, this infection can spread to the gums and bone tissue above the tooth and cause a dangerous infection called an abscess. This can result in the loss of the tooth and damage to the surrounding tissues.&lt;/p&gt;
 
&lt;p&gt;When a tooth is damaged, we drill a hole in it and remove the pulp. Then we clean the inside of the tooth and seal it to prevent bacteria from entering, and we restore the tooth with a crown.&lt;/p&gt;
 
&lt;h2 class="mt-2 h2alt"&gt;Aren’t root canals painful?&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;While root canal therapy has a reputation for being painful, with modern techniques the procedure is usually no more uncomfortable than having a tooth filled. The treatment is done under a local anesthetic and we provide sedation dentistry for anxious patients.&lt;/p&gt;
 
&lt;p&gt;If you’re suffering from a severe toothache, your tooth may have become infected. Call our office to book an examination.&lt;/p&gt;

            &lt;div&gt;&lt;p&gt;Dental restoration involves repairing and restoring the function and appearance of damaged teeth. Restorations are used in cases of decay or injury and are intended to prevent further damage to the tooth. Most restorations are made from tooth-coloured materials to match the natural shade of your other teeth.&lt;/p&gt;

	&lt;h2 class="mt-3 mb-1 h2alt"&gt;Fillings&lt;/h2&gt;
	&lt;p&gt;We use fillings to repair cavities caused by decay, and to restore teeth after root canal treatment. Fillings can be made from amalgam or tooth-coloured composite to suit your needs.&lt;/p&gt;
	
	&lt;h2 class="mt-3 mb-1 h2alt"&gt;Crowns&lt;/h2&gt;
	&lt;p&gt;Crowns, or caps, are used to completely replace a damaged tooth. Crowns have traditionally been made from metal, usually gold, but are now usually made from ceramic or porcelain. They are designed and shaded to match the rest of your other teeth.&lt;/p&gt;
	
	&lt;h2 class="mt-3 mb-1 h2alt"&gt;Inlays &amp;amp; Onlays&lt;/h2&gt;
	&lt;p&gt;In cases where a tooth can’t be repaired with a filling, but it doesn’t require a crown, we may use a restoration called an inlay. These restorations are created in a dental laboratory and cemented in place in the damaged area. Once they are in place they strengthen the structure of the tooth. Onlays provide additional strength to restore the biting areas of the tooth being repaired.&lt;/p&gt;
	
	&lt;h2 class="mt-3 mb-1 h2alt"&gt;Bridges&lt;/h2&gt;
	&lt;p&gt;Bridges are the traditional replacement for one or more lost teeth in a row. The bridge is made of the replacement tooth or teeth, and a way of attaching the bridge to the natural teeth on either side of the gap. Most bridges are held in place by dental crowns placed over the adjacent teeth.&lt;/p&gt;
