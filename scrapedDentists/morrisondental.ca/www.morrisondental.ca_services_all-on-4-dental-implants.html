All-on-4 Dental Implants in Fort McMurray, AB

Get Your Smile Back with All-on-4 Dental Implants Near You

Missing most of your teeth? Dealing with dentures that won’t stay put? All-on-4 dental implants could be your answer. Our dentist in Fort McMurray has helped dozens of patients trade their frustrating dentures for permanent teeth that actually work.
 
Here’s the best part – you only need four implants to replace your whole arch of teeth. And you walk out on surgery day with teeth you can use right away. No waiting months with an empty mouth.

What Exactly Are All-on-4 Dental Implants?

Think of it this way: instead of replacing every single tooth with its own implant (which would be crazy expensive and involve a ton of surgery), all-on-four dental implants use just four strategically placed posts to hold an entire set of teeth.
 
The secret is in how they’re positioned. The two back implants go in at an angle, which lets them grab onto more bone. That angled placement is genius because it means most people don’t need bone grafting, even if they’ve been missing teeth for years.
 
Here’s What Happens:
We place four titanium posts in your jawbone – two straight up front, two angled in the back. These posts become the anchors for a bridge that holds 10-14 teeth. Over a few months, the posts fuse with your bone, and boom – you’ve got permanent teeth that work like the real thing.

Why People Love All-on-4 Dental Implants in Fort McMurray

You Get Teeth the Same Day

With traditional implants, you might wait months before getting teeth. With all-on-four dental implants in Fort McMurray, we attach a temporary set right after surgery. You’re not walking around toothless. Once everything heals in 3-6 months, we swap them for your permanent teeth.

Just Four Implants Does the Job

Regular full-mouth implants? You’re looking at 6-8 implants per arch, sometimes more. All-on-4 Dental Implants deliver the same result with just four implants. Less surgery, faster recovery, and it costs way less.

Skip the Bone Grafting

That angled placement I mentioned? It works with the bone you’ve already got. Most people don’t need grafting procedures, which would add months to the process and thousands to the bill.

Your Jawbone Stays Healthy

Lose your teeth, and your jawbone starts shrinking. It’s just what happens. But implants trick your body into thinking you still have tooth roots, so the bone stays in place. That keeps your face from getting that sunken look.

They’re Not Going Anywhere

Dentures slip when you eat. They move when you talk. They fall out at the worst possible times. All-on-4 implants are screwed into your jaw. They’re as permanent as it gets.

Take Care of Them Like Normal Teeth

Brush, floss, and see us twice a year. That’s it. No soaking them overnight, no messy adhesives, no special cleaning tablets.

Are You a Good Fit for All-on-4 Dental Implants Near You?

This treatment works really well if you:

  • Lost most or all your teeth on the top, bottom, or both.
  • Hate your current dentures.
  • Want something permanent that doesn’t come out.
  • Have decent bone (though you need less than you’d think).
  • They are generally healthy.
  • Don’t smoke (or you’re willing to quit – smoking screws up healing).
Honestly, the only way to know for sure is to come in. Dr. Tan will take a look, do some X-rays, and tell you straight up whether you’re a candidate for All-on-4 dental implants near you.

What the Treatment Actually Looks Like

First Visit: Planning Everything 

We’ll do a full exam with 3D scans to assess your bone condition. Dr. Tan explains the whole process, what it’ll cost, and answers whatever questions you’ve got. No pressure – just information so you can decide.

Surgery Day

We start with any necessary extractions, then place the four implants while you are sedated. A temporary bridge is attached the same day, so you leave with a full set of teeth and can eat soft foods during healing.

Healing Time

Over the next few months, the implants will fuse with your bone. You’ll keep wearing the temporary bridge and stick to softer foods. We’ll see you a few times to make sure everything’s healing right.

Getting Your Permanent Teeth

After 3-6 months, you’re ready for the real deal. We remove the temporary bridge and put in your permanent one. It’s custom-made to look natural and handle everything you throw at it. These are built to last.

Taking Care of Your New Teeth

It’s not complicated:
  • Brush twice a day. Use a soft brush and pay attention to where the bridge meets your gums.
  • Floss every day. You’ll need special flossers or a water flosser to get under the bridge, but it’s not hard once you get the hang of it.
  • Come see us regularly. We need to check those implants and have everything professionally cleaned.
  • Watch out for super hard stuff in the first few months. Don’t go chomping on ice or hard candy right away.
  • Seriously, don’t smoke. Smoking is one of the biggest reasons implants fail.
Do all that, and your all-on-4 dental implants could easily last 20 years or more. A lot of our patients forget they even have implants because they feel so natural.

Questions People Actually Ask

How long do these things last?

With decent care, 20+ years easy. The implants themselves can last your whole life. The bridge might need to be replaced eventually, but the foundation stays solid.

Does it hurt?

You’re sedated during surgery, so no. Afterward, yeah, you’ll be sore for a few days. Most people handle it fine with regular pain meds. Nothing crazy.

Can I eat normal food?

Once you’re healed, absolutely. Steak, apples, popcorn, whatever. During healing, stick to softer stuff so you don’t mess up the implants.

How long am I in the chair?

Surgery takes 2-3 hours per arch. You’ll need someone to drive you home because of the sedation.

What if my bone isn't great?

The angled implants usually work even with less bone. Only in really severe cases would we need to do grafting first, and that’s rare.

Will insurance cover this?

Depends on your plan. Some cover part of it. We’ll help you determine what your insurance will cover and file everything for you.

Questions People Ask Us All the Time

How much is a full set of dental implants in Alberta?

In Alberta, a full mouth set of dental implants typically spans between $15,000 and $30,000 per arch depending on the complexity of the case. The current average cost for a single implant in the province is approximately $3,200, which includes the post, abutment, and crown.

What do All-on-4 implants cost?

The investment for All-on-4 dental implants in Alberta generally ranges from $20,000 to $30,000 per arch. This price typically covers the surgical placement of four titanium posts, the custom-made prosthetic bridge, and all necessary initial consultations and digital scans.

What is the downside of All-on-4 dental implants?

Potential disadvantages include a high upfront cost compared to dentures and a fixed design that cannot be removed at home for cleaning. Some patients may also experience complications such as speech adjustments, gum irritation, or the rare risk of implant failure if the bone does not fuse properly.

Who is not a candidate for All-on-4 dental implants?

Individuals with uncontrolled diabetes, active gum disease, or severe bone loss that cannot support the posts are generally not suitable candidates. Additionally, heavy smokers and those with certain autoimmune disorders may face a significantly higher risk of infection and implant failure.

What are the current average costs for full-mouth restorations in Alberta?

The current average price for an All-on-4 full-arch restoration in Alberta sits at approximately $25,000. If additional support is needed, an All-on-6 procedure is available, though it usually costs more due to the extra implants and increased surgical complexity.
