Overview

Understanding Halitosis Treatment

Chronic bad breath, or halitosis, can be embarrassing and may indicate underlying dental or health issues. At Dawson Landing Dental in Chestermere, we diagnose the root cause of your bad breath and provide targeted treatments to eliminate it. From professional cleanings to addressing gum disease or dry mouth, we help you enjoy fresh breath and renewed confidence.

Why It Matters

The benefits of Halitosis Treatment

What Causes Chronic Bad Breath?

While occasional bad breath is normal, chronic halitosis that persists despite brushing and mouthwash typically has an underlying cause that needs to be addressed. The most common dental causes include poor oral hygiene, gum disease, cavities, dry mouth (xerostomia), tongue bacteria buildup, and poorly fitting dental appliances. In some cases, halitosis may be linked to sinus infections, acid reflux, respiratory conditions, or certain systemic diseases. Our team at Dawson Landing Dental performs a thorough evaluation to determine the specific cause of your bad breath so we can treat it effectively.

Our Diagnostic Approach

When you visit Dawson Landing Dental for halitosis concerns, we begin with a comprehensive oral examination to look for signs of gum disease, cavities, dry mouth, and bacterial buildup on the tongue. We review your medical history, medications, and dietary habits to identify potential contributing factors. If the cause of your bad breath is dental in nature, we develop a treatment plan to address it directly. If we suspect a non-dental cause, we will refer you to the appropriate medical specialist for further evaluation.

Treatment Options for Halitosis

Treatment for halitosis depends on the underlying cause. Professional dental cleaning removes plaque and tartar that harbor odor-causing bacteria. If gum disease is present, scaling and root planing or other periodontal treatments may be necessary. For dry mouth, we may recommend saliva-stimulating products, hydration strategies, or medication adjustments. We also provide guidance on effective tongue cleaning techniques, proper brushing and flossing habits, and lifestyle changes that can make a significant difference. Our goal is to eliminate the source of bad breath — not just mask it with mints or mouthwash.
