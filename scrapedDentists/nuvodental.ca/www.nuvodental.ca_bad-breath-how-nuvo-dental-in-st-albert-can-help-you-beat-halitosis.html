Bad Breath? How Nuvo Dental in St. Albert Can Help You Beat Halitosis

  • Nuvo Dental
  • August 27, 2025

Bad breath is something we have all experienced at one point or another. Whether it is after a garlic-heavy dinner or first thing in the morning, most cases are temporary and easily fixed. But if bad breath lingers day after day, it can become more than just a personal inconvenience—it may be a sign of an underlying dental or health issue.

At NUVO Dental in St. Albert, we know how frustrating and even embarrassing persistent bad breath can be. The good news is that in many cases, halitosis (the medical term for chronic bad breath) can be treated effectively, especially when you partner with your dental team for regular care.

Why Bad Breath Happens

Bad breath is most often caused by bacteria in the mouth. These bacteria break down food particles and release sulphur compounds that create an unpleasant odour. While this is the most common cause, other factors can contribute:

Poor Oral Hygiene

When plaque and food debris are not removed with regular brushing and flossing, bacteria multiply, leading to bad breath.

Gum Disease

Periodontal infections create deep pockets where bacteria thrive, often producing a persistent odour that cannot be masked by mints or mouthwash.

Dry Mouth

Saliva helps cleanse the mouth and neutralize odour-causing bacteria. If your mouth is too dry, bad breath can develop quickly.

Diet and Lifestyle

Foods like onions, garlic, and strong spices, as well as tobacco products, can cause lingering odours.

Dental Issues

Cavities, cracked fillings, or poorly fitted dental work can trap food and bacteria, creating localized odour problems.

Why Regular Dental Visits Are Key

While mouthwash and mints may offer temporary relief, they do not address the root cause of halitosis. A professional dental visit gives us the opportunity to identify and treat the source.

Professional Cleaning

Our hygienists remove plaque and tartar buildup that daily brushing and flossing can miss. This helps reduce the bacteria responsible for unpleasant odours.

Gum Health Assessment

Gum disease is a major cause of persistent bad breath. At Nuvo Dental, we check for early signs and offer treatment to stop progression and restore gum health.

Treatment of Dental Problems

If cavities, damaged fillings, or other dental issues are contributing to bad breath, we can address them promptly, eliminating hidden sources of bacteria.

Personalized Advice

We can recommend the best home care routine for your needs, including proper brushing techniques, tongue cleaning, and products like antimicrobial rinses.

Early Detection of Other Conditions

Sometimes chronic bad breath is linked to non-dental health concerns such as sinus infections, acid reflux, or even diabetes. Your dentist can help identify these and refer you for further care if needed.

Keeping Your Breath Fresh Between Visits

While regular dental care is essential, there are habits you can follow at home to help keep bad breath under control:

  • Brush your teeth twice a day and floss daily
  • Clean your tongue regularly to remove bacteria and debris
  • Stay hydrated to promote saliva production
  • Limit foods and drinks known to cause odours
  • Avoid smoking and tobacco products
  • Replace your toothbrush every 3 to 4 months or sooner if worn

How Nuvo Dental in St. Albert Can Help

Our team at Nuvo Dental is committed to helping patients achieve healthy, confident smiles—and that includes ensuring fresh breath. We understand that halitosis can be a sensitive subject, so we provide a friendly, judgement-free environment where you can discuss your concerns openly.

We offer:

  • Thorough check-ups and gentle cleanings
  • Gum disease prevention and treatment
  • Stay hydrated to promote saliva production
  • Cavity detection and restorative treatments
  • Tailored oral hygiene recommendations
  • Modern technology for accurate diagnosis

Whether your bad breath is a new issue or something you have been dealing with for years, we can help you find the cause and create a plan to address it.

Book Your Appointment Today

If you are struggling with persistent bad breath, do not let it affect your confidence or quality of life. With the right dental care, halitosis can often be eliminated or significantly reduced.
