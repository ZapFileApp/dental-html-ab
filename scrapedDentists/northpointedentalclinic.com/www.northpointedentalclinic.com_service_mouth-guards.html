Are you an avid sports player? If you are, then it is important to keep your beautiful smile from suffering some serious damage on the field or court. Mouthguards are a device that athletes can wear to prevent teeth from damage. That being said, it is important to note that not all mouthguards are equal in terms of comfort, protection, and fit. Bite-and-Boil and Stock Mouthguards

There are many mouthguards available; however, as mentioned above, not all mouthguards provide the same level of comfort and protection. Mouthguards can be found at almost any drugstore. There are two kinds of over-the-counter mouthguards in Country Hills: bite-and-boil and stock mouthguards.

Stock mouthguards are built to fit all mouths and are not tailored to individual differences, and therefore often end up being too loose or too bulky to properly protect the teeth and allow the athlete to breathe with ease. Boil-and-bite mouthguards work by having the wearer heat the guard in hot water and bite into it to create an impression of their teeth and a proper fit. While these may fit better than stock mouthguards, they still aren’t ideal when it comes to full protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of mouthguards are there?

Custom-fit mouthguards are designed for your teeth for maximum comfort. Boil-and-bite mouthguards are easy to mould at home and are adjustable. Stock mouthguards are preformed and ready to use, but have the least comfortable fit.

How long do custom-fit mouthguards last?

With proper use and care, custom-fit mouthguards can last several years. Routine cleaning and storing in a dry container will help keep the mouthguard from losing its shape. If your mouthguard feels loose or shows signs of wear, it’s time to get it fixed or replaced.

Are mouthguards just for athletes?

No, mouthguards can also be used for individuals who grind their teeth while they sleep. Mouthguards promote protection against tooth wear, chipping, and other damage. If you are not sure if a mouthguard is right for you, ask your dentist.

Can a mouthguard be beneficial for sleep apnea?

A certain type of mouthguard keeps the airway open by moving the jaw. Mouthguards can be beneficial for mild to moderate sleep apnea. Discussing your desired mouthguard with a healthcare professional may help you select a device for your condition.

How do I care for my mouthguard?

You should rinse your mouthguard in cold water and brush it with a mild soap every day. Do not use hot water, which may change the shape of the material. When not in use, your mouthguard should be stored in a clean, dry container.
