What is the Procedure for Porcelain Veneers?

During a consultation with your dentist, they will perform a thorough analysis of your teeth to determine if you qualify for porcelain veneers in Chestermere, AB, and if they are the best-suited solution for you. To begin the procedure, your dentist will take impressions of your teeth. These impressions are sent to a dental laboratory and used to design a stone cast of your teeth in order to craft the actual custom veneers. During this stage, you and your dentist will work together to select the right shade, shape, size, and length for your veneers. You will have the final say as far as what your veneers look like and your dentist will ensure that your porcelain veneers meet your smile goals. After your porcelain veneers are ready, it’s time for the installation process. Before removing a tiny portion of the enamel from the surface of your teeth, they will apply a local anesthetic for your comfort. Dentist in Chesteremere will place the veneers on your teeth temporarily, giving you an idea of the result. If you approve, the porcelain veneers are cemented into place.

Are Porcelain Veneers Right for You?

Porcelain veneers near you are an excellent option for patients looking to improve the appearance of their smile with long-lasting, natural-looking results. They are ideal for correcting issues such as stained, chipped, uneven, or slightly misaligned teeth. Since a thin layer of enamel is removed, veneers are a permanent solution and require good oral health before treatment.

Your dentist will evaluate your teeth, gums, and overall oral condition to determine if porcelain veneers are the right choice for your smile goals. A personalized consultation ensures that your treatment plan delivers both aesthetic and functional benefits.

Caring for Your Porcelain Veneers

Maintaining your porcelain veneers in Chestermere is simple and similar to caring for your natural teeth. Regular brushing, flossing, and routine dental checkups are essential to keep your veneers looking their best. Although porcelain is stain-resistant and durable, avoiding habits like biting hard objects or grinding your teeth can help extend their lifespan.

With proper care, porcelain veneers can last for many years while preserving their strength and appearance. Your dental team will provide specific aftercare instructions to help you protect your investment and enjoy a confident smile.

Although no dental restoration will last a lifetime, porcelain veneers are long-lasting when they are properly cared for and routine checkups with your dentist are maintained. Porcelain veneers are expected to last for an average of 5 to 10 years, however, they may last longer.

FAQ’s

How long do porcelain veneers last?

Porcelain veneers will last 10 to 15 years or more, provided you take good care of them. Made of durable materials designed to endure, brushing, flossing, and regular dental appointments keep them in excellent condition. East Chestermere Dental creates veneers that are breathtaking for years.

Can porcelain veneers fix crooked or chipped teeth?

Yes, porcelain veneers will repair the appearance of chipped or crooked teeth. Porcelain veneers are bonded to the front of your teeth to give you an even, smooth, and natural smile. Veneers are an excellent solution for reshaping and enhancing the appearance of your teeth.

Are porcelain veneers a permanent solution?

Porcelain veneers are a permanent treatment, but they might have to be replaced after a couple of decades. It requires a minimal amount of enamel loss to be fitted, and therefore, it is not reversible. Veneers, if cared for well, can last an incredibly long time. We make sure your veneers are comfortable and natural-looking at East Chestermere Dental.

Do porcelain veneers require special care?

Porcelain veneers can be cared for with no more than regular brushing and flossing. You must avoid biting hard foods or using your teeth as a tool. Regularity in attending dental visits maintains veneers. East Chestermere Dental will advise you on the best ways to take care of them.
