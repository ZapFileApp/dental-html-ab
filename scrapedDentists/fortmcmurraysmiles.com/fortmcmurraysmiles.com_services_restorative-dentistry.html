What is Restorative Dentistry?

Restorative dentistry is an umbrella term for any dental procedure that focuses on repairing or replacing damaged or missing teeth. The primary goals are to restore oral health, regain proper chewing and speaking function, relieve pain, and prevent future complications. A restorative dentistry treatment plan is always personalized, taking into account your specific condition, overall health, and personal goals.

The field seamlessly blends with cosmetic dentistry; while the primary focus is on health and function, we always aim for results that are aesthetically pleasing and natural-looking.

Am I a Good Candidate for Restorative Dentistry?

If you are dealing with damaged, decayed, or missing teeth, restorative dentistry can help you reclaim your smile's health, function, and appearance. You are likely an excellent candidate for restorative care if you experience:

  • A persistent toothache or tooth sensitivity.
  • A visible cavity, crack, or chip in a tooth.
  • A tooth that is fractured or weakened.
  • One or more missing teeth.
  • Difficulty chewing or speaking due to dental issues.
  • Discomfort with your current dentures or bridges.
  • The desire to replace metal (amalgam) fillings with tooth-colored alternatives.

From a single filling to a full-mouth reconstruction, we provide personalized solutions. The best way to know your options is through a comprehensive examination.

Our Restorative Dentistry Treatments

Dental implants are widely considered the gold standard for tooth replacement. An implant is a small, biocompatible titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. Once it integrates with the bone, it provides an unmatched foundation for a crown, bridge, or denture. Implants are a permanent solution that preserves jawbone health and function just like a natural tooth.

Dental Crowns

When a tooth is too damaged or weakened to be restored with a filling, due to extensive decay, a large crack, or after a root canal treatment, a dental crown is the ideal solution. Think of a crown as a protective "cap" that is custom-crafted to encase the entire visible portion of the tooth. This restores its original shape, strength, and function. We offer high-quality porcelain and zirconia crowns that are incredibly strong and designed to match the shade and translucency of your surrounding teeth.

Dental Bridges

If you have one or more missing teeth in a row, a dental bridge can "bridge" the gap. A traditional fixed bridge consists of an artificial tooth (pontic) anchored by dental crowns placed on the healthy teeth adjacent to the space. This is a fixed, non-removable solution that effectively restores your smile and bite. For a more permanent solution that doesn't involve altering adjacent teeth, we also provide implant-supported bridges.

Dentures

For patients missing multiple or all of their teeth, dentures offer a reliable way to regain a full smile. We provide:

  • Partial Dentures: Removable appliances that replace several missing teeth, clasping onto remaining natural teeth for stability.
  • Complete Dentures: A full set of teeth that rest on the gums to replace all teeth in an arch.

For superior comfort and stability, we highly recommend implant-supported dentures. These snap securely onto strategically placed dental implants, eliminating the slipping and discomfort associated with traditional dentures.

Benefits of Choosing Restorative Dentistry

Investing in restorative dental care offers profound benefits that extend far beyond your mouth:

  • Restored Chewing and Speaking Function: Damaged or missing teeth can make eating difficult and affect your speech. Restorative treatments allow you to enjoy your favorite foods and speak clearly with confidence.
  • Prevention of Future Complications: A small cavity left untreated can lead to a painful abscess. A single missing tooth can cause neighboring teeth to shift, leading to bite problems. Restorative care addresses issues early, preventing more complex and costly problems down the road.
  • Preservation of Jawbone and Facial Structure: Tooth loss leads to jawbone deterioration, which can cause your face to take on a sunken appearance. Dental implants, in particular, stimulate the bone, preserving your natural facial contours.
  • Improved Overall Health: Oral health is directly linked to systemic health. Treating infections and restoring proper function contribute to your overall well-being.
  • Enhanced Self-Esteem: A complete, healthy smile is a huge confidence booster. Restorative dentistry allows you to smile, laugh, and socialize without feeling self-conscious.

Your Guide to Post-Treatment Care and Longevity

To ensure your new restoration lasts for years to come, proper aftercare is essential. After your restorative dentistry procedure, we will provide you with specific instructions, but here are some general guidelines:

  1. Maintain Impeccable Oral Hygiene: Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush and floss at least once a day. For bridges and implants, we may recommend special floss threaders or water flossers.
  2. Attend Regular Check-ups and Cleanings: Visit us every six months so we can monitor the health of your restoration and your surrounding teeth and gums.
  3. Wear a Night Guard if Recommended: If you grind or clench your teeth (bruxism), a custom-fitted night guard can protect your natural teeth and your new restoration from excessive wear.
  4. Adopt Healthy Habits: Avoid chewing on hard objects (like ice or pens) and try to limit sugary foods and drinks that can lead to decay around the edges of your restoration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main difference between a crown and a filling?
A filling is used to repair a cavity or small damage within the tooth structure. A crown is used when the tooth is too compromised to support a filling; it covers and protects the entire tooth.
How long does a typical restorative dentistry procedure take?

This varies greatly. A filling can be completed in one short appointment. A dental implant procedure involves a healing period of several months. After your examination, we will provide a detailed timeline for your specific treatment plan.

Are wisdom teeth extractions considered restorative care?
Wisdom teeth removal is generally classified as oral surgery. However, if a wisdom tooth is functional and suffers from decay, a restorative dentistry treatment like a filling or crown could be applied. We evaluate each situation individually to recommend the best course of action.
What happens if I choose not to replace a missing tooth?
Ignoring a missing tooth can lead to a cascade of problems. The adjacent teeth can drift into the gap, causing misalignment. The opposing tooth can over-erupt. Most significantly, the jawbone in that area will begin to deteriorate due to lack of stimulation, which can weaken the foundation for other teeth and alter your facial appearance.
