What Is Orthodontics?

Orthodontics is a field within dentistry that focuses on identifying, preventing, and correcting misalignments of the teeth and jaws. The goal isn’t just to straighten teeth—it’s to improve the way your teeth, bite, and facial muscles work together.

Common orthodontic treatments include:

  • Traditional braces: Brackets and wires that gradually move teeth into proper alignment.
  • Clear aligners: Transparent trays that straighten teeth discreetly and comfortably.
  • Retainers: Devices that help maintain your new smile after treatment is complete.
  • Space maintainers: Custom appliances that hold space for incoming teeth in growing children.

At Marda Loop Dental Choice, our dentist in Marda Loop will assess your needs and guide you toward the option that best suits your smile and lifestyle.

Why You Might Need Orthodontic Treatment

Orthodontic care isn’t just about looks—it’s about oral health and long-term comfort. You might benefit from orthodontics if you notice:

  • Crowded, crooked, or overlapping teeth
  • Large gaps between teeth
  • Difficulty biting, chewing, or speaking clearly
  • Jaw pain, clicking, or tension
  • Teeth that don’t fit together when you close your mouth

Without treatment, misaligned teeth can lead to problems such as worn teeth, gum irritation, jaw pain, or headache. When you start orthodontic treatment near you, you’re improving more than just your smile—you’re also looking after your long-term dental health.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age is it best to start orthodontic treatment?

Orthodontic treatment can begin at almost any age, although many patients start between 10 and 14.

How long does orthodontic treatment take?

The timeline varies depending on the complexity of your case, but on average, orthodontic treatment takes between one and two years.

Are clear aligners as effective as braces?

Yes, for many cases. Clear aligners work well for mild to moderate alignment issues and are a discreet alternative to traditional braces.

Will orthodontic treatment hurt?

You may feel mild pressure or soreness at first, but it typically fades within a few days as your mouth adjusts.

How do I care for my teeth during treatment?

Brush and floss daily, avoid sticky or hard foods if you wear braces, and keep up with your regular check-ups with your dentist near you to stay on track.
