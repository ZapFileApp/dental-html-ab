What to Expect at Your Checkup

At The Ranch Dental Centre, we generally recommend routine checkups and cleanings every six months for most patients. These visits are simple but essential for maintaining and promoting your oral health. During your appointment, we may start by taking any necessary dental X-rays to see areas we can’t view directly, then carefully assess your gum health and check for plaque and tartar deposits on the teeth. Your Strathmore dentist will also look for specific areas of concern, such as early cavities or worn or damaged restorations. Our checkups also include an oral cancer screening, a head and neck exam, a jaw joint (TMJ) assessment, and a lymph node exam, along with a review of anything unusual that may appear on the X-rays.

Your hygienist will then remove plaque and tartar using professional instruments, polish your teeth with a gentle paste to lift surface stains, and, when appropriate, provide a fluoride treatment to help strengthen enamel. For our younger patients, we may recommend dental sealants as an extra layer of protection against cavities. Throughout the visit, our friendly team will explain each step, answer your questions, and share straightforward home-care tips tailored to your needs—because feeling informed and comfortable is just as important as the clinical care you receive.

Preventive Dental Care in Strathmore

Preventive dental care is all about protecting your teeth and gums before problems start. Good oral hygiene habits at home, combined with regular professional checkups, are the best way to maintain a healthy smile for life. Our team is here to help you understand the best practices, tools, and techniques to keep your mouth healthy every day. Below are three essential habits that every patient should focus on:

Tooth Brushing

Brushing your teeth at least twice a day — ideally in the morning and before bed — is your first defense against plaque and tooth decay. We recommend using a soft-bristled toothbrush and toothpaste approved by Canadian Dental Association, brushing for at least two minutes each time.

When brushing:

  • Hold your brush at a 45-degree angle to your gums.
  • Use gentle, circular motions to clean the outer, inner, and chewing surfaces of each tooth.
  • Don’t forget to brush your tongue to remove bacteria and freshen your breath.

The Canadian Dental Association recommends replacing your toothbrush every 3–4 months, or sooner if the bristles are frayed.

Flossing

Flossing once a day removes plaque and food particles from between your teeth and under the gumline — places a toothbrush simply can’t reach.

When flossing:

  • Use about 18 inches of floss, winding most around your middle fingers and holding it tightly between your thumbs and index fingers.
  • Gently slide the floss between teeth, curving it into a “C” shape against one tooth surface and then the other.
  • Avoid snapping the floss into your gums; move slowly and gently to avoid injury.

Flossing regularly helps prevent cavities between teeth and keeps your gums healthy, reducing the risk of gum disease.

Rinsing

Rinsing can be a simple but effective way to help keep your mouth clean throughout the day. If you’re unable to brush after a meal, rinsing with water can help wash away food particles and reduce bacteria buildup. Some patients may also benefit from using a mouth rinse, but not all products are the same. Before adding an over-the-counter rinse to your routine, it’s best to speak with your dentist or dental hygienist to ensure it’s the right choice for your needs. Depending on your oral health, your dental professional may also recommend additional aids to support your home care.

Cavity Treatment & Dental Fillings in Strathmore

Cavities, also called tooth decay, are one of the most common oral health issues for children, teens, and adults alike. They occur when plaque and bacteria break down tooth enamel, often due to factors such as:

  • Frequent consumption of sugary or acidic drinks
  • Inadequate brushing and flossing habits

Small cavities may go unnoticed, but as they progress, they can cause toothaches, sensitivity to hot or cold, visible dark spots or holes, and discomfort while chewing.

At The Ranch Dental Centre Strathmore, our goal is to detect and treat cavities early, before they become larger and lead to more serious problems such as infections or abscesses. During your visit, we’ll gently remove the decayed portion of the tooth and restore it with a durable filling that blends naturally with your smile. Regular dental checkups (ideally every six months) help us monitor your oral health and catch any new cavities before they worsen.

Crowns & Bridges in Strathmore

When a tooth is severely damaged, cracked, worn down, or weakened by decay, a dental crown can restore its strength, shape, and appearance. Crowns act like a cap or helmet placed over the tooth, protecting it while restoring full function. They can be made from materials such as porcelain, gold, or a combination, offering both durability and a natural look.

For patients missing one or more teeth, a dental bridge is a fixed restoration that fills the gap by anchoring an artificial tooth to the surrounding natural teeth or dental implants. Bridges not only restore your smile’s appearance but also prevent nearby teeth from shifting out of position.

The crown and bridge process typically involves:

  1. Preparation Appointment – The tooth (or teeth) is shaped to make room for the restoration, and an impression is taken to create a precise model for the lab using a scanner or impression materials.
  2. Fabrication & Try-In – After about a week, we receive your custom crown or bridge from our dental lab and ensure a perfect fit and bite.
  3. Permanent Placement – Once everything looks and feels right, the crown or bridge is securely cemented in place.

Dental Extraction in Strathmore

Tooth extractions are sometimes necessary to relieve pain, prevent further complications, and maintain your oral health. A tooth may need to be removed if:

  • It is severely decayed and cannot be restored.
  • It has been badly damaged in an accident.
  • It is a problematic impacted wisdom tooth.
  • It contributes to overcrowding or severe misalignment.
  • It is loose or untreatable due to gum disease.

Before every extraction, our dentists carefully review your medical history and any medications to minimize risks of bleeding or infection. Depending on your needs, we use different surgical techniques to ensure a safe, and comfortable procedure.

If you feel anxious about extractions, please let us know—we are here to provide reassurance and make your visit as stress-free as possible. Contact The Ranch Dental Centre to learn more about whether extraction may be the right solution for you.

Wisdom Tooth Extraction in Strathmore

Wisdom teeth can sometimes cause pain, infection, or crowding if they do not erupt properly. In these cases, removal may be the best option to protect your oral health. Wisdom tooth extractions are performed with the same care and focus on patient`s needs as other tooth removals.

Dental Implants in Strathmore

A dental implant is a surgical component that interfaces with the bone of the jaw to support a crown, bridge, or dentures. Dental implants can be used to replace a single tooth, or a combination may be used to replace several teeth. Implants can also serve as an anchor to support a complete denture.

Most of the cases that we do at The Ranch Dental Centre are called guided implant placement, which requires you to take a CBCT scan (CAT scan) in order to fabricate a surgical stent. After the implant is fused to the jaw bone, an implant-supported crown will be fabricated in collaboration with our lab.

Benefits of dental implants include:

  • Restored chewing function and comfort
  • Prevention of bone loss in the jaw
  • A natural look and feel that blends with your smile

From start to finish, our team will walk you through the process, working closely with our dental labs to create restorations that bring you function and aesthetics.

Bite Therapy in Strathmore

An imbalanced bite can lead to a range of issues, including jaw pain, teeth grinding (bruxism), headaches, and uneven tooth wear. Bite therapy is designed to address these concerns, ensuring your teeth come together properly for improved comfort and function.

Our team evaluates the alignment of your teeth and jaw to recommend the most effective treatment, whether it’s an adjustment, protective appliance, or restorative care. By correcting bite issues early, you can prevent long-term damage and enjoy a healthier, more comfortable smile.

Teeth Grinding (Bruxism)

Bruxism, or teeth grinding, is more than just a habit—it can seriously impact your oral health. Over time, grinding can wear down enamel, chip or fracture teeth, and contribute to jaw pain, headaches, and muscle soreness.

At The Ranch Dental Centre, our dentists carefully monitor for signs of bruxism during your regular checkups. Because stress, sleep disturbances, or bite alignment can all contribute, we aim to identify the root cause and create a personalized plan.

One common solution is a custom-made night guard, a thin, transparent device worn over your teeth while you sleep. This helps protect your enamel, reduce strain, and prevent further damage from grinding.

Root Canal Therapy

Root Canal Therapy in Strathmore

Root canal therapy allows us to save a tooth that would otherwise need to be removed.

The pulp inside the tooth—which contains nerves and blood vessels—can become damaged by deep cavities, cracks, or trauma. If left untreated, this can lead to infection, swelling, and severe pain.

Root canal treatment involves:

  1. Gently numbing the area to keep you comfortable.
  2. Accessing and removing the infected or damaged pulp.
  3. Cleaning and disinfecting the inside of the tooth.
  4. Filling and sealing the tooth to restore function and strength.

This routine procedure helps relieve pain while preserving your natural tooth. If you have questions about whether a root canal is right for you, connect with The Ranch Dental Centre team today.
