Dr. Jay Hambleton, General Dentist

Throughout his 30+ years in general dentistry, Dr. Jay Hambleton has been at the forefront of advancements in dental technology, as both a dentist & an educator.

Everyone deserves a happy, healthy smile. Dr. Jason (or Dr. Jay as many know him) uses the latest in computerized dentistry to help his patients achieve optimal dental health with better, faster results and less discomfort or inconvenience. He specializes in laser dentistry, orthodontics, implants, and digital restorative dentistry. Dr. Jason has been a referring dentist for Infant Laser-Assisted Lip and Tongue Tie Revisions for over a decade. He is passionate about oral health for our tiniest patients. 

He graduated from the University of Alberta, DDS Dentistry in 1993. Since 2009, Dr. Jay has been an educational mentor and has taught digital dentistry in Canada and the US. Dr. Jay has been a certified CEREC (Chairside Economical Restoration of Esthetic Ceramic) mentor and trainer with ISCD, the International Society of Computerized Dentistry, for computer-aided CAD-CAM dentistry. From routine checkups to advanced dental therapies, your smile is in good hands with Dr. Jason Hambleton.

Now Offering Solea Laser Dentistry

Experience dental care without the drill. Our Solea Laser offers a quieter, faster, and more comfortable visit, often without needles or anesthesia.

Many patients feel anxious about dental care due to the sound of the drill, injections, and fear of discomfort. Traditional dental tools often require local anesthesia and can leave patients sore or numb afterward.

Breton Dental Clinic is pleased to offer Solea laser dentistry, an advanced CO₂ laser that allows us to perform many procedures without needles or drilling. Solea provides quieter, more comfortable care with minimal bleeding and faster healing. Most treatments can be completed in a single visit, allowing you to return to your day within minutes.

Pleased to Offer Infant Tongue & Lip Tie Revisions

Laser frenectomy is a painless treatment that prevents nursing difficulties now and speaking difficulties later. 

A newborn who suffers from tongue-tie may be referred for a frenectomy. This corrective surgery clips the restrictive frenum tissue to provide a greater range of motion important for nursing and speaking. Early intervention is key, but many parents are hesitant. Traditional treatment is stressful for parents and may cause undue pain and bleeding for infants.

Breton Dental Clinic is pleased to offer Laser Frenectomy as a safe and gentle alternative that renders virtually no bleeding, pain, or risk of infection. Dr. Jay has been a referring dentist for this treatment for well over a decade. The healing is swift and the procedure lasts less than 10 minutes.
