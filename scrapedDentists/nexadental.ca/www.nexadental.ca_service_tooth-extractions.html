When is a Tooth Extraction Needed?

For the most part, tooth extraction is a brief procedure that is very common amongst North American’s. Some reasons people get tooth extractions include:

  • A chipped or broken tooth
  • Gum disease that affected the structure of the tooth
  • To give wisdom teeth more space to grow
  • An extra tooth that is growing out of place
  • Tooth decay or infection

Some of these issues may seem bearable and easy to ignore, but doing so can lead to severe pain and toothaches. In order to prevent an infection from spreading to other parts of your mouth, you may need to have the infected tooth extracted. Tooth removal can prevent you from having symptoms like headaches, toothaches, sore gums, and even a fever.
