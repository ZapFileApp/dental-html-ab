Dental Implants

Tooth Replacement Supported by Clinical Assessment in Westlock, Alberta

Dental implants are a tooth replacement option that may be considered when one or more teeth are missing. At Westlock Family Dental, implant suitability is determined following a thorough clinical assessment, and patients are provided with clear information on the process, alternatives, and expected timelines before any treatment decisions are made.

Overview

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is placed into the jawbone to serve as a foundation for a dental restoration such as a crown, bridge, or denture. Over time, the implant integrates with the surrounding bone — a process referred to as osseointegration — which provides a stable base for the restoration.

Implant treatment is a multi-stage process that typically involves assessment, placement, a healing period, and the fabrication and fitting of the final restoration. The overall timeline varies depending on individual clinical circumstances.

When It May Be Recommended

Dental implant treatment may be considered in a number of clinical situations, which may include:

  • One or more missing teeth where the surrounding bone and tissue are assessed as suitable
  • A patient seeking a fixed tooth replacement option as an alternative to a removable appliance
  • A missing tooth affecting function, bite stability, or the alignment of surrounding teeth
  • A patient who has been assessed as a suitable candidate following review of medical history, bone density, and overall oral health

Not all patients are suitable candidates for dental implants. Suitability depends on a range of clinical factors including bone volume, overall oral health, medical history, and individual circumstances. Your dentist will discuss findings and available alternatives before any treatment is recommended.

What to Expect

Patient Information

Assessment and Treatment Planning

Before implant treatment is considered, your dentist will conduct a thorough clinical assessment including a review of your oral health, medical history, and imaging of the treatment area. Findings, treatment options, costs, and timelines will be discussed in detail before any decisions are made.

Implant Placement

The implant placement procedure involves placing the titanium post into the jawbone at the site of the missing tooth. Local anaesthetic is typically used to support comfort during the procedure. Following placement, a healing period is required to allow the implant to integrate with the surrounding bone before the final restoration can be fitted.

Healing and Integration

The healing period following implant placement varies depending on individual circumstances and clinical factors. Your dentist will monitor healing at follow-up appointments and advise when the next stage of treatment is appropriate.

Restoration Fitting

Once the implant has integrated sufficiently, the final restoration — typically a crown, bridge, or denture component — is fabricated and fitted. Your dentist will check the fit, function, and bite before the restoration is finalised.

Appointment Length and Overall Timeline

Implant treatment involves multiple appointments over an extended period. The overall timeline varies significantly depending on individual clinical circumstances, healing response, and the complexity of treatment. Your dentist will provide a more specific estimate based on your assessment findings.

Aftercare Considerations

Following implant placement, some patients may experience mild discomfort, swelling, or sensitivity in the treatment area during the initial healing period. This varies depending on individual circumstances and the complexity of the procedure.

General aftercare considerations following implant placement may include:

  • Following specific post-procedure instructions provided by your dentist
  • Avoiding hard, crunchy, or sticky foods during the initial healing period
  • Maintaining careful oral hygiene around the implant site as directed
  • Attending all scheduled follow-up appointments to monitor healing and integration
  • Contacting the clinic promptly if you experience significant discomfort, swelling, or other concerns

Once fully restored, implant-supported restorations require ongoing oral hygiene care and regular dental visits to support long-term function. Individual outcomes vary depending on clinical circumstances, oral hygiene, and overall health.

Important Note

Dental implant treatment is one of several tooth replacement options that may be considered following the loss of a tooth. Your dentist will discuss all available alternatives — including bridges and removable appliances — so you can make an informed decision based on your individual clinical situation and personal preferences.

Implant treatment involves multiple stages and a significant time commitment. Suitability, timelines, and expected outcomes vary depending on individual needs and clinical assessment. All findings and recommendations will be communicated clearly before any treatment proceeds.
