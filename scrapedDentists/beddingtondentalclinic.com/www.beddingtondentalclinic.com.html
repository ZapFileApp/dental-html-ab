    Dental FAQ's

    How much do dental implants cost in Alberta?

    In Alberta, dental implant costs vary, with placement ranging from $2000 to $3000, plus $1400 to $1800 for the tooth. At our dental clinic near you, we currently charge 2200 for each implant. With our expert dentists and great value, you’re in good hands for your dental needs.

    How to remove black stains from teeth naturally?

    To naturally remove black stains from teeth, try these easy methods:

    1. Baking Soda: Mix with water to make a paste; brush gently.
    2. Activated Charcoal: Use a small amount to absorb stains.
    3. Apple Cider Vinegar: Rinse diluted solution in your mouth.
    4. Fruits: Rub strawberries or pineapple on teeth for their natural acids.
    5. Oil Pulling: Swish coconut or sesame oil in your mouth for several minutes.

    Remember to brush regularly and visit our Beddington dentist for the optimal results!

    How do I find a family dentist in my hometown?

    Ask for Recommendations: Seek advice from friends and family.

    1. Check Online Reviews: Look at Google or Yelp for patient feedback.
    2. Consult Insurance: Verify with your insurance for the approved dentists’ list.
    3. Visit Websites: Explore services and testimonials online.
    4. Schedule Consultation: Meet the dentist to see if they match.

    Follow these steps to find your hometown’s ideal family dentist.

    What's really considered a dental emergency?

    A dental emergency is any situation that requires immediate attention to save a tooth, stop bleeding, or alleviate severe pain or swelling. Common examples include:

    • Knocked-out teeth.
    • Severe toothaches.
    • Broken teeth with exposed nerves.
    • Injuries to the mouth or jaw.

    If you’re unsure whether your situation is an emergency, it is recommended to contact our emergency dentist in NW Calgary or seek urgent dental care for advice.

    2. Dental Bridges: Fill the gap and keep your smile looking great.
    3. Dentures: Easy-to-use replacements for multiple missing teeth.
    4. Implant-Supported Dentures: Super comfy and secure for a worry-free smile.

    Consult a dentist near you to determine the most suitable option for your specific needs and preferences.

    What should I do if my tooth is knocked out?

    If you find yourself with a knocked-out tooth, stay calm! Hold it by the top part and rinse it carefully. If you can, place it back where it came from, or keep it in milk or saliva. Then, don’t wait—head straight to an emergency dentist near you. They’ll know just how to save your tooth and smile. Don’t hesitate; act fast for the good results!
