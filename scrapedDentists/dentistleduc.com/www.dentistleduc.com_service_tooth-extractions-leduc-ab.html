What are Tooth Extractions?

A tooth extraction in leduc is a dental process where a dentist removes a tooth from its socket in the jawbone. This is usually done when a tooth is:

  • Severely damaged or decayed
  • Infected and can’t be salvaged with a root canal
  • Causing crowding or impeding other teeth (frequent in orthodontic cases)
  • A wisdom tooth that’s impacted or painful
  • Loosening due to gum disease

Benefits of Tooth Extraction

Recipients of this treatment can experience many benefits, including:
  • Pain alleviation caused by damaged or infected teeth.
  • Preventing infection from spreading to other teeth.
  • Helps safeguard the health of adjacent teeth and gums.
  • Creates space for teeth for orthodontic adjustments.
  • Can be done in preparation for implants or dentures.

Types of Tooth Extractions We Offer

At Alpha Dental Health Centre, we offer two main forms of tooth removal:

  1. Simple Extractions – This is done when a tooth has erupted entirely and is visible above the gums. It’s conducted under local anesthesia.
  2. Surgical Extractions – This is often necessary for teeth that are fractured at the gum line or haven’t completely erupted (i.e. impacted wisdom teeth). The treatment may include creating a small incision in the gum and extraction of some bone. Local anesthesia or more moderate sedation can be provided.

Our seasoned dentist in Leduc will be at your side throughout the treatment to ensure you’re at ease.

Our Tooth Extraction Process

Here is a general overview of what you can expect when you come see us for a tooth extraction new you:
  1. We begin with a full dental exam and will take a series of digital X-rays to ascertain the precise location of the tooth and how damaged it is.
  2. A detailed treatment plan will be devised based on your preferences and needs.
  3. Local anesthesia or dental sedation is provided to ensure you don’t feel any discomfort. Dental forceps are used to thoroughly loosen the tooth in its place before removal. If needed, an incision is made in the surrounding gum tissue to better access the tooth.
  4. Once the tooth is gone, the area is cleaned and disinfected before being stitched closed. You’ll be given aftercare instructions to help your mouth heal without complications.
Don’t hesitate to let our team know if you have any questions or concerns at any point during your treatment.
