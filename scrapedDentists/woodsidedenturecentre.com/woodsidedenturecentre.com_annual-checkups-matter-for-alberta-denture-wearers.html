Why Annual Checkups Matter for Alberta Denture Wearers: What Changes after 1 Year with Dentures

by Megan Skarsen | Sep 15, 2025 | Denture Care

Why Annual Checkups Matter for Alberta Denture Wearers: Whether You’re One Year In or Three Years In

For many people, getting dentures is life-changing. Dentures restore the ability to eat, speak, and smile with confidence. But once the adjustment period is over, a common question arises: “If my dentures feel fine, do I really need to see my denturist every year?”

The short answer is yes. Whether you’ve been wearing dentures for one year or three years, annual checkups are essential for maintaining not only your dentures but also your overall oral health. At Woodside Denture Centre, serving Airdrie dentures, Alberta denture wearers, and especially Alberta seniors, we see every day how proactive care prevents serious issues down the road.

How Your Mouth Changes Over Time

One of the biggest misconceptions is that once dentures fit, they’ll stay that way. In reality, your mouth is constantly changing:

  • Bone Resorption: After natural teeth are removed, the jawbone begins to shrink. This is most rapid during the first year but continues every year after.
  • Gum Tissue Changes: The soft tissues supporting your dentures shift and thin over time.
  • Bite Alignment Shifts: Even subtle changes in how your upper and lower dentures meet can impact comfort and chewing efficiency.

These changes often happen gradually, so you may not notice them until discomfort becomes severe. Annual checkups help catch issues early, when they’re easier to fix.

Why Annual Checkups Are Essential

  1. Maintaining Proper Fit

Loose dentures aren’t just inconvenient—they can lead to sore spots, irritation, and even fungal infections. At an annual checkup, a denturist checks the fit and may recommend a reline (refitting the inside of the denture to fit your changing gums).

Without a checkup: Dentures that are left loose can accelerate bone loss, increase discomfort, and even make speaking or eating socially stressful.

  1. Protecting Oral Health

Dentures replace teeth, but they don’t replace your oral health needs. Annual visits include a comprehensive oral exam to check for:

Signs of oral cancer

  • Gum disease or fungal infections
  • Lesions or irritation caused by ill-fitting dentures

For Alberta seniors, who may be at higher risk of oral health concerns, these exams are especially critical.

Without a checkup, small issues like irritation can escalate into infections or overlooked signs of more serious conditions—something Alberta denture wearers should be especially mindful of.

  1. Improving Chewing Efficiency

Dentures that no longer align properly can reduce your ability to chew effectively. This not only impacts nutrition but also enjoyment of meals—an important quality-of-life factor for Alberta denture wearers.

Without a checkup: You may unconsciously avoid certain foods, leading to a limited diet and potential nutritional deficiencies.

  1. Preventing TMJ and Jaw Strain

When dentures shift over time, your bite alignment changes. This can put stress on the temporomandibular joint (TMJ), leading to headaches, jaw pain, and muscle fatigue.

Without a checkup: Chronic strain can develop, making even everyday activities like talking uncomfortable.

  1. Extending the Life of Your Dentures

Dentures are an investment. For Alberta denture wearers, proper care and regular adjustments can help them last 5–7 years or longer. Annual checkups allow your denturist to spot early signs of wear, make adjustments, and professionally polish your dentures.

Without a checkup: Dentures may break, crack, or wear down faster, leading to costly replacements sooner than necessary.

  1. Monitoring for Implant Options

Many Alberta denture wearers start with traditional dentures but later consider implant-supported dentures for added stability. Annual checkups are an opportunity to review whether implants may now be the right choice—especially if bone resorption or looseness is becoming a problem.

Risks of Skipping Annual Denture Checkups

To put it simply, skipping your yearly denture appointment can create a domino effect of problems:

  • Discomfort: Loose dentures cause sore spots and rubbing.
  • Health Risks: Missed detection of oral cancer or infections.
  • Functional Decline: Reduced chewing efficiency, poor nutrition, and digestive issues.
  • Speech Problems: Changes in fit may affect clarity and confidence in speaking.
  • Bone Loss Acceleration: The longer you go without adjustments, the faster bone loss can progress.
  • Costly Replacements: Neglected dentures wear out more quickly and often need replacement instead of simple maintenance.

Annual Checkups: What to Expect

At Woodside Denture Centre, a typical annual checkup for Airdrie dentures or Alberta denture wearers or patients includes:

  • Denture Fit Assessment: Checking stability, retention, and bite alignment.
  • Reline or Adjustment (if needed): Ensuring comfort and function.
  • Oral Tissue Exam: Screening for abnormalities, lesions, or irritation.
  • Professional Cleaning & Polishing: Restoring freshness and extending denture life.
  • Discussion of Options: Reviewing whether upgrades like implant-supported dentures would be beneficial.

Special Considerations for Alberta Seniors

For Alberta seniors, denture checkups are more than routine—they’re a critical part of health maintenance. Seniors may face unique challenges such as:

  • Dry Mouth (Xerostomia): Often a side effect of medications, increasing the risk of fungal infections under dentures.
  • Reduced Dexterity: Arthritis or mobility issues can make cleaning dentures more difficult, increasing the need for professional help.
  • Dietary Needs: Seniors need nutrient-dense foods, and ill-fitting dentures can make eating these harder.

By scheduling annual visits, Alberta denture wearers and seniors can preserve both their oral and overall health.

One Year vs. Three Years: Why Timing Still Matters

One Year In: This is often when the first major reline is needed, as the most rapid bone resorption has occurred. Annual checkups at this stage prevent looseness from turning into long-term damage.

Three Years In: By this time, fit changes may be more pronounced, and dentures may start showing signs of wear. Regular adjustments ensure continued comfort and prevent premature replacement.

No matter where you are in your denture journey, Alberta denture wearers should remember that annual checkups are the key to long-term success.

Final Thoughts

Dentures are not a “set it and forget it” solution. Your mouth continues to change, and your dentures need ongoing care to keep up. Whether you are one year in or three years in, Alberta denture wearers benefit from annual checkups that safeguard oral health, extend the life of dentures, and preserve confidence.
