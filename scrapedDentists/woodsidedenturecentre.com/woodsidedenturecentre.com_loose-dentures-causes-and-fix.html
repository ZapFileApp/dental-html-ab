Why Dentures Become Loose (And How to Fix Them)

by Megan Skarsen | Apr 16, 2026 | Denture Care

A Guide for Denture Patients in Airdrie, Alberta

Dentures are designed to restore your smile, improve chewing ability, and help you speak clearly. However, over time, many patients notice that their dentures no longer fit as securely as they once did. Loose dentures are a common concern and can cause discomfort, sore spots, and difficulty eating. If you’re experiencing these issues, seeking professional care from a trusted provider like Airdrie Denture Clinic can help restore the proper fit and function of your dentures.

If you live in Airdrie or surrounding communities, understanding why dentures become loose and what can be done to fix the problem can help you maintain comfort and confidence in your smile.

At Woodside Denture Centre in Airdrie, denturists regularly help patients adjust, reline, replace dentures, and repair dentures so they continue to fit properly. Here’s what you should know about loose dentures and how they can be corrected.

Why Do Dentures Become Loose?

Dentures rarely stay the same over time. The primary reason dentures loosen is that your mouth naturally changes after tooth loss.

Several factors can contribute to a loose denture fit.

Jawbone Changes After Tooth Loss

One of the most common reasons dentures become loose is bone resorption, which occurs after natural teeth are removed.

When teeth are present, their roots stimulate the jawbone. Once teeth are lost, the bone gradually shrinks because it is no longer being stimulated.

As the jawbone changes shape, dentures may:

  • Shift while speaking
  • Move while eating
  • Feel less stable
  • Create sore spots on the gums

This natural bone change can happen gradually over several years.

Normal Wear and Tear

Dentures are durable, but they are still subject to everyday wear.

Over time:

  • Denture teeth can wear down
  • The acrylic base may lose its precise shape
  • Small changes in the material can affect fit

Even minor wear can cause dentures to feel looser than they originally did.

Regular denture checkups help identify these changes before they become uncomfortable.

Weight Loss or Health Changes

Changes in body weight can also affect denture fit.

Significant weight loss may lead to changes in the shape of the gums and facial structure, which can impact how dentures sit in the mouth.

Health conditions that affect gum tissue may also influence denture stability.

If dentures suddenly feel loose after major weight changes, it may be time for an adjustment.

Aging Gum Tissue

As we age, our gums naturally change.

The tissue beneath dentures can slowly shrink or shift over time, which may cause dentures to lose suction and stability.

This is especially common with full upper dentures, which rely heavily on suction against the palate.

Routine evaluations help ensure dentures remain comfortable as these changes occur.

Signs Your Dentures May Need Adjustment

Loose dentures are not just inconvenient—they can also lead to irritation and oral health problems.

Common signs include:

  • Dentures moving while eating
  • Clicking sounds when speaking
  • Frequent sore spots on the gums
  • Difficulty chewing certain foods
  • Dentures falling out of place

If you experience any of these symptoms, it may be time to visit a denturist for an evaluation.

How Loose Dentures Can Be Fixed

The good news is that loose dentures can often be corrected with relatively simple treatments.

A denturist will determine the best solution based on your specific needs.

Denture Relines

A denture reline is one of the most common ways to correct loose dentures.

During this procedure, the inside surface of the denture is reshaped so it fits the current contours of your gums.

Relines help:

  • Improve stability
  • Reduce irritation
  • Restore proper suction
  • Extend the life of your dentures

Many patients require a denture relining every two years as their gums change.

Denture Adjustments

Sometimes loose dentures only require minor adjustments.

A denturist may smooth certain areas, adjust pressure points, or refine the fit to improve comfort and stability.

These adjustments can often be completed quickly during a regular appointment.

Denture Rebasing

In cases where the denture teeth are still in good condition but the base has changed significantly, a denture rebase may be recommended.

Rebasing replaces the denture base material while keeping the original teeth.

This option can restore the denture’s fit without needing an entirely new appliance.

Implant-Supported Dentures

For patients who struggle with persistent denture looseness, implant-supported dentures may be an excellent solution.

Dental implants are placed in the jawbone and act as anchors for the dentures.

This provides:

  • Improved stability
  • Stronger chewing ability
  • Reduced denture movement
  • Increased comfort

Implant dentures can significantly improve the quality of life for patients who have difficulty with traditional dentures.

Can Denture Adhesive Fix Loose Dentures?

Many patients use denture adhesive to help improve denture stability.

While adhesives can provide temporary support, it is not a long-term solution for poorly fitting dentures.

If you find yourself relying heavily on adhesive, it may be time to visit a denturist for an evaluation and possible adjustment.

Properly fitting dentures should remain stable without excessive adhesive use.

How to Prevent Dentures from Becoming Loose

While some changes are unavoidable, there are ways to maintain denture fit for as long as possible.

Helpful tips include:

  • Schedule regular denture checkups
  • Clean dentures daily
  • Handle dentures carefully to prevent damage
  • Avoid attempting at-home repairs
  • Visit a denturist if discomfort develops

Regular denture maintenance can help identify issues early and prevent major problems.

When Should Dentures Be Replaced?

Even with proper care, dentures typically last 5 to 7 years.

If dentures are significantly worn or no longer fit well despite adjustments, replacing them may provide the best comfort and function.

Modern dentures offer improved materials, better aesthetics, and a more natural appearance than older designs.
