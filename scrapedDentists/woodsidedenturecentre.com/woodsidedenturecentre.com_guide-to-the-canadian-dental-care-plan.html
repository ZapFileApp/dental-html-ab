Understanding the Canadian Dental Care Plan

by Megan Skarsen | May 1, 2025 | Denture Care

Access to affordable dental care is essential for maintaining overall health and well-being. In Canada, programs like the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) and Alberta’s Dental Assistance for Seniors (DASP) provide crucial support to eligible individuals, ensuring they receive necessary dental services without undue financial burden. For residents of Alberta, particularly seniors, understanding these benefits and their renewal processes is vital. Moreover, choosing the right denturist in Alberta, such as those at Woodside Denture Centre, can significantly enhance the quality of care received.​

Understanding the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP)

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) is a federal initiative aimed at making dental care more accessible and affordable for eligible Canadian residents. The plan is being implemented in phases, with applications currently open for:​

  • Seniors aged 65 and over
  • Children under the age of 18
  • Adults with a valid Disability Tax Credit certificate for 2023

Starting in May 2025, applications will open for Canadians aged 18 to 64 who do not have access to dental insurance. The application rollout is scheduled as follows:​

  • May 1, 2025: Applications open for Canadians aged 55 to 64.
  • May 15, 2025: Applications open for Canadians aged 18 to 34.
  • May 29, 2025: Applications open for Canadians aged 35 to 54.​

To be eligible for the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP), applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Income Threshold: An adjusted family net income below $90,000.​
  • No Access to Dental Insurance: Individuals should not have existing dental coverage. If you have dental coverage through a provincial, territorial, or federal government social program, you can still qualify for the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP).
  • Residency: Must be a Canadian resident.​
  • Tax Compliance: Must have filed the previous year’s income tax return.​

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) covers a range of dental services, diagnostic and preventive services, restorative services, and dentures. Specific services such as complete dentures, partial dentures, denture repairs, relines, and rebases are included in the coverage.

Renewal Process for Canadian Dental Care Plan Coverage

To continue to be covered under the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP), you will need to renew your coverage annually to confirm that you still meet the eligibility requirements. The renewal process for the 2025–2026 coverage period is as follows:

  1. File Your 2024 Tax Return: Ensure that you have filed your 2024 income tax return and received your Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).​
  2. Renewal Period: Renewals are open from March 2025 onward. It’s advisable to renew as soon as you receive your Notice of Assessment to avoid any gaps in coverage.​
  3. Renewal Deadline: Submit your renewal application before the June 1, 2025, deadline. Failing to renew by this date may result in a lapse of coverage after June 30, 2025. ​

Renewal can be completed through the following methods:​

  • Online: Via My Service Canada Account (MSCA).
  • By Phone: Through the automated self-serve system by calling 1-833-537-4342.​

It’s important to have your Social Insurance Number (SIN), client number, and updated contact information ready when renewing. ​

Alberta’s Dental Assistance for Seniors Program (DASP)

In addition to the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP), Alberta offers the Dental and Optical Assistance for Seniors Program, providing financial assistance to eligible seniors for basic dental services. The program offers up to a maximum of $5,000 of coverage every five years for select dental services and procedures aimed at maintaining a reasonable level of dental health.​

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Age: 65 years or older.​
  • Residency: Must be an Alberta resident and have lived in the province for at least three months before applying.​
  • Income: Income thresholds apply, with full or partial coverage determined based on annual income levels.

The $5,000 coverage is renewed every five years, and any unused portion does not carry over to the next period. Therefore, it’s advisable to utilize the benefits within each five-year term.
