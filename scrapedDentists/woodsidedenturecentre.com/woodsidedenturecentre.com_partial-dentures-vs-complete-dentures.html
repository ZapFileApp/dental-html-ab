Partial Dentures vs. Complete Dentures: What’s Right for You?

by Megan Skarsen | Mar 15, 2026 | Denture Benefits

A Guide for Albertans Considering Denture Options

Losing teeth — whether due to decay, trauma, or age-related wear — can impact not only your smile but also your overall health, nutrition, and confidence. One of the first questions many patients ask is: “Should I get partial dentures or full dentures?” At Woodside Denture Centre, we help you answer this question based on your oral health, lifestyle needs, and long-term wellbeing.

This comprehensive guide explains the differences between partial and complete dentures, how each supports oral health and quality of life, and how to choose the best modern dentures option in Alberta.

Understanding Dentures: Basics and Purpose

Dentures are custom-made removable prosthetics designed to replace missing teeth. They help restore:

  • Chewing ability — improving digestion and nutrition
  • Clear speech — by supporting lips and tongue positioning
  • Facial structure — preventing the “sunken” look caused by tooth loss
  • Oral health — by reducing bite imbalances and protecting remaining teeth
  • Confidence and quality of life

Whether you need a partial or full set depends on how many natural teeth remain and your overall oral condition.

What Are Partial Dentures?

Partial dentures are designed for patients who still have remaining natural teeth – filling in the gaps where teeth where once present.

Key Features

  • Support from natural teeth: Partials often use clasps or precision attachments to anchor to adjacent healthy teeth.
  • Maintain alignment: They prevent remaining teeth from drifting into empty spaces, helping preserve your bite and jaw alignment.
  • Removable for hygiene: Partials can be taken out for daily cleaning, allowing improved maintenance of gums and neighboring natural teeth.
  • Flexible and adaptable: Modern partial dentures can be customized with metal frameworks, flexible materials, or acrylic bases based on comfort and lifestyle.

Health & Wellness Benefits

  • Preserves remaining natural teeth: By stabilizing your bite, partials reduce stress on healthy teeth.
  • Supports gum health: Easy removal for cleaning helps prevent gum disease and plaque buildup.
  • Improves digestion: Restored chewing function supports better food breakdown and nutrient absorption.

Ideal Candidates

Partial dentures might be right for you if:

  • You have several missing teeth but retain multiple healthy ones.
  • You want a non-invasive, cost-effective solution without surgery.
  • You are seeking a transitional option while considering future implant-based solutions.

What Are Complete Dentures?

Complete dentures replace an entire set of teeth in either the upper or lower jaw — or both — when all natural teeth are missing or cannot be preserved.

Key Features

  • Full-arch replacement: They sit directly on the gums and are custom moulded for comfort and fit.
  • Removable: Designed to be taken out daily for cleaning and overnight rest.
  • Aesthetic support: Full dentures enhance facial structure and provide a natural looking smile.

Health & Wellness Benefits

  • Comprehensive restoration: Ideal when remaining teeth are gone or are not salvageable.
  • Speech and chewing function: Full dentures help you enjoy a wider range of foods and speak with clarity.
  • Confidence boost: A complete smile can have significant psychological and social benefits.

Ideal Candidates

Complete dentures may be appropriate if:

  • You have lost all teeth in one or both arches.
  • Tooth decay or gum disease has compromised some or all remaining natural teeth.
  • A balanced approach to restoring ability to eat with a new smile.

Both types play a significant role in oral wellness, but the right choice depends on your dental health, goals, lifestyle, and budget.

Health Considerations & Long-term Wellness of Complete Dentures

Jawbone & Tissue Health

Missing teeth can contribute to bone loss over time and changes in facial structure. Dentures help mitigate these changes by supporting tissue and distributing bite forces. Regular check‑ups ensure proper fit and prevent issues like gum irritation or sore spots.

Nutrition & Digestion

Inadequate chewing function affects digestion and nutrient absorption. Both partial and complete dentures restore chewing ability, allowing you to eat a balanced diet and maintain digestive health.

 Confidence

Tooth loss can affect self-esteem, speech clarity, and willingness to engage socially. Choosing the right denture solution restores your confidence and enhances quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What’s the main difference between partial and complete dentures?

Partial dentures replace one or several missing teeth and rely on remaining natural teeth for support, while complete dentures are used when all teeth in a jaw are missing.

Are dentures comfortable?

Most patients usually adapt to their new dentures within a few weeks; However, consistent check-ups every year are recommended to allow our team to assess any changes that occur over time.

How long do dentures last?

With proper care, dentures typically last 5–7 years. Changes in gum and jawbone structure can necessitate relines or replacements over time.

Can I switch from partial to full dentures later?

Yes. If changing oral health needs requires it, your denturist can recommend adjusting your treatment plan accordingly.
