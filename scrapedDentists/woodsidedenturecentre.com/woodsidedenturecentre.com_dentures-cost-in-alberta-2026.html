Cost of Dentures in Alberta (2026 Guide)

by Megan Skarsen | May 12, 2026 | Denture

What Patients in Airdrie Should Know Before Getting Dentures

If you’re considering dentures, one of the first questions that comes to mind is “How much do dentures cost in Alberta?”

The answer can vary depending on several factors, including the type of dentures you need, the materials used, and whether additional procedures are required. For patients in Airdrie and surrounding communities, understanding these costs can help you make informed decisions about restoring your smile and improving your oral health.

At Woodside Denture Centre in Airdrie, patients receive personalized care and guidance when choosing the right denture solution. This guide explains typical denture costs in Alberta in 2026, what affects pricing, and how dental insurance or government programs may help cover treatment.

Average Cost of Dentures in Alberta

Denture costs vary depending on whether you need partial dentures, full dentures, or implant-supported dentures. Below is a general estimate of what patients across Alberta can expect to pay.

Complete Dentures (Full Dentures)

Complete dentures replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw.

Typical cost range in Alberta:

  • $750 – $3,500 per arch
  • $3,500 – $7,000 for upper and lower dentures

The price often depends on the quality of materials, the level of customization, and the complexity of the patient’s oral structure.

Higher-end dentures may include improved aesthetics, better durability, and a more natural look.

Partial Dentures

Partial dentures are used when some natural teeth remain.

Typical cost range:

  • $800 – $2,000

Partial dentures may include metal frameworks or flexible materials that attach to existing teeth to stabilize the appliance.

Many patients choose partial dentures because they are a cost-effective solution that preserves remaining natural teeth.

Implant-Supported Dentures

Implant-supported dentures are considered one of the most stable and comfortable options available.

Typical cost range:

  • $6,000 – $30,000+

This option involves placing dental implants into the jawbone to anchor the dentures securely. While they are more expensive initially, implant dentures provide:

  • Improved stability
  • Better chewing ability
  • Reduced bone loss over time

Many patients find the long-term comfort and functionality worth the investment.

What Factors Affect the Cost of Dentures?

Several factors can influence how much dentures cost in Alberta.

Type of Dentures

Different denture styles require different materials and fabrication techniques.

Common options include:

  • Immediate dentures
  • Conventional dentures
  • Partial dentures
  • Implant-supported dentures

More complex designs typically require additional appointments, specialized materials, and precise adjustments, which can affect pricing.

Materials Used

Dentures can be made using a variety of materials.

Higher-quality dentures often use:

  • Premium acrylics
  • Flexible polymers
  • Advanced composite teeth

These materials can improve comfort, durability, and appearance, but they may increase the overall cost.

Adjustments and Relines

After receiving dentures, most patients require periodic adjustments.

Relines help ensure the denture continues to fit properly as your gums and jawbone change over time.

Typical costs for relines range between:

  • $500 – $700

Regular denture maintenance helps extend the life of your dentures and maintain comfort.

Additional Procedures

Some patients require additional treatments before dentures can be fitted.

These may include:

  • Tooth extractions
  • Bone smoothing
  • Implant placement

These procedures can add to the overall cost, but they are often necessary to ensure the dentures fit properly.

Are Dentures Covered by Insurance in Alberta?

Many extended health insurance plans provide partial coverage for dentures.

Coverage typically ranges from:

  • 50% – 80% of the cost

However, policies vary significantly depending on your provider and plan.

Patients are encouraged to check with their insurance company or speak with their denture clinic in Alberta about submitting claims and verifying coverage.

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP)

In recent years, the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) has expanded access to dental services for eligible Canadians.

Depending on your eligibility and income level, this program may help cover some denture-related treatments.

Eligibility often includes:

  • Seniors
  • Children
  • Families with lower household incomes

Your denturist can help explain whether denture services may qualify under this program.

How Long Do Dentures Last?

Dentures are designed to last many years, but they are not permanent.

Most dentures last approximately:

5 to 7 years

Over time, natural changes in the jawbone and gums can affect the fit of dentures. This is why periodic adjustments, relines, or replacements may be necessary.

Regular visits with a denturist ensure your dentures remain comfortable and functional.

Choosing the Right Denture Clinic in Airdrie

While cost is an important factor, choosing the right denture clinic is equally important.

A reputable denture clinic should provide:

  • Thorough consultations
  • Personalized treatment planning
  • High-quality materials
  • Ongoing support and adjustments

Patients in Airdrie often look for clinics that combine experience, modern technology, and patient-focused care when selecting a denture provider.
