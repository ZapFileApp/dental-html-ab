Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my dentist wear a mask and gloves?

Your health is our main priority, and this extra precaution your dentist uses is to keep you safe from possible germs. The health of our patients and staff is important.

What do I do if I don’t understand what my dentist is talking about?

There may be things you are unclear about when it comes to the anatomy and treatments for your teeth and gums, but all you need to do is ask your dentist to explain the situation to you. They can clarify any detail you are confused about.

It has been a long time since I visited a dentist; What can I expect?

It’s ok if it has been a long time because we are always here to help. You likely just need a little extra care at the initial visit. This may come in the form of an extended teeth cleaning to remove tough plaque and tartar that may have formed over time. You will also likely need an X-ray so your dentist can examine tiny details to determine where the health of your teeth, gums, and jaw are at.

What are some of the things I can do to protect my teeth at home?

In addition to visiting the dentist twice a year, you can brush twice a day, floss daily, cut down on sugars, and visit your dentist right away if you suspect a problem.

How do I know if I have tooth decay?

Your dentist will check for problems during your appointments, but some symptoms of an impending cavity are pain when you bite down on things, sensitive teeth, a toothache, and discoloured teeth. It is important you contact your dentist if you notice any of these warning signs so they can help.

How long will my very first dental appointment take?

This will vary depending on your situation, but you can typically expect to spend an hour at the dentist’s office. This allows enough time for X-rays, a check-up, and discussing everything from questions you may have to the current standing of your oral health and how to care for it.
