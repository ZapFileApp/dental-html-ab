Dental Implants Calgary: What Is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a replacement tooth designed to mimic the whole natural tooth structure. We insert a titanium “root” into your jaw’s bone that supports the dental crown, bridge, or denture. Dental implants will look, act, and feel like natural teeth and last a lifetime if cared for properly. Dental Implants will help you to:

  • Replace missing teeth.
  • Protect facial structure.
  • Enable natural speech.
  • Stop shifting in its tracks.
  • Prevent bone density loss.
  • Sturdiest tooth replacement.
  • Support a healthy bite and jawbone.

That means we will replace not only your tooth but also the tooth root. That way, your implants will be created specifically for you and feel as reliable as natural teeth. However, your dental implants  Calgary will affect more than just your oral health. You will also gain your quality of life back. Improving your smile will impact every aspect of your life.

Why Get Dental Implants Calgary

If you’re missing any number of teeth, implants are an ideal solution to help restore your visible smile and bolster your total oral health and facial structure. Here are some reasons to consider implants over other methods of tooth replacement. Dental implants Calgary offer a long-lasting, natural-looking solution for replacing missing teeth.

They Last a Long Time:

A dental bridge lasts only around ten years, but your implants will likely last you two to three times as long. Because the dental implant procedure involves using titanium that integrates with your jawbone, your implants will act like natural teeth. However, rest assured that all implants are non-toxic and will not be rejected by your body.

They Act Like Natural Teeth:

This is one of the very best benefits of dental implants. Most people who get them cannot tell the difference between their natural teeth and the dental implant. Dental implants Calgary have full chewing power and can eat, brush, and flow normally.

They Prevent Further Dental Issues:

When you are missing a tooth, you run the risk of other dental problems like:

  • Bone loss.
  • Gum disease.
  • Facial sagging.
  • Further tooth loss.

If you do not get an implant within the first year of losing a tooth, that bone area loses 25% of its volume. And this continues year after year. You also risk the adjacent teeth shifting towards the gap, pulling your teeth out of position and affecting your bite, ability to chew, and appearance. A missing tooth is a magnet and trap for food and bacteria, leading to gum disease.

Are You a Good Candidate for Dental Implants?

Most anyone is a good candidate for implants. The best candidates will have:

  • Healthy gums.
  • Mature and healthy jaw bone.
  • Generally good oral health.
  • At least one missing tooth.
 

If you have lost bone in your jaw, that doesn’t mean you’re not a candidate.

 Implants may be the best way to prevent further bone loss. Dental implants Calgary have a very high success rate for patients. We will pick the best option for you if you are considering implants. That way, you can rest assured that our dentists have your back through every step of the process.

Dental implants offer a long-term solution for missing teeth for lasting benefits and durability. When considering how many dental implants are, understanding their longevity and durability is fundamental, ensuring a reliable choice for oral health needs. Here are some key points to highlight:

Dental Implant Procedure and Aftercare

During the surgical process of dental implant placement, multiple stages are involved to ensure successful integration with the bone and proper healing of bone graft. The outpatient surgery is performed in stages, with each step vital for the overall success of the implant. After the titanium post is initially placed into the jawbone, a healing period is necessary before proceeding to the next stage of implant therapy. This healing time allows the bone to bond with the implant, providing a solid foundation for the artificial tooth. Stitches, if used, typically dissolve on their own within a week or two after the implant procedure.

During the healing phase, it is recommended to stick to soft foods to avoid putting unnecessary pressure on the dental implant procedure site. Remember to diligently follow the post-surgery care instructions your dental care team provided for the best healing and successful dental implant integration.

Dental Implant Maintenance

Proper dental implant care and maintenance are essential for ensuring the longevity and health of your implant. To keep your dental implant in the best condition, follow these essential maintenance tips:

  • Brush and Floss Daily: Like natural teeth, dental implants in Calgary require regular brushing and flossing to prevent plaque buildup and gum disease.
  • Attend Regular Dental Check-ups: Scheduled dental visits help monitor the health of your implant and address any issues early on.
  • Avoid Harmful Habits: Refrain from smoking or chewing on complex objects that can damage the implant or surrounding tissues.
  • Use Recommended Oral Care Products: Consult your dentist for the most suitable products to maintain your dental implementation.
