Common Causes of Tooth Discoloration

Understanding what causes tooth discoloration can help you maintain your whitening results longer.

External Staining

  • Coffee, tea, red wine, and cola beverages

  • Tobacco use, including smoking and chewing tobacco
  • Deeply pigmented foods like berries and sauces
  • Poor oral hygiene allowing plaque and tartar buildup

Internal Discoloration

  • Aging, as dentin naturally yellows over time
  • Medications such as tetracycline antibiotics
  • Trauma to teeth causing internal bleeding
  • Excessive fluoride exposure during tooth development

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our services.

How does professional teeth whitening work?

Professional teeth whitening at Roy & Kali Family Dental Centre uses high-concentration whitening agents that are more effective than over-the-counter products. Our in-office treatment involves applying a professional-grade hydrogen peroxide gel to your teeth, which is then activated using a special light or laser. This process breaks down stains and discoloration, resulting in noticeably whiter teeth in just one visit near you in Grande Prairie.

Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, professional teeth whitening is safe when performed by our trained dental professionals. Before any whitening treatment, we conduct a thorough examination to ensure your teeth and gums are healthy. The whitening agents we use are specifically formulated to minimize sensitivity while maximizing results. Some patients may experience temporary sensitivity during or after treatment, but this typically resolves within a day or two.

How long do teeth whitening results last?

The longevity of teeth whitening results varies depending on your lifestyle habits and oral hygiene practices. Typically, professional whitening results can last from 6 months to 2 years. Factors that can affect how long your results last include consumption of staining foods and beverages (like coffee, tea, red wine), smoking, and oral hygiene. We provide maintenance recommendations and take-home touch-up kits to help maintain your bright smile for as long as possible near you in Grande Prairie.

What's the difference between in-office and take-home whitening?

In-office whitening provides immediate results in a single appointment, typically achieving teeth that are 3-8 shades whiter in just 60-90 minutes. Take-home whitening involves custom-fitted trays and professional-grade whitening gel that you apply at home for 30-60 minutes daily over 1-2 weeks. While take-home whitening is more gradual, it allows for ongoing maintenance and can be more budget-friendly. At Roy & Kali Family Dental Centre, we often recommend a combination of both methods for optimal, long-lasting results.

Will teeth whitening work on all dental restorations?

Teeth whitening treatments will not change the color of dental restorations such as crowns, veneers, fillings, or bonds. If you have restorations that are visible when you smile, we may recommend whitening your natural teeth first, then replacing or adjusting any restorations to match your new tooth color. During your consultation at our Grande Prairie dental clinic, we'll discuss the best approach based on your specific situation.

How do I maintain my whitened teeth?

To maintain your whitened teeth, we recommend:

  1. Avoiding or limiting staining foods and beverages for the first 48 hours after treatment,
  2. Using a straw when drinking potentially staining beverages,
  3. Brushing and flossing regularly,
  4. Using a whitening toothpaste approved by the Canadian Dental Association,
  5. Scheduling regular dental cleanings,
  6. Using your take-home whitening kit for periodic touch-ups. Our team will provide you with detailed care instructions after your whitening treatment.
