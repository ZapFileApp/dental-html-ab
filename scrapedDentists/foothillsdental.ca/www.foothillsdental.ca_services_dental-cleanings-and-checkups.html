What are Dental Cleanings and Checkups?

Dental cleanings and checkups are routine appointments that play a vital part in maintaining oral health. A dental cleaning takes care of the harder plaque and tartar that collect on your teeth over time and can’t be taken off with regular brushing. A cleaning ends with polishing to remove stains and smooth the surface. This process helps prevent cavities, gum disease, and other dental issues.

A checkup is a comprehensive examination of your mouth, teeth, and gums. It allows the dentist to identify potential problems and provide early intervention.

If you are looking for a dentist in NW Calgary, these routine visits are essential for maintaining a healthy and confident smile.

Issues Addressed by Dental Cleanings and Checkups

  • Plaque and Tartar Buildup – Plaque can build up in hard-to-reach areas even if you’re regularly brushing and flossing. If not removed, it hardens into tartar, leading to cavities and gum disease.
  • Cavities – Early detection of cavities allows for less invasive treatments.
  • Gum Disease – Regular cleanings remove harmful bacteria and plaque below the gumline to help prevent gingivitis and periodontal disease.
  • Oral Cancer – Dentists screen for signs of oral cancer during checkups, increasing the chances of early detection and successful treatment.
  • Tooth Wear and Damage – Dentists can identify signs of tooth grinding, enamel erosion, and other issues that may require intervention.

Benefits of Regular Dental Cleanings and Checkups

  • Prevention of Dental Issues – Routine cleanings and checkups can prevent common dental problems, saving you from more extensive and costly treatments in the future.
  • Improved Oral Hygiene – Professional cleanings help maintain optimal oral hygiene by thoroughly cleaning areas difficult to reach with regular brushing and flossing.
  • Fresher Breath – Removing plaque and tartar buildup reduces the bacteria that cause bad breath.
  • Brighter Smile – Polishing removes surface stains, giving you a brighter, more attractive smile.
  • Overall Health – Oral issues can affect overall health. Issues like gum disease have been connected to non-oral conditions such as heart disease and diabetes.
