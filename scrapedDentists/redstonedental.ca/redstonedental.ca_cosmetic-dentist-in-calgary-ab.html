What Is Cosmetic Dentistry

Cosmetic dentistry is not a single procedure but a thoughtful plan to make your smile look its best while keeping teeth and gums healthy. At our Calgary, AB, clinic, we start with photos, a shade check, and a short conversation about your goals. Then we design a sequence that may include cosmetic dentistry procedures, like Whitening, Bonding, Porcelain Veneers, Tooth-Colored Restorations, Crowns, alignment with Invisalign or Traditional Braces, and smile framing with Gum Contouring or periodontal care when indicated. To organise your preferences, try our guided Cosmetic Smile Assessment.

Why Consider Cosmetic Dentist

A coordinated plan can solve multiple concerns at once. Whitening improves color. Bonding polishes edges and closes small gaps. Veneers shape and brighten together. Invisalign aligns teeth so other work stays minimal. When planned well, these treatments complement each other for a believable, confident smile that fits your face, lip line, and bite.

Who Is A Candidate For Cosmetic Dentistry

Most healthy teens and adults qualify. We focus on the least invasive options that meet your goals. Good candidates have the following features:

  • Healthy gums with no active inflammation
  • Stains that resist whitening alone
  • Chips, uneven edges, or small gaps in front teeth
  • Old fillings or crowns that no longer match
  • Minor crowding or rotations that show in photos

When we plan preliminary steps first or consider alternatives

  • Active gum disease treated with Deep Cleaning and our Gum Disease plan
  • Heavily cracked or root canal teeth protected with a Crown
  • Missing teeth replaced with Dental Implants, All on 4, Bridges, or Dentures
  • Strong dental anxiety supported by options on our Sedation Dentistry page

Cosmetic Services We Offer At Redstone Family Dental In Calgary, AB

A brief overview of the most common options we combine for smooth, natural results.

Professional Teeth Whitening

Brighten the baseline shade of your smile with in-office or custom take-home trays on our Whitening page. Whitening first often lets us plan thinner veneers or smaller bonding for a conservative finish.

Dental Bonding For Small Fixes

In a single visit, Bonding repairs chips, evens edges, and closes small gaps using tooth-colored composite shaped directly on the tooth.

Porcelain Veneers For Shape And Symmetry

When shape, length, or deeper color changes are the goal, Porcelain Veneers deliver lifelike translucency and stain resistance with minimal tooth reduction.

Invisalign Or Braces For Alignment

Invisalign and Traditional Braces align teeth to reduce how much tooth structure is altered for cosmetic work. We often align first, then finish with small bonding touch-ups.

Tooth-Colored Crowns For Strength And Esthetics

When a tooth is cracked or has a large failing filling, tooth-colored Crowns restore strength and match neighboring teeth. See our focus on Metal-Free Dentistry.

Bridges, Dentures, And Implants To Fill Gaps

Missing teeth change how a smile looks and functions. We plan esthetic replacements with Bridges, removable Dentures, or stand-alone Implants based on your goals.

Gum Contouring For A Balanced Frame

If teeth look short due to excess gum coverage, careful reshaping creates even heights. Explore our dedicated page for gum contouring guidance.

Your Cosmetic Dentistry Process In Calgary

Predictable steps reduce stress and help you visualize the result.

Step 1: Consultation and records

We review your goals, take photos, and check gum health. If you are new to our clinic, see our New Patient Visit page for what to expect. We discuss budget and timing up front.

Step 2: Smile design and preview

Using your Cosmetic Smile Assessment, we map priorities. When helpful, we create a mock-up so you can preview length and shape before committing.

Step 3: Health first, then esthetics

We stabilize any gum inflammation with DeepCleaning and address urgent repairs. Strong foundations protect your investment.

Step 4: Sequence the esthetic steps

Once your oral health is stable, we carefully plan and sequence the esthetic treatments needed to improve your smile and achieve the desired results.

Step 5: Comfort options and efficiency

If anxiety is a barrier, explore options on our Sedation Dentistry page. We can combine several treatments in a single visit when appropriate.

Step 6: Protect and maintain

We confirm your bite, provide a night guard if you clench, and set a maintenance schedule so your smile stays bright and stable.

Aftercare And Long-Term Maintenance

  • Brush twice daily with a soft brush and non-abrasive toothpaste
  • Clean between teeth daily with floss or interdental brushes
  • Rinse with water after dark beverages to reduce surface staining
  • Wear your night guard if recommended
  • Keep a routine cleaning rhythm: 6 months for most, 3 to 4 months for higher risk
  • Plan quick bonding polishes or whitening touch-ups as needed

FAQs

Will my smile look natural or too bright

We match shade, translucency, and texture to your features. Many patients choose a brighter but believable tone. Mock-ups and shade checks help you visualize before anything is permanent.

How long will treatment take

Simple plans like whitening and bonding can finish in one or two visits. Plans with Invisalign or multiple veneers take longer. We build a timeline with clear milestones and photo check-ins.

Do veneers damage my teeth

Modern veneers are designed with minimal shaping. We preserve as much enamel as possible for strong bonding. Alternatives like bonding or alignment are discussed if they will meet your goals with even less change.

Will whitening change the color of my crowns or fillings

Whitening lightens natural enamel only. If restorations become a shade mismatch, we can update them to the new color for a seamless look.

What if I have sensitive teeth

We customize gel strength and wear time for whitening, and we can stage care to reduce sensitivity. For longer visits, sedation options on our Sedation Dentistry page help anxious or sensitive patients relax.

Is cosmetic dentistry only about looks

A well-planned case improves function and comfort too. Smoother edges reduce chipping risk, aligned teeth are easier to clean, and properly contoured restorations support a stable bite.

How long do results last

Longevity depends on daily habits, bite forces, and maintenance. With routine care and night guard protection if you clench, veneers, crowns, and bonding can look great for years. Whitening touch-ups are simple with custom trays.
