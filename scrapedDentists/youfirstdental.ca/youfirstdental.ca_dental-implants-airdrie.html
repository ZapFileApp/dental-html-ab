Considering Dental Implants in Airdrie to Replace Missing Teeth?

Dental implants, a cutting-edge solution available in Airdrie

for missing teeth, can be an excellent alternative for filling in those unsightly gaps. Their lifespan, akin to dental veneers, is influenced by several factors, including dental hygiene, the quality of care, and the type of dental implant materials used.

Understanding the longevity of a dental implant involves getting to know its three crucial components: 1. the implant (typically a titanium or Zirconia dental implant), 2. an abutment (a sturdy metal post that links the replacement tooth to the implant), and 3. the crown (made of porcelain resin or metal, acting as your new tooth).

With optimal care, regular dental check-ups in Airdrie, and robust oral hygiene, the dental implant can last a lifetime. The crown, with careful maintenance and regular dental implants check-ups in Airdrie, can last for 10–15 years or more.

The dental implants care routine includes:

  • Brushing twice a day for at least 2 minutes with fluoride toothpaste
  • Flossing once daily
  • Regular professional cleanings every six months in Airdrie

It also means avoiding the habit of using your teeth as tools and finding solutions to manage teeth grinding, a common stress response that can potentially harm your implants.

Are Dental Implants in Airdrie Right for You?

If you have a missing tooth and are pondering a suitable replacement, dental implants in Airdrie might be the perfect solution for you. Functioning as an anchor, they hold the replacement tooth in the correct place, offering an alternative to partial or complete dentures or dental bridges.

Are you an Ideal Candidate for Dental Implants in Airdrie?

Good general health, healthy gums, and sufficient bone in your jaw to accommodate an implant make you an ideal candidate for dental implants in Airdrie. If your jawbone is inadequately developed or has reduced in size, a bone graft for dental implants might be a suitable solution. We can discuss the pros and cons of different procedures and recommend the most suitable one based on your circumstances. Remember, dental implants are an alternative to traditional dentures, not a replacement.

The Procedure for Dental Implants in Airdrie

We follow a comprehensive process at our clinic in Airdrie. We first conduct a detailed examination of your mouth, including X-rays, to determine your candidacy for dental implants. The dental implants procedure involves fitting an implant beneath your gum tissue and into your jawbone. As the healing process occurs, the implant starts bonding with the surrounding bone and gum, a process known as osseointegration, which can take several months.

Once the gum tissue heals completely, we attach an abutment to the dental implant, which links the new tooth to the implant. We then affix the replacement tooth, made of artificial material, to the abutment.

For multiple teeth replacement, a dental implant can securely hold a fixed dental bridge or crown. This dental restoration replaces one or more lost teeth and is held in place by dental implants on either side of the missing tooth or teeth.
