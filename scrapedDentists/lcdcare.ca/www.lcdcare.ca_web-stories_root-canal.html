What Is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment removes infected tissue from inside the tooth. The pulp chamber contains nerves and blood vessels, and when damaged, it causes pain. This procedure cleans and seals the tooth, eliminating the source of discomfort.

Causes of Tooth  Pulp Damage

Tooth decay, fractures, trauma, or repeated dental work can allow bacteria to reach the tooth's pulp, leading to infection, swelling, and intense pain. A root canal prevents further damage while keeping your tooth intact.

The Root Canal Process

The dentist numbs your mouth, opens the pulp chamber, removes infected tissue, and cleans the root canals. The tooth is sealed with a temporary restoration, which will be followed by a permanent filling or crown in a future visit.

Recovery After a Root Canal

Most patients recover quickly from root canal therapy! With proper care, you can return to normal activities the next day. Mild discomfort can be managed with over-the-counter pain relief. Follow-up care ensures lasting success.
