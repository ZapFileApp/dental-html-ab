    TREATMENT

    When Should a Child First See a Dentist?

     

    The Canadian Dental Association recommends that a child's first dental visit should occur within six months after the first tooth appears, but no later than the child's first birthday. This early introduction helps kids get comfortable with the dentist and allows any potential issues to be spotted and addressed before they become serious problems.

    ​

    What Happens During a Pediatric Dental Visit?

    ​

    During your child's dental visit, the dentist will examine their teeth and gums, looking for signs of decay or other problems. They'll also clean your child's teeth and provide advice on daily dental care, diet, fluoride use, and other factors that can affect oral health.

    ​

    As your child grows older, their dental visits will start to resemble your own, with regular cleanings and checks for cavities. Regular dental visits allow the dentist to monitor your child's oral health and development, intervening promptly if problems arise.

    ​

    Why are baby teeth important?

    ​

    Baby teeth, also known as primary teeth, may be temporary, but they're extremely important for your child's health and development. Here's why:

    ​

    1. Speech and Eating: Baby teeth are essential for speaking clearly and eating properly. Children with decayed or missing teeth may have difficulty pronouncing certain words and may struggle to eat certain foods, which could affect their nutrition and growth.

    2. Space Holders: Baby teeth hold space in the jaw for permanent teeth. If a baby tooth is lost too early, adjacent teeth could drift into the empty space, making it difficult for permanent teeth to find room when they come in.

    3. Prevents Cavities: Taking care of baby teeth helps prevent cavities. Tooth decay in baby teeth can lead to infections and abscesses that could harm the permanent teeth developing beneath them.

     

    In conclusion, taking care of your child's baby teeth is crucial for their oral and overall health. By teaching your child good oral hygiene habits at an early age, you're setting them up for a lifetime of healthy smiles.
