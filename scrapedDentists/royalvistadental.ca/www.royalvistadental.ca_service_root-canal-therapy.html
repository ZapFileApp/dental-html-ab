Root Canal Therapy in NW Calgary, AB

Root Canal Treatment Near You

Root canal therapy is misunderstood by many patients. However, this procedure is straightforward and it involves the removal of the soft, abscessed tissue in your damaged tooth, known as the pulp. During root canal therapy, the portion of the tooth where the damaged pulp resided will be cleaned, disinfected and sealed with a filling for protection against future damage. Our clinic is happy to offer root canal treatment near you.

Signs Root Canal Therapy is Required

Inflammation or infection is the result of a diseased tooth pulp. The following signs and symptoms can help patients pinpoint when root canal therapy near you is required:

  • Severe tooth pain when pressure is applied or when chewing.
  • Tooth discoloration (a darkened appearance).
  • Swollen or tender gums.
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot and cold temperatures.

Our dentists provide root canal therapy in NW Calgary to eliminate the risk of infection spreading and causing bone loss and tooth loss.

Note, in some cases, there may be no symptoms present which is why maintaining dental check-ups are essential.

Causes of Root Canal Therapy

When the pulp becomes inflamed or infected, root canal therapy will need to be performed by your NW Calgary dentist. There are several reasons why this can occur, these include:

  • Cavities or deep tooth decay.
  • An injury to the mouth.
  • Repeated dental procedures on the tooth.
  • A crack or chip in the tooth that exposes the pulp.
  • Repetitive trauma due to jaw misalignment.
  • Teeth grinding or clenching (bruxism).

The Process of Receiving Root Canal Therapy

Interested in receiving root canal treatment near you? Firstly, your dentist in NW Calgary, AB will perform a comprehensive exam for a deeper look at the state of the tooth. A local anesthetic is then administered to numb the tooth and surrounding area and a rubber dam is placed on the tooth to keep it free from saliva during the procedure.

Next, an opening is made at the top of the tooth and fine dental instruments (canal files) are used to remove the pulp from the root canals. Once the canal has been cleaned, a filling is placed to seal it. A dental crown is then placed onto the tooth to protect it and restore its full function.

FAQ’s

Is root canal treatment painful?

No, it is usually painless due to local anesthesia and helps relieve pain.

How many visits are required?

It may take one or two visits depending on the condition of the tooth.

How long does the procedure take?

Most treatments take about 60–90 minutes per visit.

What happens during a root canal?

The dentist removes infection, cleans the canals, and seals the tooth.
