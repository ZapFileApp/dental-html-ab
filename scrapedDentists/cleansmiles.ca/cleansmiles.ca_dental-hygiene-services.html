Guided Biofilm Therapy - GBT

Guided Biofilm Therapy (GBT) is an advanced, minimally invasive dental hygiene treatment designed to gently remove biofilm, plaque, and stains for improved oral health and comfort.
Our holistic, airway-centric approach supports healthier gums, teeth, and overall wellness while providing a more comfortable dental cleaning experience.

Dental and Periodontal Exams

We perform comprehensive dental hygiene examinations to assess your overall oral health and detect concerns such as tooth decay, gingivitis, periodontitis, and other underlying oral health issues as early as possible.
Our experienced dental hygienists also provide advanced periodontal examinations to carefully evaluate the health of your gums and the supporting bone structure around your teeth.

Read more

Comprehensive Intraoral & Extraoral Screening

Our registered dental hygienists provide comprehensive intraoral and extraoral screenings using digital X-rays and intraoral cameras to help detect tooth decay, gum disease, periodontal concerns, and other oral health issues early.
We also perform oral cancer screenings as part of your preventive dental care.

Periodontal Maintenance

For patients with active or arrested periodontal disease, our practitioners recommend a customized plan to have periodontal maintenance to maintain the gums and surrounding bone health. 

At Clean Smiles Dental, our dentist or dental hygienist in Edmonton perform the procedure thoroughly to remove biofilm, calculus (tartar buildup) and plaque from above and below the gumline.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What services are included in dental hygiene care?

 
At Clean Smiles Dental Clinic, dental hygiene care includes cleaning, exams, and preventive treatments to maintain oral health.

2. What is Airflow dental cleaning?

 
Airflow dental cleaning is a modern method that removes plaque and stains using air, water, and fine powder for a gentle experience.

3. Do you offer dental cleaning in Edmonton?

 
Yes, Clean Smiles Dental Clinic provides dental cleaning Edmonton patients trust for comfort and effectiveness.

4. Is Airflow cleaning better than traditional cleaning?

 
Dental cleaning in Edmonton patients prefer is gentler, faster, and more effective for removing stains and biofilm.

5. How often should I get dental cleaning?

 
Most patients should visit Clean Smiles Dental Clinic every 6 months for routine hygiene care. 

6. Is dental cleaning safe?

 
Yes, dental cleaning Edmonton clinics offer is safe for sensitive teeth, gums, and dental work.

7. Does Airflow cleaning remove stains?

 
Dental cleaning is highly effective at removing surface stains caused by coffee, tea, and smoking.

8. Can Airflow cleaning help prevent gum disease?

 
Yes, Airflow dental cleaning Edmonton services help remove harmful bacteria that cause gum disease.

9. Is dental hygiene treatment painful?
