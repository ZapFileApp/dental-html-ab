Custom Night Guards

If you are looking to protect your teeth during sleep, custom mouth guards offer unparalleled comfort and protection. These specialized dental appliances are meticulously crafted to fit your unique bite, providing a snug and secure fit that ensures maximum benefit.

One of the main advantages of opting for a custom mouth guard is its ability to alleviate the symptoms associated with teeth grinding and clenching or bruxism. Teeth grinding, also known as bruxism, is a common condition that affects many individuals.

It can lead to various dental issues such as tooth wear, jaw pain, chronic headaches, and even tooth fractures. Wearing a custom night guard during sleep provides the teeth with a cushioning barrier that absorbs the force of grinding teeth and prevents damage.

Moreover, custom night guards offer superior comfort compared to generic store-bought options. When you acquire a custom mouthpiece from your dentist, they will take precise impressions of your teeth and use them to create a mold for your personalized night guard.

This ensures an accurate custom fit that conforms perfectly to the contours of your mouth. With its customized design, you won’t have to worry about discomfort or the mouth guard slipping out of place during sleep.

Night Guard Benefits

Night guards, also known as dental night mouth guard, offer numerous benefits to those who wear them. 

Prevent tooth damage caused by bruxism

One important benefit of wearing a mouth guard at night is the protection it provides for the teeth. Teeth grinding and clenching put excessive force on the teeth, leading to enamel erosion, tooth sensitivity, and even tooth fractures. Mouth guards act as a cushion between the upper and lower teeth, absorbing the pressure generated during bruxism episodes. This helps to distribute the force evenly and protect the teeth from unnecessary wear and tear.

Alleviate symptoms associated with bruxism

  • Headaches
  • Jaw Pain/Facial Pain
  • Facial Tension 
  • Earaches

     

By providing a barrier between the upper and lower jaw, these devices help relax facial muscles and reduce strain on the temporomandibular joint (TMJ). This can be particularly beneficial for individuals who suffer from TMJ disorders or experience frequent jaw pain.

Improved sleep quality 

Since sleep bruxism can disturb sleep patterns by causing frequent awakenings or arousals throughout the night without conscious awareness, using a properly fitted mouth guard can minimize these interruptions, thereby promoting better restful sleep. Knowing whether your mouth guard fits properly is essential for maximizing its benefits.

Night Guards

Night guards, also known as dental night guards or teeth protectors, are oral devices designed to prevent damage caused by teeth grinding or clenching of the teeth during sleep.

These custom-made appliances provide a protective barrier between the upper and lower teeth, reducing the impact and friction that can lead to tooth wear, fractures, and other dental problems. Made from durable materials such as thermoplastic, night guards offer a comfortable fit while ensuring optimal protection for your teeth.

One common concern among individuals considering the use of night guards is how to determine if the appliance fits properly. A well-fitted night guard should feel comfortable and snug against your teeth.

It should not be loose or tight but fit securely without causing any pain or discomfort. If you experience soreness in your jaw or notice any shifting of the guard while wearing it, it may indicate an improper fit.

In such cases, it is recommended to consult with a dentist or dental professional who can assess your situation and make necessary adjustments. 

By creating a cushioning effect between upper and lower teeth surfaces during sleep, they reduce wear on tooth enamel and minimize potential damage caused by teeth grinding forces. Additionally, wearing a night guard can promote better sleep quality as it prevents disruptive teeth grinding noises that may disturb both you and your sleeping partner.

Customized Night Guards

When it comes to protecting your teeth, a one-size-fits-all approach simply won’t be the most effective. That’s where a customized night guard come into play.

These personalized dental appliances provide superior protection and comfort compared to their generic counterparts. One of the primary concerns when it comes to night guards is ensuring a proper fit.

Ill-fitting mouth guards not only compromise their effectiveness but can also lead to discomfort and even exacerbate existing dental issues. With a custom night guard, you can rest assured knowing that your appliance has been tailored specifically to fit your unique dental anatomy.

To create custom mouth guards, using an impression kit, your dentist will first take an impression of your teeth using dental putty. This impression is then used as a mold to fabricate a thermoplastic night guard or mouthpiece that perfectly conforms to the contours of your teeth.

The result is a snug yet comfortable fit that ensures maximum protection from teeth grinding or clenching during sleep or impact during sports activities. The benefits of investing in custom night guards and mouth sport guards extend beyond just proper fitting.

These personalized appliances offer enhanced durability compared to over-the-counter options, often lasting several years with proper care and maintenance. Additionally, they are designed in such a way that they do not interfere with breathing or speaking, ensuring optimal comfort while wearing them

Do you need both an upper and lower night guard to effectively manage bruxism?

You don’t necessarily need both an upper and lower night guard to help with bruxism. Most people choose a night guard for their upper teeth, as it’s generally more comfortable and effective at protecting against grinding. However, the choice between an upper or lower teeth night guard can depend on personal comfort and the specific alignment of your teeth.

A night guard for your upper teeth will typically cover the entire arch, including your front teeth, which are often most vulnerable to damage from teeth grinding. Your dentist can recommend the best option based on your individual needs and oral health.

Material Of Mouth Guards

When it comes to mouth guards, the material used plays a crucial role in their effectiveness and comfort. Mouth guards are typically made of thermoplastic materials that are flexible and moldable when heated. This allows for a customized fit that conforms to the shape of your teeth and gums, providing optimal protection while you sleep.

Ethylene Vinyl Acetate (EVA)

The most common thermoplastic material used for night guards is ethylene vinyl acetate (EVA), which is known for its durability and ability to withstand teeth grinding and clenching forces. Mouth guards designed specifically for sports are also made from various materials, each with its own unique characteristics.

Polyethylene

One common material used is polyethylene, which offers excellent shock absorption properties, protecting your teeth from impact during contact sports. 

Silicone

Another popular option is silicone, which provides a comfortable fit and optimal cushioning, reducing the risk of dental injuries.

The choice of material for both night guards and mouth guards depends on several factors such as personal preference, level of protection required, budget considerations, and any specific dental conditions or concerns. Custom mouth guards fabricated by dentists often utilize high-quality materials that offer superior protection compared to over-the-counter options.

This ensures maximum comfort during wear while also providing adequate protection against teeth grinding (bruxism) or potential injuries during physical activities. Dentists can assess the alignment of your bite and adjust the night guard accordingly to ensure proper occlusion between your upper and lower teeth.

Additionally, they will provide guidance on how to maintain the hygiene of the night guard or mouth guard and offer advice on what signs to look for to determine if the device fits properly. The choice of material for mouth guards greatly impacts their effectiveness and comfort.

Whether it’s a custom mouth guard from a dentist or a mouth guard designed specifically for sports, selecting high-quality materials ensures durability, optimal protection, and long-lasting benefits. Consulting with your dentist will help ensure that your mouth guard fits properly, allowing you to enjoy the benefits of these protective devices without compromising your oral health or comfort.

Types Of Night Guards

There are different types of custom fitted night guards available to cater to the specific needs and preferences of individuals.

Custom Night Guard

This is professionally made by a dentist to fit the unique contours of an individual’s mouth. Custom mouth guards offer a superior fit and comfort compared to over-the-counter options. Custom made guards make all the difference by providing better protection against teeth grinding (also known as bruxism) and jaw clenching during sleep. it is likely that custom fitted night guards are a more thicker night guard than some depending on the severity of your teeth grinding clenching is occurring.

Thermoplastic Night Guard

This type of guard is made from a flexible material that softens when heated and can be molded to fit snugly over the teeth. The thermoplastic material allows for some customization by adjusting the shape and thickness of the guard to achieve optimal comfort and effectiveness.

Thermoplastic night guards are more affordable compared to custom-made ones from a dentist, but they may not provide as precise of a fit. It is important to ensure that your night guard fits properly in order for it to be effective in protecting your teeth and alleviating symptoms associated with bruxism or jaw clenching.

Whether you opt for a custom mouth guard from a dentist or a thermoplastic night guard, it is crucial to ensure proper fit and comfort. Investing in a high-quality night guard can provide valuable protection for your teeth, alleviate symptoms associated with bruxism or jaw clenching, and ultimately contribute to your overall oral health.

Night Guard Maintenance

Proper Night guard maintenance is essential to ensure its longevity and effectiveness. By following a few simple steps, you can keep your teeth protector clean and in good condition for years to come.

  •  It is important to clean your custom night guard from the dentist regularly. After each use, rinse it thoroughly with cool water to remove any saliva or debris.

  • Avoid using hot water as it may warp the thermoplastic material of the night guard. Use a soft toothbrush with mild soap or non-alcoholic mouthwash to gently brush the inner and outer surfaces of the guard. This will help remove any bacteria and plaque buildup that may accumulate over time.

  • Proper storage of your tooth guard for TMJ is crucial. Avoid leaving it out in the open, where it can be exposed to dust or bacteria. Instead, store your night guard in a ventilated container specifically designed for this purpose. Be sure to keep it away from direct sunlight or heat sources, as this can cause distortion or warping of the material. 

  • Understanding how do you know if your night guard fits properly is vital for optimal maintenance and performance. Monitor the fit of your night guard to make sure it is working properly, and visit a dentist right away if it is no longer fitting.

     

By investing in proper maintenance practices such as regular cleaning and appropriate storage, you can prolong the lifespan and effectiveness of your custom mouth guard. Remember that maintaining good oral hygiene alongside using a mouth guard can provide added benefits such as reducing tooth decay risks and preventing gum disease.

While dental night guards are undoubtedly an investment, their long-term cost-effectiveness outweighs any initial expenses incurred. Taking care of your mouth guard is essential for its longevity and effectiveness.

FAQ'S

How Do I Know If My Night Guard Fits Properly?

One of the most important aspects of using a night guard is ensuring that it fits properly. A well-fitting night guard not only provides the necessary protection but also ensures maximum comfort during sleep. So, how do you know if your night guard fits properly?

There are a few indicators to look for. 

  • The fit should be snug yet comfortable. When you insert your custom night guard from the dentist, it should fit securely over your teeth without any excessive pressure or discomfort.
  • It should cover all surfaces of your teeth and sit firmly in place without any loose or shifting movements. If you find that your night guard feels too tight or loose, it may be necessary to get it adjusted by your dentist to ensure optimal fit. 
  • Another key factor to consider is bite alignment. When you wear your mouth guard, your teeth should fit together naturally and comfortably in their usual bite position. If you experience any noticeable changes in how your teeth meet when wearing the night guard, such as an altered bite or uncomfortable pressure points, this could indicate an improper fit. In such cases, consulting with a dental professional is recommended to assess the issue and make any necessary adjustments.

Ensuring that your custom mouth guard fits properly is essential for its effectiveness in protecting against tooth damage and alleviating various dental concerns. By paying attention to comfort levels, bite alignment and seeking professional assistance if needed, you can enjoy all the benefits that come with using a well-fitted night guard.

how much does a custom night guard cost?

Custom night guards typically cost between $300 and $800, depending on factors such as the complexity of the design, the materials used, and your location. Custom night guards are more expensive than over-the-counter options because they are specifically molded to fit your teeth, offering better protection and comfort for teeth grinding and clenching. Your dentist can provide a more accurate cost estimate based on your specific needs.

It’s also worth noting that if you have sleep apnea, your dentist may recommend a different type of appliance, as night guards can sometimes interfere with sleep apnea treatments. Always consult your dentist to find the best solution and perfect night guard for your needs and oral health.

is a sleep apnea night guard different from a regular night guard?

Yes, a sleep apnea night guard is different from a regular night guard. While both are custom-fitted dental devices, they serve different purposes.

  • Regular Night Guard: A regular night guard, also known as a dental or occlusal guard, is primarily designed to protect your teeth from grinding and clenching (bruxism) during sleep. It acts as a barrier between your upper and lower teeth to prevent wear and tear.

  • Sleep Apnea Night Guard: A sleep apnea night guard, often referred to as a mandibular advancement device (MAD), is specifically designed to treat mild to moderate obstructive sleep apnea. This device works by repositioning the lower jaw and tongue slightly forward to help keep the airway open during sleep, reducing the occurrence of breathing interruptions.

So, while a regular night guard focuses on protecting teeth from grinding, a sleep apnea night guard is designed to address airway issues and improve breathing during sleep.
