How Often Should I Get a Dental Checkup?

Every patient is unique. Our dentist in Calgary typically recommend that adult patients visit a dentist once or twice a year, even if your mouth seems like it’s in good condition. This way, you can get your teeth professionally cleaned on a regular basis and maintain excellent oral health.

What is the most common dental emergency?

The most common reasons our patients see us for are toothaches — that’s because pain is a way for your body to tell you that something isn’t right. Under the surface, there could be a lot going on. You could be dealing with a variety of dental issues such as decay, gum disease, cracked or worn teeth, or an abscess. We also often help those who have lost or broken a tooth. Before you start overthinking your dental emergency, book your next dental appointment with one of our family dentists today!

Will a toothache go away on its own?

If you’re feeling uncomfortable, you should always visit a dentist. Even if the pain does go away after a few days, it’s likely there’s an issue hidden under the surface that should be checked by a dental professional so that it doesn’t linger and continue getting worse. 

At What Age Should My Child First See a Dentist?

The Canadian Dental Association recommends the assessment of infants, by a dentist, within 6 months of the eruption of the first tooth or by one year of age. This helps one of our family-friendly dentists catch potential problems that can affect your child’s overall health and well-being as more teeth grow in overtime.
