How exactly does Invisalign work?

Throughout your treatment, you’ll receive a series of clear aligners. Each one makes small adjustments to your teeth, carefully planned by our doctors for your specific needs.

Wearing your Invisalign aligners.

You’ll need to wear each set of aligners for 22 hours a day. After about two weeks, you’ll switch to the next set, continuing the straightening process until your treatment is complete.

Also Designed For Young Children And Teens

We have Invisalign for Children and Teens which differs from the traditional Invisalign treatment plans in a few key ways:

Coloured Compliance tabs

Invisalign Teen aligners have a wear indicator that fades after two weeks, showing when it’s time to move to the next set. It also reminds teens to wear them for 22 hours daily.

When Kids Lose Invisalign Aligners

Invisalign for Teens includes 6 free replacement aligners, compared to the usual 2 with regular Invisalign, in case your child loses their trays.

New Teeth Eruption Tabs

Invisalign Teen aligners are designed with extra space to accommodate growing teeth. This ensures there’s room for any adult teeth that still need to emerge.

Frequently Asked Questions About Invisalign Treatment

Here are answers to some common questions about Invisalign at our Calgary dental office. 

Why choose us?

Over the last 11 years, we’ve helped thousands of patients smile with confidence. 

Is Invisalign Right for me?

For the majority of people, the answer is Yes. However, Invisalign is not a turnkey solution in that your dentist’s input into the design of the aligners can have drastic impacts to your results. As well, Invisalign must be worn a minimum of 22 hours a day to see the movements necessary.

How does Invisalign straighten your teeth?

The Invisalign® system uses a series of clear, removable aligners.  As you change to each new set of aligners in your series, your teeth gradually move until they reach the desired position. This process can take months to complete.

Why should I straighten my teeth?

Misaligned teeth can lead to airway issues, premature wear, tooth loss, and periodontal (gum) disease.  Properly aligned teeth reduce the risk of tooth chipping, breaking, and wear, which could cause pain and lead to expensive procedures in the future to save your teeth.  Proper alignment also reduces stress on the jawbone and TMJ.  

Do you provide financing options?

Yes! We offer flexible financing options such as 0% financing and extended payment plans. We will work with you to create the perfect payment plan.

Should I wait for my daughter/son’s adult teeth to come in before considering Orthodontics?

The Canadian Dental Association recommends every child have an orthodontic assessment no later than age 7. That’s because not all orthodontic problems can be treated if we were to wait for all adult teeth to be present. Waiting on treatment can lead to a less than ideal result. 

What is included as part of a free orthodontic consultation?

In an orthodontic consultation, you will get an opportunity to tour the office, meet the doctor and the team. The doctor will look at your mouth and assess the complexity of your treatment and review what treatment options are available to you. Together, we will discuss any concerns you may have with your orthodontic treatment.
