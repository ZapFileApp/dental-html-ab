Why You Might Need a Dental Guard or Appliance

Sometimes, brushing and flossing aren’t enough to protect your teeth from structural damage. Issues like teeth grinding (bruxism), jaw pain, or sleep apnea require specialized tools to manage.

Teeth Grinding:

This usually happens while you sleep. It can wear down your enamel and lead to tooth sensitivity.

Sports Protection:

A quick collision on the field can lead to chipped or lost teeth.

TMJ Disorders:

If your jaw clicks or hurts, a custom appliance can take the pressure off your joints.

Better Sleep:

Specialized mouthpieces can help keep your airway open, reducing snoring and mild sleep apnea.

FAQs

How long will my custom dental guard last?

With proper care and cleaning, a professional guard usually lasts several years. Its lifespan depends on how hard you grind your teeth or how physically active you are.

Is it hard to breathe with a mouthguard?

Not at all! Because we provide custom dental guards and appliances near you, they are slim and fit perfectly, allowing for natural breathing and easy speaking.

How do I clean my dental appliance?

Rinse it with cool water and clean it with a soft toothbrush and mild soap. Always store it in its protective case when not in use.

Can children use custom mouthguards?

Yes! Children are very active, and their teeth are still developing. Custom guards are the best way to protect their growing smiles during sports or nighttime grinding.

Will a night guard stop my jaw pain?

In many cases, yes. By preventing your teeth from clenching, the guard allows your jaw muscles to relax, which significantly reduces pain and morning tension headaches.

Do I need a guard if I only grind occasionally?

Even occasional grinding can cause micro-cracks in your enamel. It is better to be proactive and protect your teeth before permanent structural damage actually occurs.
