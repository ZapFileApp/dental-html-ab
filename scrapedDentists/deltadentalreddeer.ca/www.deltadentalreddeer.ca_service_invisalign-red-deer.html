Invisalign in Red Deer for a Straighter, Confident Smile

Invisalign Near You

We’re delighted to offer Invisalign® as a reliable method for teeth straightening at Delta Dental. If you’ve ever thought about enhancing your smile, you’ve likely thought about adjusting the position of your teeth. Whether you wish to close a few gaps or resolve some crowding, the evolution of dental techniques and technology makes achieving your ideal smile easier than ever!

In the past, straightening your teeth required the commitment to metal braces. While this option is still the best choice for severe or complicated cases of malocclusion (crooked teeth), they aren’t the sole option anymore. Clear aligners can address mild to moderate instances of crooked teeth, allowing you to realign your teeth without the necessity of visible metal and elastic bands.

Schedule your appointment at our dental practice to learn more about getting Invisalign® near you.

What is Invisalign®?

Invisalign® is a discreet and convenient method for straightening your smile. It adheres to the same concept as braces, applying sustained pressure over time to get teeth to shift, but utilizes custom-made clear aligners. These aligners, crafted from nearly invisible plastic material, are tailored to fit your individual teeth perfectly. They bear a resemblance to a night guard in terms of being snugly fitted over your teeth. When you begin your Invisalign® treatment, you’ll be provided with the full set of aligners to complete your journey.

Understanding the Process

The initial step involves meeting with a dentist near you so they can take a 3D snapshot of your mouth, which only takes a few minutes. This image becomes a reference for your dentist as they design your individualized treatment plan, as it reveals the current condition of your teeth and allows them to visualize the potential outcome. If both you and your dentist are content with the projected results, this information can be sent to an Invisalign® laboratory, where your personalized trays are crafted.

You will obtain around 20 to 30 aligners from the lab, which are utilized in a specific order. Instead of receiving orthodontic adjustments, Invisalign® applies minor, gradual changes to the aligner’s fit to accomplish alignment. As a result, your experience is more comfortable, with aligners being replaced every two to three weeks, or as frequently as your dentist advises.

Daily Usage of Invisalign®

Embarking on your Invisalign® journey may necessitate minor lifestyle adjustments, particularly regarding oral hygiene. As the success of your treatment depends on consistent pressure from the aligners, trays need to be worn every day, except during meals and tooth brushing.

Your objective should be to wear the aligners for at least 22 hours within a 24-hour timeframe. The aligners should be removed when eating and drinking and should only be reinserted once your mouth has been cleaned. Without brushing or rinsing after you eat or drink, residual food particles and sugars in your mouth will be tightly pressed against your enamel by the aligners, potentially encouraging decay and gum disease. While the aligners are in, only water should be consumed. Although this new routine might take some getting used to—particularly for habitual snackers—many patients observe an overall improvement in their health as they can no longer snack or ‘graze’ throughout the day.

Ideal Candidates for Invisalign®

Due to the level of responsibility and discipline required for maintaining the hygiene and proper use of Invisalign® aligners, it is generally not suggested for children or young adolescents who might risk enamel damage by failing to adhere to the hygiene guidelines. The aligners are thin and clear, making them prone to being misplaced if not properly stored in their case. Hence, it’s essential to handle them with care to avoid disrupting your treatment plan. If you happen to lose a set of aligners, you should revert to using the last pair you had until your dentist can guide you on the next steps.

For those experiencing issues with clenching and grinding teeth at night, dentists near you might advise against opting for Invisalign®. This is because the pressure from grinding could potentially deform or damage the thin plastic aligners.

The Freedom of Using Invisalign®

Invisalign near you allows you the freedom to indulge in your favourite foods without the dietary constraints that come with traditional braces. Whether it’s popcorn, candy, or crunchy edibles, everything can stay on your menu. With Invisalign®, there’s no need to shy away from showcasing your smile, and the aligners can even be taken off for significant events like family gatherings or professional photo sessions. Since the aligners aren’t permanently attached to your teeth, your tooth colour stays consistent and looks natural when the aligners are off. This is in contrast to traditional braces, which can leave a lighter area underneath once the brackets and wires are removed.

The conclusion of your treatment plan will involve receiving a retainer to be worn permanently at night to help keep your teeth in their new position.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How does Invisalign work to straighten teeth?
Invisalign uses clear aligners that fit over your teeth. They move your teeth into the right place in a gentle way. In Red Deer, Delta Dental offers Invisalign to help you get a smile that is straight and confident.
How long does Invisalign treatment usually take?
Most people wear Invisalign for about 6 months and 2 years, depending on how much their teeth must shift. Our experts provide Invisalign near you to guide you on what to expect during your treatment.
Is Invisalign more comfortable than traditional braces?
Yes! Invisalign aligners are smooth and removable, so they don’t poke your gums or cheeks. Many patients at Delta Dental find Invisalign near you in Red Deer much more comfortable than metal braces.
Can I eat and drink while wearing Invisalign aligners?
While eating or drinking anything, you should remove your Invisalign to keep them clean and clear.
How often should I wear my Invisalign aligners?
You should wear your aligners for 20–22 hours a day. Only take them out to eat, drink, or brush your teeth for the best results.
Can Invisalign fix all types of teeth misalignment?
Invisalign can fix many common problems like gaps, crowding, and crooked teeth. During your visit, Delta Dental will check if Invisalign is right for you.
