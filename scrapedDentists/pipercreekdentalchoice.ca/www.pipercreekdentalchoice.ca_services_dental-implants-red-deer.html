Missing teeth can be a source of frustration. Whether it’s a single gap or a full row, it can impact your confidence, speech, and even your ability to eat. But don’t despair! Dental implants offer a revolutionary solution that looks, feels, and functions just like natural teeth.

What are Dental Implants?

The artificial tooth roots come in the form of titanium. These are implanted surgically into the jaw to help create a base for your replacement teeth, such as crowns, bridges, and dentures. Dental implants cannot shift around like dentures do in your mouth; they stay put there with assured permanence and make them a permanent solution to missing teeth.

Who Can Get Dental Implants?

Most healthy adults qualify for dental implants. The ideal candidates should have:

  • Sufficient bone density in the jaw to hold the implant.
  • Healthy gingival tissues, free of periodontal disease.
  • No significant conditions could adversely affect healing.

If you have significant bone loss in the jaw, treatments that may be performed to enhance your bone density include bone grafting or sinus lifts to serve as a foundation for the implants.

Dental Implants Procedure at Piper Creek

The dental implant process involves several steps to ensure a successful and long-lasting result. Here’s what you can expect during the procedure:

  • Initial Evaluation: During your first visit, our dentist in Red Deer will conduct a thorough examination, including X-rays and digital scans, to determine if dental implants are the right solution for you.
  • Treatment Planning: Based on this evaluation, we design a customized treatment plan for our patients. In this, the quantity and the quality of implants you will be needing and what best will be suitable for you are planned.
  • Implant Placement Surgery: The titanium implant post is inserted directly into the jawbone by a surgical procedure under local anesthesia. To one patient, the process may cause some pressure, but it is painless to most people.
  • Healing and Osseointegration: Over the next few months, the implant will integrate with the bone in your mandible to create an anchorage for your new tooth.
  • Abutment and Crown Placement: After healing is complete and the implant integrates into bone, an abutment is screwed into the implant; then a crown, bridge, or custom-made denture will be placed.

 

Benefits of Dental Implants

Choosing dental implants in Red Deer comes with numerous advantages:

  • Natural Look: Dental implants look just like real teeth, blending perfectly with your smile.
  • Comfortable: Implants are fixed in place, so there’s no slipping or discomfort like with dentures. Eat, speak, and laugh confidently.
  • Long-Lasting: If you take good care of them, implants can last forever. They don’t get cavities or stains.
  • Better Oral Health: Implants keep your jawbone strong and stop other teeth from moving, keeping your mouth healthy.
  • Easy to Maintain: No special cleaning or adhesives needed. Just brush and floss them like your regular teeth.
