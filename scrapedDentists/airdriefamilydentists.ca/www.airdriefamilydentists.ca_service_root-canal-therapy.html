Root Canal Therapy Near You

Root canal therapy is a very common dental procedure that can save a tooth that has been affected by decay or infection. If you are having tooth pain or sensitivity, you may need a root canal. We provide comfortable and effective root canal therapy in Airdrie at Airdrie Family Dentists to help you get back your oral health and keep your natural smile.

What Is Root Canal Therapy?

Root canal therapy is the process of extraction of infected or damaged tissue (pulp) from inside the tooth. Pulp has plenty of nerves and blood vessels, and when infected by deep decay, cracks, or trauma, it leads to pain and swelling. Root canal therapy near you halts the pain and prevents further destruction.

Signs You Might Need a Root Canal

If you experience any of the following symptoms, you may need to have a root canal procedure:

  • Severe toothache, especially on chewing
  • Sensitivity to cold or hot foods and beverages
  • Gums that are painful or inflamed
  • Darkening or discoloration of the tooth
  • Tiny gum swelling next to the tooth

If you notice any of these symptoms, visit a dentist near you immediately. Waiting too long can lead to more serious dental problems.

How Root Canal Therapy Works

Our skilled dentist will ensure you’re comfortable throughout the process. Here’s what to expect during root canal therapy in Airdrie:

  • Examination and X-rays: The dentist will first check the tooth and take X-rays to figure out how far the damage extends.
  • Numbing the Area: The tooth and surrounding area will be numbed with a local anesthetic so that you don’t feel anything at all during the procedure.
  • Draining the Infected Pulp: The Airdrie dentist will make a small incision in the tooth and extract the infected pulp with care.
  • Cleaning and Sealing: The inside of the tooth will be cleaned and sealed so it won’t get infected later on.
  • Restoration: Most of the time, a crown will be placed on the tooth to stabilize it and restore its strength.

Benefits of Root Canal Therapy

Root canal therapy will preserve your natural tooth and avoid the loss of the tooth. Some of the key benefits are:

  • Relief of pain and discomfort of teeth
  • Conservation of your own tooth
  • Increased biting and chewing force
  • Prevention of further infection and damage

FAQs

Why would I need a root canal treatment?

A root canal is needed when the tooth’s pulp becomes infected or inflamed due to decay, injury, or deep cavities.

What is removed during a root canal treatment?

The infected or damaged pulp inside the tooth is removed, and the root canals are cleaned and sealed.

Is a root canal treatment painful?

With modern anesthesia, root canal treatment is usually no more painful than getting a filling.

Are there risks associated with root canal therapy?

Risks are minimal but can include infection, tooth fracture, or incomplete healing in rare cases.

Can I have a root canal if I have a dental infection?

Yes, a root canal is often used to treat an existing dental infection and prevent it from spreading.

Are root canal treatments the same for children and adults?

The procedure is similar, but techniques may vary depending on the size and development of the child’s tooth.
