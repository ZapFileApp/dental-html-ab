TMJ Treatment Near You

If you find that you are having any pain in your jaw, headaches, or issues chewing, youmay have a temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder. TMJ could make eating and talking hurt. We offer great TMJ treatment in Airdrie at Airdrie Family Dentists so you can feel comfortable and enjoy yourself without discomfort.

What is TMJ Disorder?

The temporomandibular joint links your jawbone to your skull. TMJ disorder occurs when this joint is inflamed or out of position, causing symptoms like:

  • Pain or tenderness in the jaw
  • Clicking or popping of the jaws upon opening or closing
  • Chewing difficulty or pain during eating
  • Headaches or ear pain
  • Jaw locking

TMJ disorder may result from grinding of the teeth, jaw injuries, stress, or misalignment of the jaw and teeth.

How TMJ Treatment Can Help

Our dentist in Airdrie will begin with a comprehensive exam to determine the cause of your TMJ disorder. Once we have determined the problem, we develop an individualized treatment plan for TMJ treatment near you that helps reduce your symptoms and increase jaw function.

FAQs

What are common symptoms of TMJ disorder?

Common signs that you might have a TMJ problem include feeling pain or soreness in your jaw, especially near your ear. You might also notice a clicking or popping sound when you open your mouth to eat or talk, or you might find that your jaw feels “stuck” or hard to move.

How long does TMJ treatment take?

The time it takes to feel better depends on how serious the jaw issue is and what kind of treatment you need. For some people, simple changes like using a mouthguard at night can bring relief in just a few weeks.

How can TMJ disorder affect daily activities?

TMJ can make everyday tasks quite uncomfortable because it affects how you move your mouth. You might find it painful to chew tough foods, yawn, or even speak for long periods of time. Because it can cause headaches and neck pain, it can also make it harder to focus at work or enjoy your hobbies, often leaving you feeling tired or stressed from the constant discomfort.

When should I see a dentist for TMJ symptoms?

You should book an appointment if you have jaw pain that won’t go away or if you notice that you can’t open or close your mouth all the way. It is a good idea to see a dentist if the clicking in your jaw becomes painful or if you find yourself taking pain medicine every day to manage your jaw or face aches.

Can TMJ treatment improve my bite or jaw alignment?

Yes, treating TMJ is often focused on making sure your upper and lower teeth fit together correctly. By using special tools like splints or adjusting how your teeth meet, a dentist can help your jaw sit in a more natural, relaxed position.

How often should I follow up during TMJ treatment?

During your treatment, you will likely need to visit the dentist every few weeks or months at first so they can check your progress. These follow-up visits are important because they allow the dentist to adjust your treatment plan and make sure your jaw is healing properly, ensuring you get the best possible results for a pain-free smile.
