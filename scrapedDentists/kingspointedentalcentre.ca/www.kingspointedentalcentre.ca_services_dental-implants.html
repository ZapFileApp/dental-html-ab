Dental Implants in Airdrie

Dental Implants Near You

Dental implants are ideal for replacing missing teeth and stabilizing loose dentures. Our dental implants in Airdrie are made from biocompatible titanium and provide strong support for the jawbone and other dental restorations such as crowns, bridges, or dentures. With proper care, the results are long-lasting, allowing implants to serve you well for nearly a lifetime.

What Can Implants Fix?

  • Replace missing teeth without affecting nearby teeth.
  • Fix jaw pain or bite issues caused by teeth moving into the empty space.
  • Restore a patient’s confident smile.
  • Restore chewing, speech, and digestion.
  • Restore or enhance facial tissues.
  • Help hold a bridge or denture in place, making them more stable and comfortable.

If you’re thinking about getting dental implants near you, contact an Airdrie dentist to book a consultation. They can walk you through the process and answer any questions.

The Process of Getting Dental Implants in Airdrie

Getting implants requires a number of visits over several months.

X-rays and molds of the jaw and teeth are taken to check the bone, gum tissue, and space for the implant. Once the area is numb, the implant is surgically placed into the bone and allowed to heal for up to six months. Depending on the type of implant, a second procedure may be needed to place the “post” that will hold the new tooth. For other types of implants, the post and anchor are placed together in one procedure.

After a few weeks of healing, the artificial teeth are created and attached to the post part of the implant. This process may take one to two months because multiple fittings might be needed. Once fully healed, the artificial teeth are securely attached, providing the patient with stability and comfort.

After your treatment, you will receive care instructions to follow. Keeping good oral hygiene, healthy eating habits, and visiting the dentist in Airdrie regularly will help your implant last longer.

FAQ'S

Does getting a dental implant hurt?

Most people find that the procedure is not as complicated as they thought. Your dentist will give you numbing (anesthesia), and you won’t feel anything when it’s being done. You might feel a couple of days of discomfort afterwards when the implant is inserted, but this can normally be addressed with over-the-counter pain medication. Most people are healthy enough to return to work the following day.

How soon can I eat after getting an implant?

After the procedure is complete, you can generally resume eating soft foods once numbness passes. Do not chew on the implant site for a while. It will be possible to resume eating again within a few days for most people. Your dentist will instruct you on exactly what and when to eat.

Do dental implants look natural?

Yes, implants can be made to look and feel exactly like natural teeth. They are crafted to perfectly match the shape and colour of your smile. Few people will even realize that you have an implant. You will be able to eat and speak without any problem, knowing they are extremely realistic.

How do I take care of dental implants?

Caring for your implant is very similar to caring for your teeth. Brush and floss regularly to make the area as clean and healthy as you can. You should go see your dentist for checkups and cleanings on a regular basis. Caring properly will extend the life of your implant for many years.

Can I get implants if I have diabetes?

Yes, the vast majority of individuals with diabetes can have successful implants. Your diabetes must be well-managed before beginning treatment. Your dentist will assess your overall health to ensure healing is uneventful. With proper care, your results can be just as positive as anyone else’s.

Can dental implants be used to replace multiple teeth?

Yes, this treatment can replace a single tooth, several teeth, or a whole set. Your dentist will create a plan that fits your needs and goals. Implants can be utilized to support bridges or dentures to restore more extensive spaces. This restores your bite and keeps your smile healthy and strong.
