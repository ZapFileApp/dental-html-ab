Dental Crowns in Red Deer, AB

Dental Crowns Near You

A damaged tooth, whether cracked, worn down, or has been weakened by decay, can lead to other issues with your smile. Fortunately, many solutions are available to fix it, including receiving a dental crown.

Our dental clinic offers dental crowns in Red Deer. We encourage you to contact our dental crown specialist for more information about this process.

Types of Dental Crowns

Several materials can be used to fabricate dental prosthetics, including dental crowns.

• Metal (silver, gold).

• Porcelain-fused-to-metal.

• All porcelain.

• All resin.

• All ceramic.

Each option has its advantages; for instance, metal and porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns are durable and often the most affordable. Resin, ceramic, and all-porcelain are worth considering if you don’t want your dental crown to stand out.

Talk to a dental crown specialist near you to learn more. They’ll invite you to come in for a preliminary consultation, where they’ll take x-rays, physically examine your teeth and gums, and discuss your preferences and expectations regarding treatment. Our dentist will be able to identify the best option for you.

The Dental Crown Process

For at least 2 appointments, our dentist will prepare your smile to receive the prosthetic and place it correctly to boost your teeth’ functionality.

• Impressions are taken of your smile so that the crown will fit your oral dimensions exactly.
• The damaged tooth will be cleaned and cleared of decay or other debris, or chipped teeth.  Then it will be carefully trimmed so that the crown can fit.
• It takes a few weeks for the crown to be made at the dental lab. While you wait, our dentist will give you a temporary crown.
• Once it’s ready, you’ll be called in to have it placed. The temporary crown will be swapped with the permanent crown. It’s glued into place.
• Your tooth will be polished, and your bite alignment will be checked before you go.
Let our dental team know if you have concerns or require additional care to remain comfortable. If you’re worried about sensitivity or pain, Sedation dentistry is available.
