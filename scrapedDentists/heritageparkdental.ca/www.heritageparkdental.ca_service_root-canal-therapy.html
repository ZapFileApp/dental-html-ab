Root Canal Therapy Near You

It is important to practice good oral hygiene to prevent oral health issues from arising. Tooth decay is a common oral health issue that is caused by poor oral hygiene. The plaque and tartar that accumulate on your teeth can turn into a cavity when left untreated.

If a cavity is left untreated, it will begin to spread through your tooth to the roots and nerves. This can lead to a potential extraction.

Our clinic offers root canal therapy in SW Calgary to preserve your natural tooth and prevent a potential extraction.

Why is Root Canal Therapy Necessary?

Many people are very hesitant when it comes to a root canal. There are many misconceptions that a root canal is extremely painful. However, it is very much the opposite! A root canal will eliminate your pain and improve your oral health.

Your dentist in SW Calgary will examine your mouth and determine if you require a root canal. Typically, patients who require a root canal will experience pain and discomfort.

There are some cases where a patient will feel no pain but require root canal therapy in SW Calgary. A few common signs that you need a root canal treatment include the following:

  • Pain radiating from the area
  • Increased tooth sensitivity
  • Lingering pain
  • An abscess is forming in the area
  • Discolouration on the tooth

If you notice anything unusual in your mouth, it is important to contact your dentist immediately. The more you wait, the more chances you may develop a severe toothache.

The Process of Receiving a Root Canal

During the root canal therapy near you, our dentist will first numb your mouth using a local anesthetic to ensure you feel no pain during the procedure. A small hole will then be created to access and remove the decay.

We will then thoroughly clean your tooth and seal it up. Typically, a dental crown is recommended after the procedure to provide your tooth with additional protection and strength.

It is normal to feel some discomfort and sensitivity after a root canal. The pain should subside relatively quickly, and after a few days, you should be back to your routine.
