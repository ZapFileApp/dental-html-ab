What are Dental Implants?

If you’ve lost one or more natural teeth and are troubled by the functional and aesthetic implications, you might be contemplating the option of dental implants. When weighing the choice between dentures and implants, it’s crucial to be well-informed. Dentures, typically recommended for those unable to support their natural teeth, entail a substantial lifestyle adjustment. Achieving comfort with dentures necessitates a precise fit. However, denture wearers often encounter challenges as the shape of dental arches changes over time, leading to the need for repeated fittings and potential discomfort.

Dental Implants vs Dentures

Advantages of Dental Implants 

A key advantage of dental implants lies in their ability to closely resemble natural teeth in both appearance and sensation. Crafted to seamlessly integrate with your smile, they enhance your confidence and elevate your daily life experience.

In contrast to alternatives like dentures or dental bridges, dental implants are securely anchored within your mouth, offering permanence. This stability ensures they remain steadfast during everyday activities such as eating, speaking, or laughing, eliminating worries about slipping or shifting.

The Challenges and Lifestyle Changes Associated with Dentures

Dentures have long been a common solution for individuals unable to maintain their natural teeth, yet they often entail a considerable adjustment to one’s lifestyle. Initially, achieving a comfortable fit is essential for dentures. However, wearers often find that even with a good fit initially, changes in the shape of their dental arches over time necessitate frequent adjustments, leading to potential discomfort and inconvenience.

Jawbone Health and Denture Challenges

The jawbone relies on the distribution of bite forces transmitted through the root systems of teeth to maintain its strength and mineralization. Without these forces, vital minerals may be redirected away from the jaw, leading to a weakening of its structure.

This deterioration can notably affect the lower third of the face, causing a reduction in profile length. Additionally, frequent adjustments may be necessary for dentures, complicating their retention and making eating challenging. Poorly fitting dentures can cause discomfort, including hives, swelling, and irritation, hindering proper nutrition if they are too uncomfortable to wear.

Consuming food with dentures requires small, evenly distributed bites across the arches. However, dentures do not function like natural teeth, making the adjustment to eating with them difficult. Moreover, if dentures have a full palate, it significantly diminishes the ability to taste food.

What is the Cost of Dental Implants?

Dental implants are a significant investment in health and well-being, with individual implants starting around $3000.00 each in Canada. The overall cost can increase for multiple implants or a full mouth restoration. The pricing is influenced by factors such as the amount of materials needed and the expertise required for the implantation procedure.

Given the uniqueness of each patient’s requirements, it’s advisable to consult with one of our dentists at Life Dentistry for personalized information. If you’ve received a quote from another dentist, we offer a complimentary consultation to provide a second opinion, ensuring you explore the best available options.

Customizable Dental Implants

Dental implants are versatile and can be tailored to meet individual patient needs. While single implants can be placed as required, they can also be adapted for full-mouth restorations, such as in the case of all-on-four implants. In this approach, implants are strategically installed in the four quadrants of each dental arch to provide support for a complete set of prosthetic teeth. All-on-four implants effectively address the bite force requirements of the entire jawbone, mitigating concerns about bone resorption.

How to Care for Dental Implants

Dental implants boast exceptional longevity, often lasting for over 30 years with proper care, making them the gold standard in terms of durability. The materials used in dental implants are resistant to the effects of plaque bacteria, eliminating the risk of developing cavities in the implant itself. However, it’s crucial to emphasize that maintaining cleanliness is still essential.

While the implant material is resilient against bacterial and acid attacks, the surrounding tissues, including bone and gum tissue, remain susceptible. Therefore, diligent oral hygiene practices are vital to ensure the overall health and longevity of the implant. Regular cleaning and care of the surrounding tissues contribute significantly to the success of dental implants.

An overgrowth of bacteria can infiltrate gum tissue, leading to irritation and inflammation, which can, in turn, contribute to gum disease and potentially jeopardize the success of an implant. Whether you have one implant or several, regular cleaning is essential, similar to the care provided to natural teeth.

To ensure the long-term success of your implant, it is crucial to practice good oral hygiene, including brushing twice daily, flossing, and maintaining a nutritious diet. These habits are key factors in supporting the implant’s health and preventing potential issues.

If you are considering dental implants, consult with your dentist to determine your candidacy, discuss associated costs, and explore the overall oral health improvements that implants can offer.
