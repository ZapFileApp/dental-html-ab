What Is Gum Contouring?

Gum contouring, also known as gum reshaping or gingivectomy, is a cosmetic dental procedure that removes or reshapes excess gum tissue to improve the appearance of your smile. Whether you have a gummy smile where too much gum is visible, or an uneven gum line that makes your teeth appear different sizes, contouring creates a symmetrical, proportionate frame for your teeth.

This procedure is typically cosmetic. Still, it can also address issues from gum overgrowth.

The Procedure

Gum contouring is typically completed in a single visit. After administering local anesthesia for your comfort, we carefully remove or reshape excess gum tissue using precision instruments or a soft-tissue laser. The laser option promotes faster healing and reduced bleeding. The entire procedure usually takes 30 to 60 minutes depending on the extent of reshaping needed, and results are visible immediately.

Recovery and Aftercare

Recovery from gum contouring is relatively quick and straightforward. Most patients experience mild tenderness and slight swelling for the first few days, which can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication and ice packs. We recommend eating soft foods and avoiding spicy or acidic meals for the first week. Most patients return to their normal routine within a day or two, with full healing complete in one to two weeks.

Why Consider Gum Contouring?

  • Gummy Smile: When excess gum tissue covers too much of the teeth
  • Uneven Gum Line: When the gums appear asymmetrical
  • Teeth That Look Too Short: More gum exposure can make teeth seem smaller
  • Better Oral Health: Getting rid of extra tissue helps with cleaning and lowers the risk of gum issues

If you feel uneasy about your gums, this procedure can improve things.

Laser vs. Traditional Gum Contouring

Laser gum contouring is less invasive and causes minimal discomfort. It seals the gum tissue as it reshapes, reducing bleeding and swelling. Because the laser cauterizes as it works, the risk of infection is lower. Healing time is usually faster.

Traditional scalpel contouring involves carefully trimming excess gum tissue. It works well but might need stitches and takes longer to heal.

Many patients prefer laser gum contouring. It's more accurate, less painful, and helps them recover faster.

Gum Contouring Aftercare

Proper aftercare helps ensure smooth healing. Follow these tips:

  • Avoid spicy or hard foods for a few days
  • Use a gentle mouth rinse to keep the area clean
  • Follow up with your dentist if you experience prolonged discomfort

Frequently Asked Questions

Does gum contouring hurt?+
Most patients experience little to no pain. We use a local anesthetic to ensure a comfortable procedure.
How long does recovery take?+
Healing usually takes a few days to a week, depending on the extent of the reshaping.
Does gum tissue grow back after contouring?+
No, the removed gum tissue does not grow back. The results are permanent.
Can I eat normally after the procedure?+
Yes, but it's best to avoid spicy, crunchy, or very hot foods for a few days.
Is gum contouring safe?+
Yes, it is a routine and safe procedure when performed by an experienced dentist.
