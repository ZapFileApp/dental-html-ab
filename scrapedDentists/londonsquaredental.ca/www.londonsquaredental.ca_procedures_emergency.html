What Counts as a Dental Emergency?

Severe, persistent toothache
Knocked-out (avulsed) tooth
Broken, cracked, or chipped tooth
Dental abscess or facial swelling
Lost filling, crown, or bridge
Uncontrolled bleeding from the mouth
Jaw pain, injury, or trauma
Loose or displaced tooth

If any of these sound like you, don't wait it out — call us or walk in.

What To Do Right Now

Severe Toothache

Rinse with warm salt water, floss gently to remove trapped food, and take over-the-counter pain relief as directed. Avoid heat on the cheek. Call us for a same-day appointment.

Knocked-Out Tooth

Hold the tooth by the crown, rinse gently if dirty, and try to place it back in the socket. If not, keep it moist in milk. Aim to be at our office within 30 minutes.

Broken or Chipped Tooth

Save any pieces, rinse your mouth with warm water, and apply a cold compress to reduce swelling. Call us — we'll often see you the same day.

Swelling or Abscess

Facial swelling can indicate a serious infection. Don't delay — call us immediately. If swelling is affecting your breathing or swallowing, go to the ER first.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as a dental emergency?+
A dental emergency includes severe or persistent tooth pain, a knocked-out or fractured tooth, swelling or abscess, uncontrolled bleeding, lost crowns or fillings, and any trauma to the mouth or jaw. If you're unsure, call us — we'll triage you over the phone and bring you in the same day if needed.
How fast can I be seen?+
We reserve same-day emergency slots every day. Most emergency patients are seen within a few hours of calling, and we offer late evening appointments to fit urgent issues that come up after work or school.
What should I do for a knocked-out tooth?+
Pick the tooth up by the crown (never the root), gently rinse it with milk or saline if dirty, and try to place it back in the socket. If you can't, store it in milk or saliva and get to our office within 30 minutes — that's the window where reimplantation success is highest.
Will you bill my insurance directly?+
Yes. We submit claims directly to your insurer on your behalf, so you only pay the portion not covered. We also offer 0% financing and accept NIHB to make emergency care accessible.
