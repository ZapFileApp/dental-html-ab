What Are Dental Crowns & Bridges?

Crowns and bridges are dental restorations. They help strengthen or replace missing or damaged teeth.

Crowns

A crown is a protective cap placed over a damaged or weakened tooth. It restores the tooth's shape, size, and function while providing long-term durability.

Bridges

A bridge replaces one or more missing teeth. It consists of artificial teeth (pontics) anchored to neighboring natural teeth or dental implants.

Who Needs Crowns & Bridges?

Crowns and bridges are ideal solutions for individuals dealing with the following dental concerns:

Broken or worn-down teeth: It helps strengthen and restore teeth damaged by grinding, injury, or decay.
Teeth weakened by root canal treatment: It helps protect the treated tooth from fractures by strengthening its structure.
Missing teeth affecting chewing and speech: Helps you eat comfortably and speak clearly.
Teeth shifting due to gaps: Maintain your teeth in their proper position to prevent misalignment and bite problems.
Cosmetic concerns: Hides discolored, crooked, or misaligned teeth. This creates a more natural and attractive smile.

Benefits of Crowns & Bridges

Crowns and bridges restore function and appearance with a durable, natural-looking solution.

Restores tooth strength: Protects weak or damaged teeth.
Improves appearance: Matches natural teeth for a seamless look.
Enhances function: Restores normal chewing and speaking ability.
Prevents shifting: Keeps remaining teeth in proper alignment.
Long-lasting solution: With proper care, crowns and bridges can last for years.

What to Expect During Your Crown or Bridge Procedure

Here's a step-by-step guide to your crown or bridge procedure.

1
Consultation & Evaluation: Our dentist will check your teeth and talk about your needs. Then, they will decide if a crown or bridge is right for you.
2
Tooth Preparation & Impressions: For crowns, the affected tooth is reshaped to fit the cap. For bridges, the surrounding teeth are prepared to support the artificial tooth. A mold or digital scan is taken to create a custom restoration.
3
Temporary Crown or Bridge: A temporary restoration is placed to protect your teeth while the permanent one is being made.
4
Final Placement: When your custom crown or bridge is ready, we bond it to your tooth or nearby teeth. This ensures a natural and comfortable fit.

FAQ

How long do crowns and bridges last?+
With proper care, they can last 10-15 years or longer.
Does getting a crown or bridge hurt?+
The procedure is done under local anesthesia to ensure comfort.
Will my crown or bridge look natural?+
Yes, we use high-quality materials that match the color and shape of your natural teeth.
How do I care for my crown or bridge?+
Brush and floss daily, avoid biting hard foods, and visit the dentist regularly for check-ups.
Can I replace a removable denture with a bridge?+
Yes, a fixed bridge is a more stable and comfortable alternative to removable dentures.
