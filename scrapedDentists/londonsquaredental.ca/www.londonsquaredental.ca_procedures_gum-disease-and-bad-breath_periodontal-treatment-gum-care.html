Periodontal Treatment & Gum Care in Calgary

Protect your smile from the inside out. Our expert periodontal treatments halt gum disease progression and restore your gum health.

Gum disease affects nearly half of all adults, yet many people are unaware they have it. At London Square Dental, our periodontal specialists identify gum disease early and provide targeted treatments to preserve your teeth, bone, and overall oral health. From gentle deep cleanings to advanced gum therapy, we create personalized treatment plans for every stage of periodontal disease.

Comprehensive Periodontal Assessment

Every treatment begins with a thorough periodontal evaluation. We measure pocket depths around each tooth, assess gum recession, check for bone loss with digital X-rays, and evaluate your overall gum tissue health. This detailed assessment allows us to determine the exact stage of your gum disease and create a targeted treatment plan.

Deep Cleaning & Scaling

For patients with gingivitis or early periodontitis, professional deep cleaning removes bacteria, plaque, and tartar from below the gum line where regular brushing cannot reach. Using ultrasonic scalers and precision hand instruments, we thoroughly clean the root surfaces to eliminate infection and promote gum reattachment.

Gum Therapy & Maintenance

After initial treatment, ongoing periodontal maintenance is crucial to prevent disease recurrence. We schedule regular maintenance visits every three to four months, during which we re-evaluate pocket depths, remove any new buildup, and adjust your home care routine as needed. Consistent maintenance keeps your gums healthy long-term.

Comfort-Focused Care

We understand that periodontal treatment can cause anxiety. Our team uses gentle techniques, effective local anaesthesia, and sedation options to ensure your comfort throughout every procedure. We take the time to explain each step and answer your questions so you feel confident and at ease during your visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the early signs of gum disease?+
Common signs include red, swollen gums, bleeding while brushing, bad breath, and gum recession.
Is periodontal treatment painful?+
Most treatments are performed with local anesthesia, ensuring minimal discomfort. Post-procedure care helps manage any temporary soreness.
Can gum disease affect overall health?+
Yes, untreated gum disease has been linked to systemic issues like heart disease, diabetes, and stroke.
How often should I see a periodontist?+
Regular checkups every 6-12 months are recommended for maintaining optimal gum health.
