Sealants & Fluoride Treatment in Calgary

Give your child a head start on cavity prevention with protective sealants and strengthening fluoride treatments.

Prevention is the best medicine, especially when it comes to your child's dental health. At London Square Dental, we offer dental sealants and professional fluoride treatments as two of the most effective, painless ways to protect young teeth from decay. These quick, non-invasive procedures can save your child from cavities and the need for more extensive treatment down the road.

Dental Sealants

Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth (molars and premolars), where most childhood cavities develop. These surfaces have deep grooves and pits that trap food and bacteria, making them difficult to clean with a toothbrush alone. Sealants fill these grooves and create a smooth, easy-to-clean barrier that prevents decay.

Professional Fluoride Treatments

Fluoride is a naturally occurring mineral that strengthens tooth enamel and makes it more resistant to acid attacks from bacteria and sugars. Our professional fluoride treatments deliver a much higher concentration than toothpaste or mouth rinse, providing enhanced protection. The treatment is quick — applied as a varnish, gel, or foam — and takes just a few minutes during your child's regular visit.

How Sealants Are Applied

The sealant application process is completely painless and takes only a few minutes per tooth. First, the teeth are cleaned and dried. A mild etching solution is applied to roughen the surface slightly, helping the sealant bond securely. The liquid sealant is then painted on and hardened with a special curing light. Your child can eat and drink normally right after the procedure.

Long-Term Protection

Studies show that dental sealants reduce the risk of cavities in molars by up to 80%. Combined with regular fluoride treatments, good brushing and flossing habits, and routine dental visits, sealants form a comprehensive defence against tooth decay. We monitor the condition of your child's sealants at every check-up and reapply them as needed to maintain full protection.

What to Expect During Your Appointment

  1. 1

    Initial Examination

    We ensure your teeth are free of cavities or gum disease before proceeding with treatment.

  2. 2

    Sealant Application

    Teeth are cleaned, dried, and a mild etching solution is applied. The sealant is then painted on and hardened with a special light.

  3. 3

    Fluoride Application

    Fluoride is applied using a mouthguard, brush, or tray with fluoride gel. It takes just a few minutes and is completely painless.

  4. 4

    Absorption Time

    Avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes after treatment to allow full absorption of the fluoride.

Aftercare Tips

  • Avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes after fluoride treatment
  • Continue regular brushing and flossing
  • For sealants, avoid chewing hard or sticky foods immediately after

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fluoride treatment and sealant application safe?+
Yes, both are completely safe. They're standard dental procedures used worldwide to help strengthen teeth and prevent cavities.
How often should I get fluoride treatment and sealants?+
Most patients benefit from fluoride treatments once a year. Sealants can last for several years, and your dentist will check their condition during regular checkups.
Can adults benefit from fluoride treatment and sealants?+
Yes! Adults can benefit from fluoride, especially if they have sensitive teeth or are at a higher risk for cavities. Adults can also benefit from sealants if their molars have deep grooves.
What are the side effects?+
Fluoride treatment has no known serious side effects. Some people may experience mild sensitivity that resolves on its own. Sealants are generally well-tolerated.
